# v2.18 — Retirement-Reversal Protocol Amendment

## Problem

Under the current rubric (v2.14 A-v2.14-01), adopted axes can accumulate refute weight and move to `adopted-contested`, `queued-for-de-adoption`, or `de-adopted`. The pathway is one-way: once refute weight is incurred, it is retained until an **explicit retirement-reversal event** lifts it.

But **the protocol does not define what a retirement-reversal event is.** As of v2.17:

- **A3 Interaction** is at 4E/1R. The 1 refute-game (Wingspan) remains on the ledger despite two subsequent successful counter-pressure designs (Vigil + Covenstat) earning A3 canonically.
- **A7 Emergence-Replayability** is at 4E/1R. The 1 refute-game (AFoS Halifax Hammer) remains despite Covenstat earning A7 canonically in two sessions of one game.

Both axes are **qualitatively recovered** — the panel record shows specific designed counter-pressures that earned the axes in multiple plays. But the formal refute weight holds. If a future game refutes either axis again, the ledger moves them to `adopted-contested` formal status — even though counter-pressure evidence exists in between.

**Without a retirement-reversal protocol, the ledger cannot reward successful counter-pressure design.** The factory produces counter-pressure originals, the panel confirms they work, and the ledger doesn't update. This creates a disconnect between what the factory demonstrates and what the ledger records.

## Proposed amendment (v2.18 A-v2.18-01)

Add explicit retirement-reversal protocol to the two-way ledger:

### Trigger

An adopted axis carrying ≥ 1 game of refute weight becomes eligible for **refute-weight reduction** if both conditions hold:

1. **Counter-pressure earnings** — at least 2 earnings on the axis across ≥ 2 different games whose designs were specifically structured to counter the refute. Evidence of counter-pressure design must be documented in the game's concept.md and/or design.md (e.g., as an explicit anchor-or-secondary-focus declaration).
2. **Collision-vote confirmation** — at least one of the counter-pressure games must have produced a collision vote (by the panel) confirming the axis earned canonically, not merely defensively.

### Effect

When the trigger fires, the axis's formal refute weight is reduced by 1 game. This can occur once per contested-watch-cycle (not repeatedly from the same set of counter-pressure earnings).

If an axis's refute weight falls to 0, the axis exits `contested-watch` formally and returns to standard `adopted` status.

### Rate-limiting

Retirement-reversal counts as a protocol-affecting event but does NOT consume the 2-game cycle amendment rate-limit (because it is a ledger-movement mechanism, not a new protocol addition). However, the user must **apply** retirement-reversal events manually in `personas/axis-pool.md` — not automatically via `/tigris-amendment`. This preserves the adversarial-review ethos: the rubric evolves via argument records, and retirement-reversal is a consequential enough movement to warrant an explicit user step.

### Retroactive application

The amendment applies forward-only. However, a one-time retroactive application is permitted at v2.18 adoption for the two axes currently eligible:

- **A3 Interaction**: counter-pressure earnings at Vigil (mandatory-discussion design) + Covenstat (public-sheet draft-denial). 2 games, both documented as A3-targeted in concept.md. Collision-vote confirmation at Vigil (A3↔A6 orthogonal-preservation) and Covenstat (A7↔A6 collision secondary-confirmed A3 earned independently). **Eligible for refute-weight reduction: 1R → 0R. Axis returns to standard adopted.**
- **A7 Emergence-Replayability**: counter-pressure earnings at Covenstat (12-sheet × shared-dice design). 1 game with 2 earnings. Falls **short** of the "≥ 2 different games" requirement. **Not eligible for refute-weight reduction yet.** Awaits 1 more counter-pressure earning in a different game.

## Worked example: Vigil's dual-axis outcome (per A-spec-2.18-01)

Vigil (game #18) demonstrates that counter-pressure design targets one axis but can have independent effects on others.

- **A3 Interaction (targeted)**: Vigil earned A3 canonically via mandatory-discussion + shared-Clue-Track + suspicion-driven-play. Counter-pressure success. **Contributes to A3's retirement-reversal eligibility.**
- **A6 Teachability (untargeted, partial-refute)**: Vigil's 14-minute first-play teach was over the A6 canonical target. Chvátil's A3↔A6 collision (round 5) recorded A6 as partial-earn rather than canonical. A6 did not lose adopted status because the overall ledger position remained healthy (A6 has 9+ earnings across history), but A6 would accumulate refute weight under v2.14 A-v2.14-01 if the pattern repeats.

**Protocol rule**: axis accounting is independent. A counter-pressure success on axis X does NOT immunize other axes from refute weight in the same game. A single game can simultaneously earn axis X canonically and contribute to axis Y's refute weight.

**Corollary**: counter-pressure designs should be scoped to the target axis's counter-pressure domain without overly damaging other axes. Vigil's A6 partial-refute is an acceptable cost (A6 is rich in prior earnings); a counter-pressure design that refutes 3+ axes to defend 1 would be a failure even if the target axis earns.

## Non-goals

- **Auto-refute-weight-reduction**: the amendment does NOT allow refute weight to decrease automatically via ordinary re-earnings of a contested axis. Only explicitly-designed counter-pressures count.
- **Symmetric protocol for retired axes**: C5 retirement is not affected. Retired-axis reversal is a separate question (see I-dominion-02 which already handled C4's case ad-hoc).
- **Multi-step refute-weight-reduction**: retirement-reversal reduces 1 game of refute weight at a time. An axis at 3+ games of refute cannot jump to 0; it must go through `adopted-contested` → contested-watch → adopted across multiple counter-pressure cycles.
