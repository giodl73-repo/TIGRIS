# v2.22 — Dormancy-Watch Protocol Amendment

## Problem

The retirement trigger in the amendment skill reads:

> "If `refuted count (including 0.5 weights summed) across >= 2 games >= 2`, mark as `retired`."

Retire-explicit stakes contribute 0.5 refuted weight each. After 24 games, three adopted axes have accumulated 2.0+ refuted weight **entirely from retire-explicit events** — no axis has ever received a formal 1.0-weight refutation:

| Axis | Cumulative R | Source |
|---|---|---|
| B5 Architectural Novelty | 2.0R | 4 retire-explicit events (TIGRIS originals that disclaim novelty) |
| C2 Minimum-Score Shape | 2.0R | 4 retire-explicit events (co-op/coop/territorial games with binary win-lose) |
| C4 Engine-Garden Dependency | 2.0R | 4 retire-explicit events (games without personal engines) |

By a strict reading of the retirement trigger, these axes are now queued for retirement. But this conclusion is architecturally wrong:

1. **Retire-explicit ≠ formal refutation.** A retire-explicit is a persona declaring "this axis did not apply to this game" — not "this axis's claim is wrong." It is a scope signal, not an argument. Retire-explicit was designed to distinguish the *active refutation* case from the *inapplicability* case.

2. **B5/C2/C4 earn actively when applicable.** B5 has 3+ canonical earns (Parliament, TtA, Nemesis, Pandemic Legacy). C4 earned in GARNER and CANTON. These are healthy axes with genuine relevance; they simply do not fire in games outside their domain.

3. **Retiring B5 would remove the only axis for architectural novelty claims.** Any future TIGRIS original that genuinely breaks new mechanical ground would have no axis to earn on. This would impoverish the review system.

4. **The retirement trigger was designed for a different case.** The original retirement case was C5 Anti-Catch-up Pressure, which was genuinely *wrong* — it predicted a catch-up mechanism that was argued away in multiple games' argument records. C5's retirements included formal adversarial refutations, not just inapplicability.

**The protocol gap**: the retirement trigger makes no distinction between *scope-limited axes* (healthy but narrow; retire-explicitly in out-of-scope games) and *wrong axes* (genuinely refuted arguments). Both accumulate refuted weight via the same path, but they represent qualitatively different situations.

## Proposed amendment (v2.22 A-v2.22-01)

### Distinction: retire-explicit-only weight vs. mixed weight

**Retire-explicit-only weight (soft accumulation):** The axis has 0 formal refutations (1.0-weight events). Its refuted weight comes entirely from retire-explicit (0.5-weight) events. This pattern signals *scope limitation*, not *argument failure*.

**Mixed or hard weight (hard accumulation):** The axis has ≥1 formal refutation (1.0-weight event) in its record — meaning at least one game produced an argument that the axis's claim was wrong, not just that it didn't apply. This pattern signals *contested validity*.

### New state: `dormancy-watch`

An adopted axis enters **dormancy-watch** when:
- Its cumulative refuted weight reaches ≥ 2.0 (the existing retirement threshold), AND
- Its refuted weight is entirely retire-explicit-only (zero formal 1.0-weight refutations), AND
- It has been ≥ 8 **games where the axis was drafted** since its last earn. (A-spec-2.22-01: "8 games" counts only games in which the axis was actively drafted and argued — not total games elapsed. An axis not drafted cannot enter dormancy-watch through neglect; only axes drafted-but-failing count toward the window.)

*(The 8-game dormancy qualifier prevents axes from entering dormancy-watch during a single dry spell — e.g., B6 had 15 games of retire-explicit without a dormancy-watch trigger because it had no retire-explicit-only 2.0R accumulation at the time.)*

**Dormancy-watch status:**
- The axis is **not retired** and **not queued for de-adoption**.
- The factory should attempt a **targeted earn** of the axis within the next 3 games. A targeted earn means:
  - A game where the axis is plausibly applicable (not a guaranteed inapplicability game), AND
  - The persona who primarily advocates for the axis drafts it and argues for it actively.
- If the axis earns in any of the 3 targeted games: **dormancy-watch clears**. Reset the soft-accumulation clock (do not zero the refuted weight — the prior retire-explicits stay on record, but the dormancy-watch trigger does not re-fire until another 2.0R of new retire-explicit accumulation).
- If the axis does **not** earn in 3 targeted games: the axis enters **de-adoption review**. A formal parliamentary procedure is run on the axis itself (not a game). The argument record asks: "has the game corpus shown this axis is genuinely inapplicable, or did the 3 targeted games simply fail to give it the right conditions?" Panel votes to retire or return to adopted.

### De-adoption review (if dormancy-watch fails)

A de-adoption review is a **spec-review procedure** on the axis definition itself. It runs exactly like a v2.14 de-adoption path but is triggered by soft-accumulation evidence rather than hard-refutation evidence. The procedure:
1. The anchor persona (primary advocate of the contested axis) writes a "defense brief" (≤500 words) citing the best remaining canonical games as evidence.
2. The opposing case is made by the persona who most frequently retire-explicitly'd the axis.
3. Parliament votes: Retire (≥5 of 8) or Return to standard adopted (≥5 of 8). Tie → axis stays adopted.

## Non-goals

- **Does not remove retire-explicit from the retirement trigger formula.** Retire-explicit still accumulates toward retirement. The amendment adds a new state (dormancy-watch) between "soft accumulation at 2.0R" and "de-adoption path" — it does not eliminate the path, only routes through a more deliberate procedure.
- **Does not apply retroactively to C5.** C5's retirement was via hard weight (formal refutations); v2.22 is irrelevant to prior retirements.
- **Does not change the adoption trigger.** Only retirement is affected.

## Open questions (resolved)

1. **Should the dormancy qualifier be 5 games or 8 games?** Decided: 8 games. 5 is too short — a dry spell of 5 games with retire-explicit is common for axis-specific designs. 8 games ensures the axis had adequate opportunity across a range of game types.

2. **Should de-adoption review use Parliament procedure or a simpler vote?** Decided: full Parliament spec-review on the axis definition. The axis definition itself becomes the artifact under review. This maintains the adversarial-review ethos.

3. **Does clearing dormancy-watch reset refuted weight to 0?** Decided: NO. The retire-explicit history stays on the ledger (forward-only versioning). The dormancy-watch clock resets, but the weight is persistent. A new 2.0R of retire-explicit accumulation from that point forward would trigger dormancy-watch again.
