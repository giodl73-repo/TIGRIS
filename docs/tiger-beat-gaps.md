# TIGER BEAT Design Gap Opportunities

Five empty regions in 150-game corpus TIGER space. Each is a genuine unsolved design
problem, not an accident. Nearest existing game + design concept + 5 meetup inspirations.

---

## GAP 1: E+R — Experiential + Range
**2 games in corpus.** ~~0 games. Nearest: Codenames (dist=3.5).~~

**Corpus entries:**
- **RITE #153** (TIGRIS original, Chvátil/A26, 3–6p, 30 min) — E=8.5, R=8. Room party game with social contracts + physical dexterity. Gap 1 anchor confirmed.
- **UPSTAGE PARLOR #1** (PARLOR original, Chvátil/A26, 4–8p, 20–30 min) — E≈8, R≈8. Scene-stealing party game; DOUBLE mechanic. PARLOR pipeline's first entry.

**The tension:** Physical/social games have fixed ritual structure (low R).
Replayable games tend to be mechanically procedural (low E).
Nobody has made a game where the social/physical character itself generates infinite variety.

**Design concept:** A physical dexterity game with asymmetric social roles that rotate
every game. Players have hidden physical objectives AND hidden social contracts
simultaneously — not mechanically hidden like Secret Hitler, but ritually hidden.
The physical surface generates the tension; the social layer generates the variety.

**5 Meetup Inspirations:**
1. **Crokinole meets Secret Hitler** — physical precision + hidden role pressure; your
   dexterity goal changes based on who you secretly are
2. **Klask meets Cosmic Encounter** — real-time dexterity + wildly asymmetric alien powers;
   each game the physics feel different because your powers feel different
3. **Wavelength meets Crokinole** — estimation/social calibration + physical execution;
   you aim where you think your partner means, not where they said
4. **Dixit meets Jenga** — narrative imagination + physical tension; the story you tell
   determines which block you must pull
5. **Just One meets Carabande** — cooperative word deduction + dexterity racing;
   clues are only valid if you can physically execute them on the track

## GAP 2: G+E — Deep Architecture + Experiential
**0 games in corpus. Nearest: Underwater Cities (dist=3.6)**

**The tension:** Heavy euros demand cognitive focus (kills social/physical layer).
Social/physical games need low cognitive overhead to sustain the experiential character.
Spirit Island comes closest — cooperative engine with social contract — but physical
affordance is low and the social layer is cognitive, not embodied.

**Design concept:** A Lacerda-weight game built around a shared physical workspace.
Players literally build structures on a common surface and spatial arrangement creates
the social contract. The engine is deep but emerges from physical negotiation over
table real-estate — not abstract resource tracks.

**5 Meetup Inspirations:**
1. **Lisboa meets Gloomhaven** — economic engine depth + cooperative embodied narrative;
   you're building a city AND living in it as characters
2. **Brass: Birmingham meets Chinatown** — network-building engine + physical negotiation
   over shared space; deals are binding because they're physically placed
3. **On Mars meets Root** — heavy engine + asymmetric physical presence on a shared map;
   each faction's engine works differently AND occupies space differently
4. **Food Chain Magnate meets Pandemic Legacy** — corporate engine depth + emotional
   stakes that accumulate physically on the board over campaigns
5. **The Gallerist meets Spirit Island** — economic optimization engine + cooperative
   social contract where players embody spirits with physical ritual components

## GAP 3: G+R — Deep Architecture + Range
**0 games in corpus. Nearest: Ark Nova (dist=5.2)**

**The tension:** Deep engines have optimal paths — once you know them, replayability drops.
High-R games stay fresh because rules vary; but varied rules usually mean shallow engines.
Wingspan is close (high R from bird cards) but engine depth is moderate.
Ark Nova is close (deep engine) but the card pool constrains strategic variety.

**Design concept:** Modular engine rules — you draft the rules of your own engine
before building it. Like Dominion's card randomness applied to Brass-level depth.
Each game the available engine types change, so depth is always present but the
optimal path is never the same.

**5 Meetup Inspirations:**
1. **Dominion meets Brass: Birmingham** — card-randomized deck variety + network-depth
   engine; each game you're building a different kind of industrial network
2. **Race for the Galaxy meets Lisboa** — tableau-building variety + Lacerda engine depth;
   you're always building an engine but never the same one twice
3. **Spirit Island meets Viticulture** — scenario cards that vary the engine rules +
   deep seasonal engine; each island changes how your engine fundamentally works
4. **7 Wonders meets Through the Ages** — draft determines your civilization's engine
   rules before you build them; deep civ engine with drafting variety baked in
5. **Ark Nova meets Cosmic Encounter** — engine-building zoo + wildly asymmetric powers
   that break the engine rules; each game your engine has a different exploit built in

## GAP 4: T+E — High Tension + Experiential
**0 games in corpus. Nearest: Clank! (dist=4.4)**

**The tension:** High-tension games achieve pressure through mechanical stakes
(elimination, point cliffs, catastrophe). Social/physical character requires cognitive
space and ritual — incompatible with pressure. Secret Hitler comes close (social
contract + tension) but physical affordance is zero.

**Design concept:** A negotiation game where the social contract IS the tension mechanism.
Breaking a deal has physical/ritual consequences — not mechanical penalties, but tangible
physical effects on the table. Players physically handle components to make binding
commitments. The stakes live in the social/physical layer, not the score track.

**5 Meetup Inspirations:**
1. **Chinatown meets Skull** — neighborhood negotiation + physical bluffing with
   real elimination; deals are made by placing tiles, broken by placing skulls
2. **Dead of Winter meets Crokinole** — hidden traitor pressure + physical skill test;
   your loyalty is proven or broken by how you play the physical game
3. **Cosmic Encounter meets Hanabi** — alien power chaos + cooperative hidden information;
   you don't know your own power and neither does anyone else, only hints
4. **Twilight Imperium meets Quacks of Quedlinburg** — political elimination stakes +
   push-your-luck physical bag-pulling; political deals are backed by physical risk
5. **Secret Hitler meets Klask** — hidden role social pressure + real-time physical
   stakes; your role is revealed through how you play physically, not what you say

## GAP 5: T+G+R — High Tension + Deep Architecture + Range
**1 game in corpus.** ~~0 games. Nearest: Ark Nova (dist=5.2). The holy grail.~~

**Corpus entries:**
- **CODEX #151** (TIGRIS original, Knizia/A1, 2–5p, 90–120 min) — Schools of Magic engine-builder on shared hex map. 21 earned stakes (full Parliament v2). First intra-axis collision in corpus. Gap 5 anchor confirmed.

**The tension:** This is three hard things simultaneously:
- Deep engines need long learning curves (limits new-player range)
- High tension needs stakes (catastrophic failure breaks engine investment)
- High range needs varied optimal paths (contradicts deep engine mastery)
Through the Ages has T+G but low R. Ark Nova has G+R but moderate T.
Spirit Island has T+G but range is limited by island/spirit combinations.

**Design concept:** Civilization-scale engine game with scenario randomization AND
elimination stakes. The engine depth of Through the Ages, the scenario variety of
Spirit Island, with real elimination pressure. Each game a different civilization
template randomizes your engine access — so depth is always present but mastery
of one setup doesn't transfer cleanly.

**5 Meetup Inspirations:**
1. **Through the Ages meets Spirit Island** — civ engine depth + scenario randomization
   that changes which engine paths are viable; your civ has to adapt to the threat
2. **Twilight Imperium meets Ark Nova** — political elimination pressure + engine-building
   depth; your zoo/empire can be destroyed and you have to rebuild under threat
3. **Brass: Birmingham meets Pandemic Legacy** — network engine depth + campaign stakes
   where failure has permanent consequences; the engine accumulates but so does damage
4. **Small World meets Viticulture** — territory elimination stakes + deep seasonal engine;
   your engine can be conquered and you have to rebuild it from scratch
5. **Race for the Galaxy meets Nemesis** — fast engine-building variety + traitor/elimination
   horror pressure; you're racing to build an engine while something hunts you
