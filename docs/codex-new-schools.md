# CODEX New Schools

Two new Schools for the CODEX expansion. Each School has 15 Founding Doctrine candidates
scored against the same rubric as the original four Schools. Top 5 per School are selected.

**Scoring Rubric (max 25)**

| Criterion | 1 | 3 | 5 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **L — Legibility** | Confusing edge cases | Clear after slow read | Memorised after one game |
| **S — Strategic direction** | Generic bonus | Shapes one decision | Defines a completely different engine |
| **I — Draft pull** | Draft same way regardless | Changes 1-2 draft picks | Changes all 4 draft picks |
| **B — Balance** | Dominant alone OR useless | Good but not game-warping | Only shines with right drafted Doctrines |
| **F — Flavor fit** | Could be any School | Feels like this School | Quintessentially this School |

## WIND — School of the Howling Wind

**Identity:** Disruption, opponent manipulation, high mobility. Where Tide moves its own
components, Wind moves *opponents'* components. Wind does not build the strongest engine —
it prevents others from building theirs. A Wind player wins by making every other player's
plan two steps slower than it needed to be.

**Color:** Light grey / silver / white

**Home terrain:** Void Rifts + Ember Peaks (volatile, unstable ground — Wind thrives where
nothing stays fixed)

**Component:** The **Gale Marker** — a translucent silver token shaped like a stylized
spiral. Where other Schools anchor themselves to terrain, Wind marks *disruption points*:
positions it controls not through mass but through leverage.

### Wind — Notable Combos to Watch

- **Slipstream + Interference:** Wind spends Void to relocate an opponent's production component, then Interference taxes the relocated position. Stack this twice (via a drafted Void-gen Doctrine) and you can reduce an opponent's effective production by 3 in a single round.
- **Slipstream + The Weathervane:** Name "Activate" on The Weathervane, then use Slipstream to move an opponent's component off its territory type — now they pay 1 extra to activate a component that's lost its terrain bonus.
- **Scattering Law + The Inversion (Veil Event):** The Inversion turns off Conflict Doctrines but does not affect Scattering Law (which is a Wind action, not a Conflict Doctrine). Wind becomes the only School that still disrupts when The Inversion fires.
- **Turbulence + Sacred Ground Revealed (Veil Event):** Displace an opponent off the newly revealed Ancient Monument. Wind has no Conflict Doctrine that normally scores — Turbulence gives it a displacement kill without the "destroy" cost.
- **The Weathervane + Veil Toll (Veil Event):** Name "Conflict" on The Weathervane. Opponents who can't pay skip their Conflict actions. Then Veil Toll fires — and only opponents who took Conflict advance the Veil. Wind's positioning denial doubles as Veil control.

## ASH — School of the Dying Ember

**Identity:** Decay, recycling, second chances. Ash profits from destruction — both its own
and opponents'. It gets stronger as components are destroyed anywhere on the board. It
specializes in Void mana and uses Doctrine cards that were cut during Convocation. Ash does
not fear losing components; it is designed to benefit from that loss.

**Color:** Dark grey / black with ember-red highlights

**Home terrain:** Void Rifts + Forge Grounds (decay and smoldering industry — Ash emerges
where things have burned out and been left behind)

**Component:** The **Cinder Marker** — a matte black token with an ember-red rim, slightly
irregular in shape to suggest erosion. Cinder Markers gain visible markings (small stickers
or scratches) when they've been destroyed and reclaimed, tracking how many times Ash has
rebuilt from ruin.

### Ash — Notable Combos to Watch

- **Smoldering Core + Cinders of War:** Ash's Founding Doctrines stack beautifully under sustained assault. An opponent who destroys Ash's component gives Ash: (a) a free redeployment, (b) 1 permanent decay on their own attacker, and (c) 1 Void via From the Ashes (if also in hand). Attacking Ash is a losing proposition without a strategy to bypass all three.
- **From the Ashes + Blood Price (Veil Event):** Blood Price makes all Conflict free. Any game state with high Conflict volume is a production round for Ash. If multiple components are destroyed, Ash gains a Void per destruction — potentially 3-4 Void in one high-conflict round.
- **The Salvage + Scrap Doctrine:** Ash claims 2 cut Doctrines at setup. If it also draws Scrap Doctrine, it can later temporarily activate *additional* cut Doctrine cards for 3 Void each. Ash effectively operates with a private side pool of discarded laws.
- **Ashfall Doctrine + The Suppression (Veil Event):** The Suppression makes every player suspend 1 Doctrine. Ash gains 2 Void per suspended Doctrine = 6-8 Void in 4-player games from a single Veil Event. This is the interaction to watch most carefully in playtesting.
- **Cinders of War + Decay Rate (drafted):** Decay Rate is already aggressive; combined with Cinders of War's decay tokens, any opponent who attacks Ash accumulates production penalties from two sources simultaneously. The second copy of Decay Rate stacks with the first — watch for runaway degradation in long games.
- **Ember Memory (A4, not selected) + The Ruination (A7, not selected):** These two narrowly missed the cut but are natural combo partners — voluntarily destroy components, then score them at game end. If playtesting shows Ash is underpowered, these are the first two to reconsider adding.

## School Comparison: Wind vs Ash vs Original Four

| School | Core resource | Victory condition | Primary threat |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Flame** | Ember | First-mover advantage, early VP | Conflict aggression |
| **Tide** | Flow | Spatial flexibility, late repositioning | Doctrine adaptability |
| **Iron** | Weight | Snowball accumulation, VP at end | Engine depth |
| **Earth** | Flow/Void | Edict immunity, terrain bonuses | Resilience and late scoring |
| **Wind** | Void | Disruption, opponent slowdown | Forced suboptimal play |
| **Ash** | Void | Recycling, destruction profits | Punishing aggression back |

*Both Wind and Ash run primarily on Void — this is intentional. They share terrain preferences
(Void Rifts) and will contest each other for it. In games where both Wind and Ash play, expect
fierce early competition for Void Rift hexes, which shapes both Schools' trajectories.*
