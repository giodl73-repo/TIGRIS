# Famiglia — Rules Summary (Review Import)

Condensed for panel review. Canonical rules in the Rio Grande 2011 / 2F-Spiele rulebook.

## 1. Overview

2-player card game. Each player builds a rival crime family over ~30 turns. Cards are recruited from a shared display via a pairing mechanic: you play TWO cards of level N to recruit ONE card of level N+1. Cards have levels 0-5 and themed characters (Beggars, Bouncers, Drivers, Lawyers, Bosses, etc.). Each card has a special ability triggering on play or on recruit. Game ends when one player recruits a specific high-level card combination OR the deck runs out.

**Object:** build the strongest crime family by recruiting high-level characters.

## 2. Components

- ~85 cards total, distributed across levels 0-5
- Each card: level, family type (color), special ability text
- Starting hand: 2 Level-0 Beggars per player
- Card market: shared display (usually 8-10 cards face-up at a time)
- Rulebook + player aid card

## 3. Setup

- Each player gets 2 Level-0 cards to start
- Shuffle remaining deck; reveal 8-10 as initial market
- Determine first player

## 4. Turn structure

Each turn, active player takes ONE of these actions:

### 4.1 Recruit a card

Play 2 cards from your hand matching a specific recruit-cost on a market card. Take the market card; place in your play area.

Recruit-cost pattern: lower-level cards of matching color or type combine to recruit higher-level cards.

### 4.2 Activate a card ability

Some cards have ongoing abilities — tap them (turn sideways) to activate once per round.

### 4.3 Draw level-0 cards

Draw 1-2 Level-0 Beggars from the supply (fallback if no good recruitment option).

## 5. Card levels

| Level | Theme | Recruit requirement |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Beggars | Free — starting cards + drawable |
| 1 | Bouncers | 2× Level-0 |
| 2 | Drivers / Regulars | 2× Level-1 |
| 3 | Lawyers / Fixers | 2× Level-2 |
| 4 | Bosses | 2× Level-3 |
| 5 | Godfathers | 2× Level-4 |

The hierarchy creates a **conversion chain**: starter cards chain upward through pairings. Deep recruits require building up the family pyramid.

## 6. Card abilities

Each card has a unique effect. Examples (approximated):

- **Consigliere**: take any 1 card from opponent's play area
- **Enforcer**: block opponent's next recruitment action
- **Fixer**: ignore 1 recruitment cost requirement
- **Driver**: draw additional Level-0 cards
- **Boss**: permit recruiting a Level-N card with 1x Level-N−1 instead of 2x

Abilities create tactical variety; the chain's spine is the recruit-cost pattern.

## 7. End condition + Scoring

The game ends when a player recruits the first Level-5 Godfather (instant-win), OR when the deck is depleted and all players have taken their final turn.

**Scoring** (on deck-depletion end):
- Cards in play area score their printed level value.
- Highest total wins.

Tiebreaker: more Level-5 cards, then Level-4, etc.

## 9. Weight & audience

Weight ~1.8/5. Teach: 10-15 min. Play: 30-45 min. Audience: 2-player households, Euro-light enthusiasts.

## 10. Variants

Base game is 2p-only. No multiplayer variant published. Solo mode does not exist.

## 12. What this game is NOT

- Not multiplayer (2p only).
- Not thematic-heavy (mafia theme is decorative).
- Not long (30-45 min).
- Not variance-heavy (card market is public; luck comes from market-timing and initial draw).

## 13. What makes this Friese-signature

- Tight math (every recruit pair converts cleanly).
- Elegant core rule (pair-for-recruit).
- Short play time / deep replay.
- Compact component count (~85 cards only).
