# Famiglia — Concept (imported for review)

## Premise

Review of *Famiglia* (Friedemann Friese, 2011). A 2-player-only card game about building rival crime families. Light weight; compact brilliance in the Friese tradition.

- **Players:** strictly 2 (2p-only; no multiplayer scaling)
- **Length:** 30-45 min
- **Weight:** ~1.8/5 (light)
- **Mechanic:** card recruitment via lower-level pairs; each card has a level 0-5 and a special ability
- **BGG rank:** consistent mid-rank; under-appreciated Friese title

## Anchor: Vaccarino on B3 Conversion Chain Depth

**Vaccarino's first anchor on a published game** (after UNFOLD original). Famiglia's recruit-chain — play pairs of level-N cards to recruit level-N+1 cards — is a canonical conversion-chain mechanic. Vaccarino's newly adopted axis (v2.8 adoption) gets a third-game validation test.

## Why Famiglia for game #9

1. **First Vaccarino-on-published anchor.** Vaccarino previously anchored only UNFOLD (original). Friese is not in our roster, so Vaccarino proxies card-design lens.
2. **Canonical B3 Conversion Chain Depth case.** The recruit-from-lower-level mechanic IS the chain in pure form. Validates B3's adopted status.
3. **2p-only edge case for D3 Count-Robustness.** A game with exactly one player count tests whether D3 is still meaningful. Stegmaier will likely retire-explicit.
4. **Short review.** 30-45 min game; compressed pipeline. Good for maintaining momentum between heavier reviews.
5. **Pool-stability confirmation.** Does the 18-adopted-axis Pool correctly identify a light Euro's strengths and weaknesses?

## Artifact-as-story

The **card deck itself** is the centerpiece. Each card has: level (0-5), family color, special ability, and a recruit-cost (pair of matching lower-level cards). Compact deck; all game state lives in cards + a small shared market.
