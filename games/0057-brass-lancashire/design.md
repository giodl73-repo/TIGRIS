# Brass: Lancashire — Design (Review Mode: Rules Summary)

## Published rules summary

**Players:** 2–4. **Length:** 120–150 min. **Weight:** BGG 4.0.

### Two-era structure (Canal + Rail)

**Canal era:** Players build Level 1 industries connected by canal links. Each turn: play 2 cards; each card enables 1 action. Actions: Build (place industry tile on a town matching the card's industry type), Network (add a canal link), Develop (advance an industry type up the development track), Sell (deliver cotton to a merchant, consuming the industry tile), Loan (take £10 against VP penalty).

**Rail era transition:** At end of Canal era, ALL unconsumed buildings are removed. The map resets; only consumed (linked and sold-through) buildings survive as VP. The era transition is the game's shock event — mid-game lock-in that punishes over-building without selling.

### Coal and iron dependency

Cotton mills require coal to build; shipyards require iron and coal. Coal mines are consumed as they supply resources. This creates a shared resource economy: your coal mines may also supply opponents' builds, earning them VP. The B1 mechanism: coal→cotton→merchant is a 3-stage dependency chain.

### B3 mechanism

Building → Merchant delivery path: Coal Mine (builds) → Cotton Mill (fueled by coal) → Merchant Port (sells cotton; consumes the mill tile) → VP. Three transformation stages = depth-3 conversion. With the network link requirement (canal or rail must connect the path), it becomes depth-4 (Network → Coal → Cotton → Merchant).

### Scoring

VP earned at: (1) Canal era end — each canal link scores, each consumed building scores based on level. (2) Rail era end — same scoring for rail-era buildings. VP on links + buildings. Income track advances throughout both eras.
