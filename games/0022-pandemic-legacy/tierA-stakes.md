# Tier-A Stakes — Pandemic Legacy Season 1

## Anchor

**Chvátil** anchors **B5 Architectural Novelty** (adopted). Anchor claim: "Legacy mechanism (permanent modification to physical components, sealed-content unlocks, one-shot campaign arc) is architecturally novel in the Euro-adjacent tradition. Daviau invented it; Pandemic Legacy is its canonical expression. No Euro-lineage mechanic before 2011 made permanent physical alteration a first-class design principle."

Adjacency partners: A7 Emergence-Replayability (Lacerda) — the foundational tension: novelty vs. replayability. **GATE passes.** (Criterion 3: B5↔A7 collision candidate; Criterion 2: Bands A/B/C/D all covered below.)

## Drafts (24)

Draft order Round 1: Knizia → Rosenberg → Feld → Lacerda → K-K → Stegmaier → Vaccarino → Chvátil (anchor last).

### Knizia
1. **A1 Elegance.** 6/10. "Pandemic base is among the most elegant cooperative designs (4 actions × 4-color symmetry × single-outbreak-chain rule). Legacy adds complexity layer-by-layer without breaking the underlying rule structure. The base elegance holds through Session 6. Hold-leaning — complexity accumulation may erode it by Session 12."
2. **C1 Tension Budget.** **8/10.** "Dual tension-clock: epidemic escalation (spaced-shuffle) + infection rate (ratcheting integer). Outbreak counter = shared depletion resource. The budget is transparent, well-distributed across the session, and builds from Session 1 onward as city panic escalates. Earn."
3. **C2 Minimum-Score Shape.** 1/10. "Binary win/lose per session. No score curve. Retire-explicit."

### Rosenberg
1. **D4 Late-Game Lock-in Point.** **9/10.** "Legacy stickers are the mechanism of lock-in. Every session's end-phase is a late-game lock-in event — city upgrades, character improvements, panic markers, box-openings all permanently alter the game state. The lock-in accumulates; by Session 12 the board is unrecognizable from Session 1. Canonical D4. Earn."
2. **C3 Scarcity Bite.** 4/10. "4 actions per turn is real scarcity — each action costs 1, and everything meaningful costs at least 1. But this is turn-sequence scarcity, not resource-pool starvation. Closer to Pandemic's original design intent than Rosenberg's canonical scarcity. Hold-leaning."
3. **B1 System Gearing.** 7/10. "Infection deck → epidemic → intensify (reshuffle discards on top) → outbreak cascade → outbreak counter → game-loss. 5-gear escalation chain from card-draw to terminal outcome. Earn."

### Feld
1. **B2 Catastrophe Pressure.** **10/10.** "Quintuple-plus catastrophe: epidemic escalation (infection-rate ratchet), outbreak cascade (geographic propagation), city-panic-track (3-level city-specific escalation), disease-cube-depletion loss condition, player-deck-exhaustion loss condition. Legacy adds a 6th: permanent post-session catastrophe in the form of fallen cities (locked at panic-3). Pandemic is the canonical multi-catastrophe reference in the corpus. Earn."
2. **A2 Decision Density.** 7/10. "Per-turn decision: 4 actions, choose from 8 types (Move, Direct Flight, Charter Flight, Shuttle, Build, Treat, Share, Cure), with full disease-state awareness, role-ability tracking, and — uniquely in TIGRIS — legacy-consequence weighting (curing fast avoids city panic = future-session benefit). Earn."
3. **C6 Point-Salad Incommensurability.** 1/10. "Binary win/lose per session. No score to compare. Retire-explicit."

### Lacerda
1. **A7 Emergence-Replayability.** 5/10. "**Contested stake.** The campaign arc produces genuine emergent narrative: each session's outcome permanently modifies the next session's starting conditions, creating an unrepeatable story. However, the axis name is Emergence-Replayability, and replayability is explicitly 0 after the campaign ends. The game is designed to be played once. Staking as 5/10 (emergence earns; replayability refutes) — expecting collision with B5."
2. **B4 Information-Transparency-Cost.** **9/10.** "Sealed boxes are the most extreme information-cost design in the TIGRIS corpus: future game states are physically inaccessible until play events unlock them. Players cannot plan for sealed content; they cannot preview session-7 rules during session 3. Additionally, epidemic cards are placed face-down in the player deck — a second information-cost layer. Earn."
3. **B3 Conversion Chain Depth.** 4/10. "Pawn → treat disease cube → accumulate 5 same-color city cards → cure → eradicate. 4-step pipeline. Moderate depth but not the load-bearing axis — the chain is a means to win, not the game's primary design surface. Hold."

### Kramer-Kiesling
1. **D1 Family-to-Expert Scaling.** **9/10.** "Session 1 is a family-accessible cooperative game (weight ~2.0; rule-introduction order is carefully sequenced). Session 12 is a uniquely-complex game with custom sticker rules, named characters with individual scar histories, upgraded cities, and accumulated legacy narrative weight (weight approaches 3.5+). The scaling is not an option — it is baked into the campaign arc. The steepest progression-scaling in the TIGRIS corpus. Earn."
2. **D2 Spatial-Interaction Presence.** **8/10.** "City graph with outbreak chains is the game's core spatial mechanism. Outbreaks propagate along graph edges — treating one city may cascade to a neighbor. Epidemic cards seed infection by geography. The board's spatial structure determines which diseases are containable and which will cascade. Earn."
3. **C8 First-Turn Compression.** 6/10. "Session 1: standard Pandemic setup (~5 min). Session 7-8: legacy board state requires reading accumulated event history, applying pending stickers, checking city-panic levels, reading any new unlocked rules (~15-20 min). Session 11-12: setup is a substantial overhead and requires the entire group's engagement before play begins. First-turn compression grows inversely with session number — the opposite of typical compression. Earn."

### Stegmaier
1. **A6 Teachability.** 7/10. "Session 1 is among the most teachable games in the cooperative genre — Pandemic's role-first + 4-actions + single-escalation-rule teach structure is a design standard. Session 8+: new players cannot join mid-campaign (they would need the full campaign history). But A6 asks 'can I teach this to new players?' at entry. For the entry context (Session 1), A6 earns canonically. Earn."
2. **D3 Count-Robustness.** 5/10. "2-4 players; co-op action economy scales via fewer-players-per-disease-region. The game works at all counts but is optimized at 3-4 (2-player requires more multi-tasking per player). Hold — present but not canonical."
3. **C4 Engine-Garden Dependency.** 1/10. "No personal engine building. Character roles give asymmetric abilities but they are fixed at role-selection, not built during play. No garden analogy. Retire-explicit."

### Vaccarino
1. **A4 Variance Calibration.** 7/10. "Epidemic cards use a spaced-shuffle mechanism: the player deck is divided into N equal piles, one epidemic placed in each pile, piles reassembled. This guarantees epidemics are evenly spaced through the deck (controlled variance). Additionally, after each epidemic the infection-discard is reshuffled onto the infection deck (intensify), re-exposing already-infected cities — a variance escalation that is predictable in structure if not in city. Earn."
4. **C7 Action-Menu Clarity.** 6/10. "4 actions from an 8-type menu. Each action type is clearly defined; icons on cards reduce lookup. Pandemic's action menu is a teaching standard. Hold — present and clean but not the game's distinctive achievement."
5. **A5 Downtime/Pacing.** 3/10. "Co-op table-talk is the source of downtime in Pandemic Legacy, not mechanism pacing. Per-turn actions are fast. However, from Session 5 onward, the legacy phase (applying stickers, reading new rules, deciding upgrades) adds overhead. Retire-explicit — too external to mechanism to earn."

### Chvátil (anchor)
1. **B5 Architectural Novelty (anchor).** **10/10.** "Legacy mechanism is architecturally novel: no Euro-tradition design before Risk Legacy (2011) made permanent physical modification a first-class mechanic. Pandemic Legacy is the first Euro-adjacent expression of this architecture (Pandemic base × legacy layer). The sealed-box delivery system, sticker-modification protocol, and one-shot campaign structure compose an architecture unavailable to any game designed before 2011. Canonical reference game for B5."
2. **A3 Interaction.** 8/10. "Full cooperative at all player counts; mandatory interaction enforced by the action-economy scarcity (4 actions per player is insufficient to manage all disease fronts; specialization requires coordination). Earn."
3. **B6 Scoring Multiplier Dependency.** 1/10. "No score. Win/lose outcomes drive legacy consequences but these are not score multipliers. Retire-explicit."
