# Pandemic Legacy Season 1 — Rules Summary (Review Import)

Condensed summary for review purposes. Canonical rules in the Z-Man Games 2015 rulebook. Fair-use: critical commentary. No verbatim rulebook text.

## 1. Overview

2-4 players manage a global pandemic across a 12-session campaign. Each session is one "month" in the fictional year. Sessions are played sequentially on the same copy of the game, which is physically modified between sessions. Players lose content forever (tear up disease cubes, cross out rules, apply permanent stickers) and unlock new content from sealed boxes as triggered by play events. The game cannot be replayed after the campaign ends — it is explicitly designed as a one-time experience.

The base mechanism is Pandemic (Leacock, 2008): cooperative disease-cube-management on a world map with epidemic cards, outbreak cascades, and a cure-find win condition. Legacy adds a second layer: between sessions, the game world permanently evolves.

## 2. Components (summary)

- World map board with 48 cities in network/graph form.
- Disease cubes (4 colors × 24 cubes each).
- Infection deck (48 city cards, one per city) + Epidemic cards (4-6 per deck tier).
- Player cards (city cards + event cards) shuffled into player deck in equal-epidemic-spaced groups.
- Role cards: 5 starting roles with unique abilities; additional roles unlocked via legacy.
- **Legacy components**: sticker sheets (city panic, upgrades, "faded" city overlay), sealed boxes (0–5, labeled with unlock conditions), legacy deck (event cards drawn when conditions trigger), scarring tokens for characters, relationship cards.
- Funding track: total sessions lost increases funding (unlocks player-card-type advantages).

## 3. Session structure (each of 12 sessions)

### Pre-session setup
- Read any pending legacy cards from prior session.
- Apply any pending stickers to the board.
- Set up standard Pandemic board state (infection rate, outbreak counter).
- Reveal which sealed boxes (if any) may have been unlocked.

### In-session play (standard Pandemic structure)
1. **Player turn**: active player takes 4 actions (move / build research station / treat disease / share knowledge / discover cure / use event card / character-specific action).
2. **Draw phase**: draw 2 player-deck cards; shuffle and insert epidemic(s) if drawn.
3. **Infect phase**: draw from infection deck; place 1 cube per card drawn; trigger outbreak if a city reaches 4 cubes of one color.

### Win/loss per session
- **Win**: find cures for all 4 diseases during the session.
- **Lose**: run out of disease cubes; 8 outbreaks; player-deck exhausted.

### Post-session (legacy phase)
- Apply legacy consequences from the session: win/loss affects which cards are drawn from the legacy deck.
- Players may spend stickers to upgrade cities or characters.
- Some events trigger box-openings (sealed content revealed for the first time).
- Characters may receive **scars** (permanent ability penalties from wounds during play).
- **Funding track**: session losses increase overall funding level (minor catch-up mechanism).

## 4. Legacy escalation over the campaign

The game's state accumulates in one direction:
- **City panic**: cities repeatedly outbreaking get escalating panic levels (1-3), changing infection rules. At level 3, the city is "fallen" — permanently altered behavior.
- **Faded** (late-campaign): certain cities may become "Faded" after specific events — overlay stickers that change disease cubes to Faded tokens with unique behavior.
- **Scarred characters**: characters wounded during play receive permanent scar stickers reducing their ability. Characters can "retire" from active service.
- **New diseases**: mid-campaign box-opening introduces new mechanics (sealed rules).
- **Rule overrides**: later sessions may physically cross out rules from the original rulebook.

By Session 12, the board may have dozens of stickers, crossed-out rules, upgraded cities, and custom character sheets — a unique game state unrepeatable by any other copy.

## 5. Key mechanics summary

| Mechanic | Role |
|---|---|
| **Epidemic cards** (spaced-shuffle) | Managed-variance catastrophe trigger |
| **Infection cascade** (network geography) | Spatial outbreak propagation |
| **Legacy deck** (event-triggered) | One-time unlock sequence |
| **Sticker application** (permanent) | Irreversible state modification |
| **Sealed box system** | Information inaccessible until play-triggered |
| **Funding track** | Soft catch-up after session losses |
| **Character scars** | Permanent negative legacy on player assets |
| **City panic track** | Escalating long-term catastrophe |

## 6. Edge cases relevant to TIGRIS review

- **Replayability**: exactly zero for the campaign. A second playthrough with the same copy is physically impossible once all unlock conditions are triggered. A fresh copy resets the game, but players know the campaign narrative — the surprise is non-recoverable.
- **Teaching at session start**: Session 1 is fully teachable from the base Pandemic rules. Session 8+ requires reading the accumulated legacy-card history before play; new players cannot join mid-campaign.
- **Cooperative structure**: full co-op throughout; no personal objectives; no traitor mechanism.
