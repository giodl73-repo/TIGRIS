# Pandemic Legacy Season 1 — Concept (review-import)

## Anchor declaration

**Anchor persona:** Chvátil
**Anchor axis:** B5 Architectural Novelty (adopted)
**Anchor claim:** "Pandemic Legacy's **legacy mechanism** — permanent physical modification to game components between plays, sealed content unlocked by play events, one-shot campaign arc — is the defining architectural innovation of its decade. Daviau invented the legacy mechanism with Risk Legacy (2011); Pandemic Legacy is its canonical Euro-adjacent expression. No component of the Euro tradition (worker-placement, engine-building, auction, resource-conversion) has this structure. Chvátil has not anchored in 13 games (since TtA #8); B5 is the strongest available claim."

**Secondary focus:** Legacy-mechanism gap closure. TIGRIS corpus had 0 legacy/campaign reviews pre-#22. Pandemic Legacy S1 is the BGG canonical entry for the mechanism.

## Context

- 5th consecutive anchor on a published game (own-anchor will follow in an original).
- 2-4 players; BGG weight ~2.84; ~60-75 min per session; 12 sessions per campaign.
- **Fully cooperative** — all players win or lose together per session. Individual agency is through role selection and action economy, not competing objectives.
- Legacy mechanism: stickers, crossed-out rules, torn-up cards, sealed boxes unlocked by play events, named cities, scarred characters. After session 12 the game is physically altered and cannot be reset.
- Closes legacy-mechanism gap identified in `ideas/gaps.md` v2.0 (refreshed 2026-04-20).

## Expected signals

**Earn-candidates:**
- **B5 Architectural Novelty (anchor; Chvátil)** — legacy mechanism as paradigmatic structural innovation.
- **B2 Catastrophe Pressure (Feld)** — epidemic escalation + outbreak cascade + 8-outbreak loss + city panic + contamination. Pandemic is the canonical multi-catastrophe design in the corpus.
- **D4 Late-Game Lock-in Point (Rosenberg)** — legacy stickers are the literal mechanism of lock-in; every session-end is a lock-in event.
- **D1 Family-to-Expert Scaling (K-K)** — Session 1 is accessible family game; Session 12 is a uniquely complex game with custom rules and evolved components. Steepest progression-scaling in the corpus.
- **A3 Interaction (Chvátil)** — fully cooperative; mandatory interaction at all counts.
- **B4 Information-Transparency-Cost (Lacerda)** — sealed boxes represent the most extreme information-cost design in the TIGRIS corpus (future game states are literally inaccessible until unlocked).
- **D2 Spatial-Interaction Presence (K-K)** — city graph with outbreak chains is the game's core spatial mechanism.
- **A2 Decision Density (Feld)** — 4 actions from 8-type menu, with disease-state, role-synergy, and legacy-consequence tracking.
- **C1 Tension Budget (Knizia)** — epidemic deck + infection rate = dual tension-clock. Classic C1.
- **B1 System Gearing (Rosenberg)** — infection deck → epidemic → intensify → outbreak cascade → loss. 4-gear escalation chain.

**Key contested axis:**
- **A7 Emergence-Replayability (Lacerda)** — staked as contested. Campaign emergence is genuine (evolving board state across 12 sessions). Replayability is explicitly zero — the campaign is designed to be played exactly once. The axis name requires both components; B5 attacks via architectural-novelty = one-shot-by-design.

**Retire-explicit candidates:**
- C6 Point-Salad Incommensurability — binary win/lose per session; no score.
- C4 Engine-Garden Dependency — no personal engine building; roles give asymmetric abilities, not gardens.
- C2 Minimum-Score Shape — binary win/lose; no score curve.
- B6 Scoring Multiplier Dependency — no score.
- C3 Scarcity Bite — action economy scarcity is real but not Rosenberg-canonical starvation.
- A5 Downtime/Pacing — co-op table-talk overhead is the source of downtime; not a mechanism-level concern.
