# MERIDIAN — Concept

## Premise

MERIDIAN is a 2–4 player road-network game on a shared hex grid. Players build road segments connecting towns; adjacency creates direct value (connected towns share trade routes); blocking is immediate and spatial. The A1 secondary: the complete game is described by exactly two player actions — Place Road Segment, Score Adjacency.

D2 anchors because hex-grid road networks create the most direct form of spatial coupling: a segment you place blocks that hex permanently; your network's topology directly determines whether opponents can complete their routes.

## Anchor mechanic

**D2 Spatial-Interaction Presence.** Hex-grid road placement with permanent segments. Direct blocking: a road hex claimed by one player is unavailable to others. Network topology matters: connecting two towns through a third player's segment forces negotiation or detour. Adjacency scoring: your network scores based on how many towns your roads connect, creating immediate spatial tension.

**A1 secondary** — two rules: Place Road Segment (claim a hex edge on the grid), Score Adjacency (at round end, score 1 VP per connected town pair in your network). Complete game.
