# Tigris & Euphrates — Rules Summary (Review Import)

Condensed summary for review purposes. Canonical rules in the FFG 2015 rulebook; this document exists to ground the TIGRIS panel's stakes.

## 1. Overview

Players lead dynasties building civilizations along the Tigris and Euphrates rivers c. 3000 BC. Each player has a dynasty of 4 leaders (King, Priest, Trader, Farmer — corresponding to Black, Blue, Green, Red tile colors). Over ~20 turns, players place civilization tiles and leaders on a gridded board to form kingdoms, fight wars and revolts, play catastrophes, and collect victory points in four colors. Final VP = **minimum VP across the four colors** — the game's defining scoring rule.

## 2. Components

- Game board (11×16 grid, with a river pattern pre-drawn).
- 153 civilization tiles in 4 colors (Red ~ settlements, Blue ~ farms, Green ~ markets, Black ~ temples). Approximate distribution: 57 Red, 36 Blue, 30 Green, 30 Black.
- 9 catastrophe tiles (removed, placed on board to destroy tiles and break kingdoms).
- 8 treasure tiles (worth 1 VP each, colorless / wild).
- 16 leader figures (4 per player in 4 colors — King black, Priest blue, Trader green, Farmer red).
- 4 player screens (hide tile hand and VP cubes).
- VP cubes in 4 colors (per player, counted at game end).

## 3. Setup

- Board placed. 6 starting temples (Black tiles) are placed on predetermined spaces.
- Tile bag filled with all civilization tiles.
- Each player draws 6 civilization tiles (kept hidden behind screen).
- Each player takes 4 leaders and 1 set of VP cubes.

## 4. Turn Structure

On your turn, take **exactly 2 actions** from this menu:

1. **Place a civilization tile** on an empty square (not on river unless Blue-farm). May:
   - Extend a kingdom (if adjacent to one).
   - Create a new kingdom.
   - Join two kingdoms (may trigger external conflict — see §5).
   - Score 1 VP in the tile's color to the leader of matching color in the kingdom where it's placed (if any).
2. **Place a leader** from your reserve onto an empty square adjacent to a Blue-farm-tile (or river-with-blue-tile). May:
   - Start a new kingdom.
   - Join a kingdom (may trigger internal revolt if same-color leader exists — see §5).
   - Replace your own previous leader (free relocation).
3. **Play a catastrophe tile** onto any non-treasure tile (removes it from play, can break kingdoms — see §6).
4. **Trade tiles** — discard any number from hand, draw to refill to 6.
5. (Leaders return to reserve) — implicit when displaced.

After actions, **draw back up to 6 tiles** from the bag.

## 5. Conflicts

When placing a tile or leader joins two kingdoms, conflict may result.

**Internal revolt** (same-color leader): triggers when a leader is placed in a kingdom already containing another player's leader of the same color. Attacker and defender each reveal red temple tiles from hand + connected red temples on board. Higher total wins; loser's leader is sent back to reserve. Winner gets 1 Red VP.

**External war** (kingdom unification via tile): when placing a tile that would join two kingdoms each with a leader of the same color, external war triggers. Fought per color on leaders that share a color. Winner keeps leader; loser's leader is removed and all supporting tiles of the fought color are destroyed. Winner gains 1 VP per destroyed tile in matching color.

## 6. Catastrophes

A player may use an action to play a catastrophe tile on any non-treasure tile on the board, permanently removing it. Catastrophes cannot be adjacent to a monument and cannot remove temples anchoring a leader who'd be stranded. Strategic use: break opponent's kingdom, isolate a leader, redirect a treasure.

## 7. Monuments

When 4 like-colored tiles form a 2×2 square in a kingdom, they may be converted to a monument (2 colors, one of which matches the square). The leader of a matching color in the kingdom gains 1 VP per turn *per matching color* from the monument for the rest of the game. Monuments are durable — they bring a slow passive income.

## 8. Treasures

Treasure tiles sit on certain temples at setup. When a kingdom containing a treasure has a Trader (Green leader), that leader takes the treasure. Treasures score as any color (wild 1 VP). Controlling multiple treasures is a fast VP route but attracts attention.

## 9. Game End

The game ends when:
- The tile bag is empty AND cannot refill a player's hand, OR
- Only 2 or fewer unclaimed treasures remain on the board.

## 10. Scoring

Each player's **final VP = MINIMUM color VP across the four colors** they collected. Ties broken by second-lowest, then third, then fourth. Treasures may be distributed to any color at endgame (wild).

This rule is the game's defining move: you cannot win by stockpiling one color. You must spread broadly or you're capped by your weakest color.

## 11. Key mechanics summary

| Mechanic | Role |
|---|---|
| Minimum-score VP | The game's scoring backbone — the Knizia shape this rubric is named for. |
| Catastrophe tiles | Agents of reversal; 9 per game; targeted chaos. |
| External war / internal revolt | Direct interaction via same-color conflict. |
| Monuments (2×2 tile squares) | Passive income engine; rewards kingdom consolidation. |
| Treasure tiles | Wild VP; incentive for Trader leaders + kingdom design. |
| Hidden-hand tiles | Information asymmetry — opponents don't know what you'll play next. |
| Leaders tied to colors | Each leader scores only in their color; leader type is identity + VP gatekeeper. |
| River adjacency rules | Blue tile placement is geography-constrained. |
| 2 actions per turn | Tight action economy. |

## 12. Edge cases (review-relevant only)

- **Catastrophe on a river-tile** — not allowed on Blue farm tiles ON the river squares; allowed on other Blue tiles.
- **Stranded leader** — if catastrophe or war would strand a leader (no adjacent temple), the leader is returned to reserve.
- **Treasure distribution** — at endgame, a player with treasures assigns each to a color of their choice BEFORE computing the minimum. Strategic decisions here.
- **Tile-bag run-out** — when the bag can't refill a hand, the end triggers at end of current turn (not immediately).

## 13. Weight & target audience

- Weight ~3.5/5 on BGG.
- Teach time: 30–45 min for first play.
- Play time: 90 min (4p).
- Audience: Euro-gaming enthusiasts, Knizia collectors, tournament-scene regulars.

## 14. What this game is NOT

- Not a theme-forward experience. Mesopotamian theme is light; the game is a mathematical engine first.
- Not a cooperative game. Aggressive direct interaction via conflicts.
- Not a short game. 90 min is minimum; first plays run 2h+.
- Not a luck-driven game. Tile draw introduces variance; strategic decisions dominate.
