# Lisboa — Rules Summary (Review Import)

Condensed summary for panel review. Canonical rules in Eagle-Gryphon 2017 rulebook.

## 1. Overview

1-4 players rebuild the city of Lisbon following the 1755 earthquake, tsunami, and fire. Players play cards to clear debris, commission ships, open stores, construct public buildings, and influence three government figures (Prime Minister Marquês de Pombal, King José I, and the Cardinal). Cards drive everything: every card has a primary action, a role trigger for all players to use as an adjacent effect, and a Nobility / Church / Commerce / Shipping / Masonry trait that interacts with tracks and officials.

**Object:** most Victory Points at the end of the 5th book-keeping phase (each phase triggered when the deck runs out).

## 2. Components

- Modular board: city grid with debris tokens; government row (Prime Minister / King / Cardinal); Nobility / Church / Commerce / Shipping / Masonry tracks
- Action cards (~180): each has a primary action + a trigger + a trait + a category
- Officials: wigs (Nobility), mitres (Church), anchors (Shipping)
- Ship tokens, Store tokens, Public Building tiles
- Goods cubes (wool, cloth, tools, books, textile, gold)
- Favor cards (from officials)
- Plan cards (hidden personal objectives)
- Courtier cards
- Queen tokens
- Royal Treasury / decrees

## 3. Setup

- Each player: 5 starting cards, 1 Plan card (hidden), starting goods and gold.
- Board: debris tokens scattered across the grid; ships placed on harbor slots; 3 government tracks empty or with starting officials.
- Deck constructed from the three eras of cards; draw pile seeded.
- First player chosen; turn order marker placed.

## 4. Turn structure

Each turn, active player chooses ONE:

### Option A — Play an Action Card

1. Play card from hand onto the matching government row cell (Prime Minister / King / Cardinal). Placement influences which official track moves, and by how much.
2. Execute the card's **primary action** (see §5 Actions below).
3. Announce the card's **trigger** — all other players may spend a card from hand to take a matching secondary action (pays the active player a small favor).
4. Advance the matching category track (Nobility / Church / Commerce / Shipping / Masonry) by +1.
5. Draw replacement cards up to hand limit.

### Option B — Take a Decree

Spend a Favor card from an official to gain a special effect (draw extra card, swap goods, immediate scoring, etc.).

### Option C — Meet with an Official

Spend cards matching an official's color to receive a Favor (which becomes the decree fuel above) and trigger that official's threshold bonus.

## 5. Actions

### 5.1 Clear Rubble

Remove debris tokens from the city grid. Each cleared square frees a building plot. Rubble-clearing earns immediate VP + shifts Masonry track.

### 5.2 Commission a Ship

Purchase a ship (placed at harbor). Ships produce goods on shipping-track advances and serve as endgame VP multipliers.

### 5.3 Open a Store

Build a store on a cleared plot. Stores produce goods + contribute to set-collection bonuses at book-keeping.

### 5.4 Construct a Public Building

Spend goods + hire an architect (named designer cards embedded in deck: Manuel da Maia, Carlos Mardel, Eugénio dos Santos) to build a Public Building. Public Buildings gate endgame scoring: they score against adjacent stores and produce Queen's favor.

### 5.5 Meet the Prime Minister / King / Cardinal

Each official sits on a different row. Meeting:
- **Prime Minister**: gains political favors, influences card re-sort, sets turn order shifts.
- **King (José I)**: gains Nobility wigs + may mitigate catastrophes.
- **Cardinal**: gains Church mitres + triggers Inquisition-style scoring markers.

Officials have threshold triggers: when Nobility / Church / Masonry / Shipping / Commerce hits certain marks, the official unlocks additional VP.

## 6. Scoring / end condition

### Book-keeping phases

Five book-keeping phases trigger through the game, each when the draw deck exhausts. At each book-keeping:
- Goods on stores score VP
- Ships earn VP per goods-type shipped
- Queen's favor scoring checks (per Public Building built)
- Track-threshold VP from each active official

### End-of-game scoring

At the end of the final (5th) book-keeping:
- Plan card (hidden personal objective) reveals and scores.
- Courtier cards reveal: set-collection-style VP bonuses (complete your noble court).
- Majority on each of 5 tracks (Nobility / Church / Commerce / Shipping / Masonry) earns bonus.
- Queen satisfaction: sum of Public Buildings × threshold on each track.
- Wigs / Mitres / Anchors sets score.

Total VP = accumulated scoring across book-keepings + endgame.

## 7. Key subsystems

### 7.1 Card hand (B4 Info-Transparency-Cost canonical)

Each player holds 4-6 cards (hidden). Cards reveal officials, categories, and triggers only when played. Opponents' hands are hidden — paying attention to played-card patterns is rewarded. The card-order-decision is itself hidden strategy.

### 7.2 Government influence (B1 System Gearing)

Playing ANY card advances the card's category track. Category tracks gate officials. Officials score thresholds. Thresholds trigger scoring. One card play touches: official advancement, category track, trigger action for others, the player's tableau, and potentially the clock (each book-keeping).

### 7.3 Plan cards + Courtiers (D4 Late-Game Lock-in)

Hidden Plan = personal objective that scores only at endgame. Players commit to a Plan around mid-game (you can't pivot easily given action-card rhythm). Courtiers stack over time as favor rewards and score end-of-game sets.

### 7.4 Rubble / Plots / Buildings (A3 Interaction)

Clearing rubble opens plots. Plots are shared: once a plot is claimed for a building by one player, others can't use it. Store placement competes for adjacency to Public Buildings (adjacency = scoring).

### 7.5 The three officials

- **Prime Minister**: card turnover, order, and political favor. Action-economy mediator.
- **King**: Nobility / catastrophe mitigation (unique scoring on the Nobility track).
- **Cardinal**: Church / event-trigger. Scoring Church mitres + Inquisition mechanics.
