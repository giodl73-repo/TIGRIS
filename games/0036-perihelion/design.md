# Perihelion — Design

## 1. Overview
2–4 players, 45 minutes. Rondel where positions represent orbital zones. Each player pilots a research station collecting phenomena from zones they orbit through. Incommensurable zone scoring forces trajectory specialization.

## 2. The Rondel
12 positions arranged in a circle. Three orbital zones divide the circle:
- **Inner Belt** (positions 1–4): Thermal events, solar flares. Scores: +2 VP per chain of 3+ consecutive thermal event tokens collected.
- **Gas Giants** (positions 5–8): Atmospheric phenomena. Scores: +VP per matched pair of atmospheric readings (set collection).
- **Deep Space** (positions 9–12): Unique phenomena. Scores: +3 VP per unique phenomenon type collected (diversity, not quantity).

## 3. Movement
On your turn, choose one:
- **Gravity-Assist**: Advance your station 1 position on the rondel. Cost: 1 fuel. Collect the phenomenon token at the new position.
- **Direct Burn**: Advance your station 2–4 positions. Cost: fuel equal to positions skipped (skipping 3 = 4 fuel). Collect the token at landing position only.
- **Station-Keep**: Do not advance. Collect the phenomenon at your current position again (if any remain). Cost: 1 fuel.

## 4. Fuel
Each player starts with 8 fuel. Refuel by visiting Inner Belt position 2 (solar collector: gain 3 fuel). The single refuel point creates a tension between efficient collection and fuel management.

## 5. Trajectory Commitment
Rondel is one-way (you can only advance, not retreat). Once you have passed the Gas Giants, you cannot return to them without completing a full orbit (12 steps). This is the D4 permanent lock-in: committing to an orbit path means forgoing other zones until you come around again.

## 6. End-Game Scoring
Game ends after 6 full rondel laps have been completed collectively (roughly 18–24 turns in a 4-player game). Each player scores all three zone formulas independently — Inner Belt chains, Gas Giant pairs, Deep Space diversity. The three formulas are incommensurable: optimizing Deep Space diversity (collect 1 of each unique type) directly conflicts with Inner Belt chaining (collect multiples of the same type).

## 7. Teaching
The rondel is the whole game: move, collect, score. Gravity-assist vs. burn is the single strategic decision. Teach in 5 minutes.
