# Puerto Rico — Concept (imported for review)

## Premise

Review of *Puerto Rico* (Andreas Seyfarth, 2002). Held BGG #1 for nearly a decade; launched the role-selection + shared-action genre (Race for the Galaxy, San Juan, Glory to Rome, Gloomhaven).

- **Players:** 3-5 (best at 4)
- **Length:** 90-150 min
- **Weight:** ~3.2/5
- **Mechanic:** role selection (each player picks role → everyone takes action, selector gets bonus); plantation-building; shipping; VP from buildings + shipped goods + endgame

## Anchor: Knizia on C1 Tension Budget

C1 Tension Budget has **0 earnings across 5 games** — always weak-defended or ignored. Puerto Rico is the canonical case for tension budget: colonists, plantations, doubloons, and role-turn order are all scarce. If C1 doesn't earn here, the axis may need retirement.

This is a stress-test anchor: either rehabilitate C1 permanently or produce strong refutation evidence.

1. **C1 stress-test.** Dormant axis gets a canonical instance or fails to earn and heads toward retirement.
2. **I-parliament-02 third instance.** Puerto Rico's endgame building-bonus scoring is multiplicative (Factory × goods types; Residence × plantations). If the panel notes this, cluster reaches 3 instances → ready-for-adoption.
3. **A4 Variance Calibration genuine test.** Puerto Rico has **zero random elements** — all public information. Vaccarino's primary axis will stake LOW (2/2) for diagnostic. First A4 stake was also low (Scythe 7); Puerto Rico 2 shows the other end of the axis.
4. **C3 Scarcity Bite reversal candidate.** Queued-for-retirement; Puerto Rico is scarcity-biting.
5. **B3 Conversion Chain Depth canonical case.** Corn→Indigo→Sugar→Tobacco→Coffee chain; plantations→colonists→buildings→VP. First B3 earning probable.
