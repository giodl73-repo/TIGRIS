# Puerto Rico — Rules Summary (Review Import)

Condensed for panel review. Canonical rules in Rio Grande 2002 rulebook.

## 1. Overview

1500s Caribbean colonial economy. Players develop plantations, hire colonists, build structures, produce goods (corn/indigo/sugar/tobacco/coffee), trade for doubloons, and ship for VP. Round structure: each player takes a role (Settler/Mayor/Builder/Craftsman/Trader/Captain/Prospector); ALL players then take that role's action, but the selector gets a bonus. Game ends when victory points, colonists, or building spaces run out. Highest VP wins.

**Object:** accumulate most VP via buildings, shipped goods, and endgame bonuses.

## 2. Components

- Player boards (island + city grids)
- ~50 plantation tiles (5 crops + quarries)
- Colonists (wooden discs), goods barrels, doubloons, VP chips
- 7 role cards (one per round, per player); 1-2 unused
- ~35 building tiles (small production + violet special buildings)
- Ships (3) for goods; colonist ship

## 3. Setup

- Each player gets starting island + 1 indigo plantation (3-4p) or 1 corn plantation (5p).
- Plantations face-up (number = player count + 1).
- Buildings available for purchase.
- Role cards placed; governor (first player) chosen.
- Doubloons distributed (2-4 per player by count).

## 4. Turn structure — round = 1 role cycle

Starting with governor and going clockwise, each player picks a role not-yet-picked this round. ALL players (starting with selector) then take that role's action. The selector gets a bonus (extra production, extra doubloon, etc.).

After all players pick, any unused roles get 1 doubloon each added to them (incentive for next round).

Next round: governor rotates clockwise.

## 5. Roles (7 total)

- **Settler** — take a plantation tile. Selector gets priority + option of quarry.
- **Mayor** — draw colonists from ship. Selector gets +1 colonist.
- **Builder** — buy a building (paying doubloons). Selector pays 1 less.
- **Craftsman** — produce goods. Selector gets +1 good of any type.
- **Trader** — trade 1 good for doubloons. Selector gets +1 doubloon.
- **Captain** — ship goods for VP. Selector gets +1 VP.
- **Prospector** — selector gets 1 doubloon. No general action.

## 6. Buildings (key subsystem)

35 buildings in 4 tiers (cost 1-10 doubloons):
- **Tier 1 production** (cost 1-4): small indigo/sugar/tobacco/coffee plants. Provide production + VP at game end.
- **Tier 2-3 violet** (cost 3-7): Construction Hut, Hacienda, Hospice, Small Warehouse, Office, Market, Factory, Large Warehouse.
- **Tier 4 endgame** (cost 10): Residence, Fortress, Guild Hall, Customs House, Townhall, Harbor, Wharf — these are the **endgame scoring multipliers**.

### Endgame multipliers (I-parliament-02 relevant)
- **Residence** — VP = plantation count (scales with your plantation engine)
- **Guild Hall** — VP = production buildings count × 1, × 2 for large
- **Fortress** — VP = ⌊city buildings / 3⌋ × 1
- **Customs House** — VP = ⌊ship VP / 4⌋
- **Harbor** — bonus VP per shipping action
- **Townhall** — VP = violet buildings count

Multiple multipliers stack. Player choosing which multipliers to target drives late-game strategy.

## 7. Scoring / end condition

Game ends when:
- VP chip pile empty, OR
- Colonist ship can't refill to colonist count, OR
- Some player builds their 12th city space.

Complete current round; then score.

**Final VP = shipped-goods VP + building printed-VP + endgame-multiplier VP**.
