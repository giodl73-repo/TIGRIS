# UNFOLD — Concept

## Premise (v1.1)

UNFOLD is a 2-4 player, 45-60 minute tile-placement game where players develop a shared island from pristine forest into a lived-in landscape — but each player sees the island through a different **cultural lens** (Feng Shui, Medieval Village, Permaculture, Modernist Grid), and their scoring runs on their own aesthetics AND on a shared communal "Development Level" track.

The signature is the **physical pop-up tile**: each tile has three states. **Closed-left** is a natural or quiet form (grass, closed canal, clearing). **Open-up** is a transformed, vertical form (tree, bridge, building, tower). **Closed-right** is an alternate closed form (road, pathway, wall, waterway). Tiles flip between states, and **every flip cascades** — when one tile opens, an adjacent tile must close (and vice versa). This is pop-up-book physics as mechanic: energy is conserved; you can't have everything open or everything closed.

The game's central tension lives in two places:
1. **The Development Level track** — a single communal axis (0 = pristine forest; 40 = full town). Every open-up tile raises it; every closed-left lowers it. Each culture has a different **ideal Development Level**. At endgame, your distance from your ideal scores (or penalizes) your cultural track.
2. **The forced-fold cascade** — changing any tile's state forces an adjacent tile to change. You can't place a building without affecting someone's forest. You can't preserve a tree without shifting someone's road.

Every tile placement is a vote on what the island should be, and no vote satisfies every culture. Players place and flip tiles on the shared board, each scoring their cultural ideal, and the winner is whoever best navigates the **shared Development Level** while expressing their culture's aesthetic.

This is TIGRIS's first co-designed original board game.

## Players and length

- **Players:** 2-4 (likely best at 3-4).
- **Length:** 45-60 min once rules are learned (90 min first play).
- **Weight:** ~2.5/5.
- **BGG target:** mid-weight family-strategy; Carcassonne+ complexity.

## Anchor mechanic (v1.1 — after panel feedback)

**Physical pop-up tiles (3 states per tile) on a shared island with asymmetric cultural scoring AND a shared Development Level track AND forced-fold cascades.**

**Four intertwined systems** (up from 3 — panel noted architecture needed tighter gear-teeth):

1. **Pop-up tile physical architecture** — accordion-fold or hinged cardboard; three distinct printed faces per tile; tile's state is its physical orientation.
2. **Forced-fold cascade mechanic** — when you PLACE a new tile or FLIP an existing one, **one adjacent tile MUST state-change in the opposite direction**. Open one tile → close a neighbor. Close one → open a neighbor. Pop-up-book physics as rule.
3. **Cultural lens asymmetric scoring** — each player picks a culture at start; each culture reads the island differently.
4. **Development Level shared-threshold track** — one communal axis from 0 to 40. Each culture has a different IDEAL level. At endgame, distance from your ideal modifies your cultural score.

Together, these systems gear multiplicatively:
- A tile-state choice affects the Development Level (system 4)...
- Which triggers a cascade (system 2)...
- Which affects an opponent's culture-lens scoring (system 3)...
- Which lives on the physical 3D board (system 1).

No cultural lane runs parallel to the others. Every placement is a 4-system decision.

## Artifact-as-story

The **physical pop-up tile + the shared island board** are the centerpiece. The tiles are:

- **Organic shapes** — leaf-like or mushroom-like silhouettes with tabbed edges; no straight lines (per the design brief). 5-6 edges per tile; interlocking.
- **Three-face printing** — each tile's left-closed, open-up, right-closed states painted distinctly. Art emphasizes 3D vertical structure in the open-up state.
- **Tile types** — ~6-8 distinct types (Tree, Canal, Road, Bridge, Meadow, Cottage, Temple, Market — each with a 3-state identity).

The **shared island board** begins with ~15 tree-tiles in closed-left state (pristine forest). Empty island territories surround the initial forest for players to place into.

## Inspiration / lineage

- **Carcassonne (Wrede, 2000; BGG 822)** — edge-matching tile placement on shared board; scoring per connected feature. UNFOLD borrows the tile-placement + shared-board spine but replaces feature-scoring with asymmetric cultural scoring.
- **Suburbia (Isensee, 2012; BGG 123260)** — tile-placement urban sprawl; building types with adjacency bonuses. UNFOLD borrows the sprawl frame but adds 3D pop-up transformation.
- **Pop-up books (publishing tradition, not a game)** — the architectural bet. Pop-up books transform flat pages into 3D vistas via folded paper engineering. UNFOLD is the first board game (to our knowledge) to use pop-up physics as the primary state mechanic.
- **Sprawlopolis (McPherson, 2018; BGG 251658)** — asymmetric scoring per game via card combinations. UNFOLD's cultural-lens scoring is closer to persistent asymmetric roles.
- **Quadropolis (Boelinger, 2016; BGG 184267)** — urban planning with placement. Similar aesthetic; different mechanic.
- **Feng shui / wabi-sabi / permaculture / modernist traditions** — not games, but cultural frames that define what "good" means. UNFOLD treats these as scoring lenses.

**The novelty claim (Chvátil anchor)**: no published board game combines (1) pop-up physical 3-state tiles with (2) culture-lens asymmetric scoring on (3) a shared island. Remove any one and UNFOLD is a different game. Architecture passes the Chvátil re-skin test.

## Core mechanic details (to be refined in design.md)

### Cultural asymmetry (v1.1 — 4 base cultures; minimal tools)

**Panel feedback (Feld, K-K):** 6 cultures is cognitive overload at 4p; Feng Shui / Wabi-Sabi / Permaculture overlap as nature-aesthetic lenses. **Cut to 4 base cultures** with distinct non-overlapping aesthetic axes. Wabi-Sabi and Indigenous Steward deferred to "Nature Cultures Expansion."

**Four base cultures** (each with 4 asymmetric elements):

| Culture | Aesthetic axis | Tool (1-action modifier) | Spirit visual | Ideal Dev Level |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Feng Shui** (Eastern directional) | cardinal alignment + water-flow + balance | PLACE can rotate tile 90° free (align to compass axes) | Compass / lantern | 20-30 (balance) |
| **Medieval Village** (European fortified) | road network + central temple + walled perimeter | PLACE can add a short wall-edge between tiles | Knight banner | 25-35 (developed but not sprawling) |
| **Permaculture** (regenerative) | tree-water adjacency + building-on-forest-edge | PLACE-tree-state once per turn is free (0 actions) | Sapling / seedling | 10-25 (preserved with settlements) |
| **Modernist Grid** (urban-planning) | dense building clusters + road efficiency + minimal green | PLACE road-state tile triggers up to 2 extras if adjacent | Pylon / modern sculpture | 30-40 (dense) |

**Each culture has 4 asymmetric elements:**

1. **Aesthetic scoring lens** (same as before; per-culture criteria define "good island" differently).
2. **Unique Tool** (small 1-action modifier — cultural technique).
3. **Spirit token** (place on Temple; claims adjacent 6-8 tiles; double-scores adjacent criteria).
4. **Ideal Development Level** (per-culture target on shared threshold track; distance from ideal modifies endgame score).

**Shared Development Level track** (NEW v1.1 — P1 answer): one communal axis from 0 (pristine) to 40 (full development). Every tile placement or flip modifies it:
- Place or flip to open-up: +1 Development
- Place or flip to closed-right: +0.5 Development
- Place or flip to closed-left: 0 Development (natural state)

At endgame, each player's cultural score is multiplied by a **Development Alignment** factor based on |actual Dev Level − your culture's ideal|. Close = bonus; far = penalty.

**Thematic payoff:** each Temple tile looks different depending on whose Spirit inhabits it. A temple with no Spirit is a neutral shrine; with a Spirit, it becomes that culture's sacred site (visual: pagoda / church / grove-center / civic center).

**Deferred to expansion** (Nature Cultures Expansion): Wabi-Sabi (asymmetric-beauty / transience), Indigenous Steward (minimum intervention / sacred sites).

### Tile state system

Each tile has exactly 3 states (physical orientations):
- **◀ closed-left** — a "quiet" state (grass, closed canal, clearing, meadow)
- **▲ open-up** — a "structured" state (tree, bridge, building, tower, temple)
- **▶ closed-right** — an "active but flat" state (road, pathway, wall, waterway)

State examples per tile type (working):

| Tile type | ◀ closed-left | ▲ open-up | ▶ closed-right |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tree | grass | big tree (3D) | road |
| Canal | closed canal | bridge/toll (3D) | waterway |
| Cottage | garden | 3D cottage | courtyard path |
| Temple | shrine-ground | 3D temple | stone-walled plaza |
| Market | empty square | 3D market stalls | trading road |
| Bridge | river | 3D bridge | stepped waterway |

Each tile physically encodes the 3 states. Flipping between them is the game's most tactile action.

### Action economy (v1.1 — tightened after Knizia/Rosenberg feedback)

**Turn: 2 actions per turn.** Each action is ONE of:

- **PLACE** a new tile (1 action). Draw from tile pool; choose state (closed-left, open-up, or closed-right); place on island adjacent to existing tiles.
- **FLIP** an existing tile (1 action). Choose any tile on the island (yours or opponent's); cycle its state one step (left→up→right→left).
- **MOVE SPIRIT** (1 action). Move your Spirit from one Temple to another (path of placed tiles required).
- **USE TOOL** (1 action). Apply your culture's unique tool (see table above).

**FORCED-FOLD CASCADE (NEW v1.1 — P2 answer)** — triggered on every PLACE or FLIP:

> When you change a tile's state, you MUST immediately cause one adjacent tile to state-change in the OPPOSITE direction. Open one → close a neighbor. Close one → open a neighbor.

- The cascading neighbor is chosen by YOU (the acting player).
- The cascading tile's state-change follows the rule: if your action opened a tile (closed → open-up), the cascade closes a neighbor (open-up → closed-left OR closed-right, your choice).
- The cascade does NOT trigger a further cascade (single-step ripple; keeps resolution fast).

**Why this matters:** pop-up-book physicality as mechanic. You can't change the island without also changing a neighbor. This forces every decision to be a two-tile decision and creates visible mechanical rippling across the board.

**Thematic**: pop-up books conserve energy — one panel opens, another must close. Island has a limited capacity for simultaneous openings.

### Dusk Phase (NEW v1.1 — P3 answer, Rosenberg feeding-equivalent)

When the **Development Level** crosses a threshold (10, 20, 30), a **Dusk Phase** triggers immediately. Every player resolves ONE of three Dusk actions:

1. **REWILD**: flip one closed-right tile to closed-left (remove development; trees return).
2. **CONSOLIDATE**: move your Spirit for free, OR place a free wall-edge (Medieval-style).
3. **ADVANCE**: your culture's Tool can be used twice this round (bonus action economy).

Dusk is mandatory; skipping costs 1 endgame VP. This is the feeding-equivalent Rosenberg flagged as missing — a periodic obligation that converts abundance to shortage and forces strategic commitment.

### Scoring (v1.1 — 4 cultures × Development-Level modifier)

Each player picks a culture at game start. At endgame, each player applies their culture's scoring rules to the final shared island state AND multiplies by the **Development Alignment** factor.

**The 4 base cultures score:**

- **Feng Shui** — cardinal-aligned rows (tiles along N-S or E-W axes), water-flow pairs (canal → bridge → canal), curved paths (non-straight road runs). Penalty for sharp-angle sightlines through Temples.
- **Medieval Village** — connected road length × 1 VP, buildings adjacent to Temple × 2 VP, walled perimeter around a cluster × bonus. Penalty for detached buildings (no road connection).
- **Permaculture** — tree tiles adjacent to canal (+2 VP), buildings touching tree-edge (+1 VP), layered greens (3 trees in a row across closed-left / open-up / closed-right = +5 VP). Penalty for cleared areas >3 tiles of non-tree.
- **Modernist Grid** — dense building clusters (3+ open-up buildings adjacent = +3 VP per cluster), road-network length × 1 VP, penalty for green-space wasted (trees without adjacent buildings).

**Development Alignment factor** (NEW v1.1):
- |actual Dev Level − your culture's ideal| ≤ 3: ×1.5 multiplier on cultural score
- |actual − ideal| 4-8: ×1.0
- |actual − ideal| 9-15: ×0.8
- |actual − ideal| >15: ×0.5

**This means**: a Modernist player whose island ended at Dev Level 38 (ideal 30-40) gets ×1.5. A Permaculture player whose island ended at Dev Level 38 (ideal 10-25) gets ×0.5. Same island, opposite outcomes — the SHARED THRESHOLD makes every placement a collision between cultures' ideals.

**Spirit bonus**: when your Spirit occupies a Temple at endgame, the 6-8 tiles adjacent to that Temple score DOUBLE for your culture's criteria (unchanged from v1.0).

Each culture's scoring specifics refined in design.md. Key property: cultures are **incommensurable** (different formulas) AND **mechanically linked** (same Dev Level; contested Temples). No player can win by only scoring their own lane — they must navigate the shared track.

### Interaction (locked answer: **shared board**)

One island. All players place and flip tiles on it. Your flip changes my score; my flip changes yours. Maximum physical-board interaction.

## Non-goals

- Not theme-forward beyond "cultural aesthetic lenses" — no fiction / world-building beyond the cultures themselves.
- No combat, no catastrophes, no resource-gathering economy (just tile-place + flip).
- No individual player boards — the island is the only board.
- No solo mode in v1.0 (could add later).
- No randomized tile-draw from hidden hand (for now) — public market or set tile pool.
- No tech tree / no progression across rounds — placement + flipping IS the game.
- No BGG ID yet (original design; may publish later).
