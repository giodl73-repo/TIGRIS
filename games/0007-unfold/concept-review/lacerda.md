# Lacerda on UNFOLD — concept review

I want to like this. The artifact-as-story is ambitious — physical 3-state tiles on a shared island, with cultural lenses reading the same board differently. That is closer to Lisboa's "one machine, five angles" ambition than any concept I have seen from TIGRIS. Before I score, I have to stress-test whether the three systems *gear* or merely *coexist*.

## Diagnostic: do the subsystems gear multiplicatively?

Name the three subsystems: (1) pop-up tri-state placement, (2) cultural-lens scoring, (3) Spirit-on-Temple claim. Now I ask my Lisboa question: does each change the value of the other two?

- **State choice ↔ cultural lens.** Yes — the Medieval player wants the right-closed "road" state; the Permaculturist wants the open-up "tree" state on the same tile. One tile, two state-choices, two score-deltas. That is one gear tooth.
- **Cultural lens ↔ Spirit.** Yes — Spirit doubles adjacent scoring by *your* lens, so Spirit placement is a lens-amplifier, not a generic bonus. Second tooth.
- **State choice ↔ Spirit.** Weaker. Spirit sits on Temple tiles (open-up state). Flipping a Temple displaces the Spirit. The state-machine touches Spirit only through Temple tiles — one specific type out of six. That is a narrow gear, not a broad one.

So: two strong gear-teeth, one narrow one. This is multiplicative in the Medieval-vs-Permaculture placement duel, but the Spirit subsystem is partially bolted on. Compare On Mars, where every resource action touches three subsystems. UNFOLD at concept stage has roughly 2.3 subsystem dependencies per action. Acceptable, not signature.

## Can this be re-skinned?

Strip the cultural names and call them "Red player scores roads, Blue player scores trees." The lens asymmetry survives. That is the test the concept partly fails. The cultures are currently *scoring functions with names attached*. Feng shui's cardinal-alignment rule is structural; wabi-sabi's "imperfect beauty" is prose aspiring to be a rule. Lisboa earned its theme by making the 1755 earthquake a temporal force that *reshapes the action economy*. UNFOLD's cultures must reshape the action economy of the culture that holds them — the Tool and Spirit are the right instinct, but six lines of "modifies one action" is thin. Kanban EV's departments feel like departments because the rules *require* you to act as that department. The cultures here do not yet require anything of the player except a scoring filter.

## Parallel scoring or genuine asymmetry?

The concept states "no two cultures value the same thing, so player strategies collide." Collide on placement, yes — the shared board is where the interaction lives. But each culture's scoring runs in parallel at endgame. No culture's score depends on another culture's state, adjacency, or presence. That is parallel scoring, not asymmetric gearing. A genuine asymmetry would have (e.g.) the Indigenous Steward's preservation VP *increase* when a Modernist builds nearby — sacred-by-contrast. Without that, the cultures are six independent scorepads sharing a tile pool.

## Scores

**B1 System Gearing — 6/10 at 3-4p, 5/10 at 2p.** Two strong gear-teeth (state↔lens, lens↔Spirit), one narrow (Spirit↔state via Temple only). Remove the Spirit subsystem and the game still functions — Lisboa fails that test. The concept has room to tighten before design.md.

**B4 Information Transparency Cost — 4/10.** Concept specifies "no randomized tile-draw from hidden hand — public market or set tile pool" and "cultures fixed at start." Everything is open. There is no economy of paying-to-learn; nothing is hidden worth learning. My axis does not earn here. I would consider hidden culture reveal at midgame, or face-down tile states until adjacency forces reveal.

**A7 Emergence-Replayability — 7/10.** Six cultures choose-4-of-6 at 4p yields 15 culture-combinations; variable starting seed multiplies further. The 10th play will find cross-lens combinations invisible at the start. The concept's strongest axis. If the cultural gearing tightens per above, this climbs to 8.

## Flag for design.md

Make one culture's scoring depend on another culture's board-state. Then we can talk about the Kanban-EV lineage with a straight face.
