# Feld — UNFOLD Concept Review

*"Every turn should give you something — and something you didn't ask for."*

## Opening read

The structural bet is interesting to me. Six incommensurable scoring lenses on one shared canvas, place-or-flip as the action grammar, 3 actions per turn — that is a decision-density architecture on paper. I am not here for the pop-up tactile story; I am here for the conversion math. Let me stress-test it.

## A2 Decision Density — score: 6.8 (target count: 3-4p)

Three actions per turn across a menu of PLACE (1 action, 3 state choices at placement) and FLIP (2 actions, changes a tile on the shared board) produces a live multi-axis turn on paper. At 3p-4p with a populated island mid-arc, a player is weighing: place-which-tile-in-which-state, flip-whose-tile, move-Spirit, trigger-culture-modifier. That is the Trajan menu density I want to see.

My concern: action economy at 3 actions means a turn that burns 2 on a FLIP plus 1 on a PLACE is a two-beat turn, not three. The concept does not specify whether unused actions bank or whether sub-action micro-choices (rotation under Feng Shui, free tree-place under Permaculture) multiply the menu. If modifiers are the density multiplier, they need to fire every turn, not once per game. Clarify in design.md or density drops to ~5.5.

**Dead-turn risk at A2:** late-arc, when the bag thins and all remaining tile types are in unhelpful states, a Permaculture player with no tree-adjacencies left to exploit is placing into dead air. This is the Castles of Burgundy late-turn trap — rescue it with an end-trigger that fires before the tile pool empties into noise.

## C6 Point-Salad Incommensurability — score: 7.4 (target count: 3-4p)

This is my signature axis and the bet it wants to claim. The test: are the six lenses genuinely incommensurable, or do they collapse to similar scoring gradients?

**Earned:** Indigenous Steward (undisturbed closed-left) and Modernist Grid (dense building clusters, road efficiency) are mechanically opposed — one scores unplaced-forest, the other scores development. Permaculture (tree-adjacent-water-building-edges) and Medieval Village (connected roads, walled clusters around Temples) score structurally different topologies. These pairs collide on placement. The Castles of Burgundy test passes for those four.

**Refuted partially:** Feng Shui (cardinal alignment, curved paths) and Wabi-Sabi (asymmetric, partial-concealment) risk overlap with Permaculture's organic-placement logic. Three lenses that all reward "irregular arrangement with vegetation" is collapsing the salad. Tighten: Feng Shui must score something the other two cannot — specifically, **directional axes** (a scored N-S water-flow line) that wabi-sabi and permaculture do not recognize. Until scoring formulas pin specifics (open question #2 in the concept), I am scoring this at 7.4 on predicted-incommensurability.

**Separate-lanes hazard:** if each culture lives in its own lane and players optimize independently, C6 earns but **A3 Interaction drops**. The flip-at-2-actions cost is load-bearing here — it is the only mechanism forcing cross-lane collision. If flipping rarely happens because 2 actions is too expensive, cultures score parallel and interaction hollows.

## Anti-catch-up (C5) — ignored, correctly

C5 is retired in v2.6 and I am not drafting it. UNFOLD has no plague phase and does not claim one. A catastrophe mechanic grafted onto this architecture would fight the cultural-lens arc — I do not want it here. Note for design.md: the *absence* of a penalty phase means runaway-leader risk is unhedged. The Spirit-displacement cost (2 actions to flip a Temple, Spirit returns to supply) is the closest thing to a catch-up lever. Tune it.

## Structural verdict

The architecture has the right shape for my axes. Decision density is plausible, not proven; point-salad passes for four of six lenses and needs scoring-formula work for the other two. My greatest fear is that the place-or-flip menu, under a 3-action economy, produces forced PLACE turns when the FLIP targets are all distant or scored-by-someone-else. That is the dead-turn pattern.

Move to design.md. Pin the scoring formulas before I re-score.
