# Lacerda on UNFOLD v1.1 — validation pass

I flagged v1.0 at B1 6/10: two strong gear-teeth and one narrow, with cultures
running as parallel scorepads sharing a tile pool. The designer has added a
fourth subsystem (Development Level) and a forced-fold cascade. I want to see
whether the machine now gears, or whether a fourth dial has been bolted to the
same parallel engine.

## Anchor-mechanic §4: four multiplicative systems?

Trace one placement. A Modernist places a Cottage in open-up state.
- System 1 (pop-up architecture): the tile rises; a 3D silhouette appears.
- System 2 (forced-fold cascade): an adjacent tile — say a Permaculturist's tree
  — must close. The Permaculturist's scoring lens *just lost a layered-green
  combo* because of my Cottage.
- System 4 (Development Level): +1 Dev. The track nudges toward Modernist's
  ideal (30-40) and away from Permaculture's (10-25).
- System 3 (cultural lens): my cluster-VP ticks up; Permaculture's penalty
  condition (cleared >3 tiles) crept closer.

One action, four subsystems, each changing the value of the other three. That
is the Lisboa "one machine, five angles" shape I was looking for. §4 now reads
as multiplicative, not additive.

## P1: does Dev Level create cross-culture dependency?

Yes, and structurally. In v1.0, Permaculture's endgame number could be computed
without looking at Modernist's board state. In v1.1 the ×1.5 / ×1.0 / ×0.8 /
×0.5 multiplier means my Modernist opponent pushing Dev from 25 to 32 is
*directly* subtracting from my Permaculture score — not by blocking a tile, but
by moving the shared dial past my tolerance band. That is the Kanban-EV
department lineage: the track *is* the interaction. Cultures are no longer six
independent scorepads; they are four opposing vectors on a common axis.

## The Lisboa test

Remove any subsystem and count what loses anchor value.
- Remove Dev Level → cultures revert to parallel scoring (v1.0 failure).
- Remove forced-fold cascade → placement stops rippling; 3-state physicality is
  decoration; Dev Level still moves but cheaply.
- Remove cultural lens → Dev Level becomes a single scalar race; no collision.
- Remove pop-up 3-state → cascade has no direction to flip into; Dev Level has
  no source signal.

Four-for-four. Every subsystem is load-bearing for at least two others. That is
the passing condition.

## Residual concerns

The Spirit-Temple subsystem I flagged as narrow in v1.0 is unchanged. It is now
the weakest gear — still touching only Temple tiles, still additive-feeling
next to the new Dev-track spine. Not a blocker for B1, but I'd revisit it in
design.md: a Spirit whose presence *shifted the Dev-Level contribution* of
adjacent tiles would tie the sixth tooth in.

## Score

**B1 System Gearing — 8.5/10 at 3-4p, 7/10 at 2p.** The Dev-Level track and
forced-fold cascade convert three-subsystem coexistence into four-subsystem
gearing. Earns the axis. Greenlight to design.md.
