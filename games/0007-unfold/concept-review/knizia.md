# Knizia — UNFOLD concept review

## Verdict

Hold. The architecture is novel, but the math is undeclared. Six cultural scoring lenses on a shared canvas is an ambitious parallel-Ingenious; the concept does not yet show the conversion curves, and without them I cannot tell whether C1 Tension Budget is present or whether the 3-action turn is a free lunch. Approve the novelty premise, refuse the move to DESIGN until scoring shape and action economy are pinned in numbers.

## Three concerns

**1. Action economy: 3 actions, PLACE=1, FLIP=2 — the ratio is wrong against C1 Tension Budget.**
At 3 actions a turn a player can PLACE + FLIP, or PLACE ×3. Flipping an opponent's tile costs your entire turn minus one. In Samurai an action commits you fully; in Ra a bid commits your tile-bank. Here the PLACE=1 price makes placement cheap — every turn a player lays a new tile *and* still has 2 actions spare. C1 requires the player ends each turn knowing the next best action and unable to take it. At this budget they take it. Fix: either drop to 2 actions per turn, or raise PLACE to 2 and keep FLIP at 2, so the choice between "build mine" and "break yours" is exclusive, not additive.

**2. Scoring shape: six incommensurable lenses, no minimum rule — C2 is explicitly refuted in the concept and nothing replaces it.**
The concept predicts C2 refutation and celebrates it. I will not. Six parallel scoring functions with no cross-lens coupling is point-salad by construction (C6 anchor-10 — the concept claims this as a greenlight). But point salad without a forcing rule means each player optimises their own track in isolation; the shared board becomes a coincidence, not a contest. Ingenious forces breadth via minimum-color. T&E forces breadth via four-aspect minimum. UNFOLD's shared island has spatial collision but no scoring collision — my Permaculture score ignores your Modernist density entirely. Either add a cross-cultural minimum (lowest-lens-score component × multiplier, à la B6), or add a shared island-health score every player shares, or accept that breadth is not forced and C1 weakens.

**3. End-trigger: unspecified. Open question #6 lists four candidates, none chosen.**
Ra's sun-track, T&E's tile-bag depletion, Modern Art's four-round cap — each earns its keep by creating the "one turn before" drama. UNFOLD lists "tile bag empty? Island filled? Player triggers?" as an open question. The end-trigger is not a detail. It is the instrument that converts placement-greed into regret. Pick one at concept stage. Bag-depletion is the obvious Knizia choice because it makes the tile pool itself the clock, and it interacts with FLIP (every flip is a turn not drawing, so flips drain time differently than placements).

## Two greenlights

**1. Pop-up 3-state tile earns its keep on B5 Architectural Novelty.**
Three printed faces per tile × flippable-by-action is a structural bet I have not seen. Remove it and UNFOLD collapses into Carcassonne+cultures. The state-change cost (2 actions) creates a genuine temporal lever: the board at turn 20 is not the board at turn 10 because tiles have *moved through state*, not merely accumulated. This is the Tzolk'in-gears-class novelty claim that Chvátil's anchor requires.

**2. Shared island + asymmetric lens produces interaction without aggression.**
Modern Art puts players in the same auction room with different portfolios; UNFOLD puts them on the same island with different aesthetics. A3 Interaction and D2 Spatial-Interaction Presence both earn here because a flip is a reinterpretation — it changes what another player's board *is*, not what they have. That is Samurai-class indirect conflict, correctly built.

## One high-leverage revision

**Add a shared-island scoring component that every culture contributes to and every culture reads.**

Take 20–30% of total VP and make it a communal track — call it Island Coherence, or steal Ra's civilization-count idea. Every tile placed adds to it; every disruptive flip subtracts. Each culture scores its share via its own lens, but the track itself is shared. This does three things at once: it creates C1 Tension Budget (spend actions on my lens or the shared floor?), it imports C2 Minimum-Score Shape through the back door (a player who ignores the shared track loses regardless of lens score), and it ties the end-trigger to board-state saturation rather than a naked tile count. One rule, three axes, no new components.

## Stake scores

**B5 Architectural Novelty (concept target):** 8.0/10. Pop-up 3-state tile on shared board is a structural bet without a sibling. Point dropped for manufacturing fragility — if the pop-up cannot be produced, the architecture is theoretical.

**C1 Tension Budget (my primary):** 4.5/10. The 3-action turn at PLACE=1 / FLIP=2 leaves slack every round. No resource scarcity, no hand limit, no feeding phase. The Spirit token is the only contested scarce object and there are 6 of them across 4 players — abundant. Fix the action economy and this climbs to 7.
