# UNFOLD Concept Review — Panel Synthesis

## Headline

**Architecture is ambitious; mechanical glue is loose.** All 8 designer voices acknowledge the architectural-novelty claim (B5 mean 7.25 where scored — earning range), but four voices converge on the same critique: **the six cultures score in parallel lanes rather than colliding**. The pop-up's 3D physicality is decorative, not mechanical. Action economy has slack. Before design.md, concept.md needs one structural revision and three tuning fixes.

**Weighted mean: ~7.25** — would earn as a stake in a Tier-B argument, but not comfortably. The anchor persona (Chvátil) scored lowest, and his critique drives the top punchlist item.

## Key cluster findings (multi-voice concerns)

### Cluster 1 — Cultures run parallel, not geared (4 voices: Knizia, Lacerda, Feld, Vaccarino)

**The dominant structural critique.** Each culture scores the shared island by its own rules, but no culture's scoring depends on another's choices. Result: point-salad without collision. Players can play in their own scoring lane and win without direct confrontation with opponents' cultural logic.

- **Knizia**: "No cross-lens forcing rule — six parallel scoring functions on a shared board is point-salad by construction without a minimum-shape or shared track to punish isolation."
- **Lacerda**: "Two strong gear-teeth (state↔lens, lens↔Spirit) but Spirit↔state is narrow; cultures run as parallel scoring rather than genuine asymmetric gearing."
- **Feld**: "Separate-lanes hazard would hollow A3 Interaction if FLIP cost deters cross-cultural collision."
- **Vaccarino**: "Low variance is load-bearing, but the 6 cultures × 4 seeds matrix must do the arc-generation work Dominion's shuffle does — currently under-defended."

### Cluster 2 — Pop-up physicality doesn't pay rent (1 voice, anchor)

**Chvátil (anchor B5 score 5/10)**: "The pop-up doesn't pay rent. Open-up needs a rule that exploits the 3D-ness — adjacency collision, line-of-sight blocking, or forced-fold cascades — otherwise it's Tzolk'in's gears without the gears computing anything."

Single voice, but THE anchor — his stake is the bet UNFOLD claims. If the pop-up is decorative, the architectural-novelty claim collapses.

### Cluster 3 — Action economy slack (2 voices: Knizia, Rosenberg)

- **Knizia**: "Action economy (3 actions, PLACE=1, FLIP=2) leaves slack every turn and fails C1 Tension Budget. A player lays a tile and still has 2 actions spare." Scored C1 at 4.5/10.
- **Rosenberg**: "Missing feeding-equivalent, flat mid-game engine, flip-weight contingent on cultural-lens divergence." Scored C3 Scarcity Bite 4/10, C4 Engine-Garden 3/10 — both well below threshold.

### Cluster 4 — Too many cultures, some overlap (2 voices: Feld, K-K)

- **Feld**: "Overlap risk between Feng Shui / Wabi-Sabi / Permaculture — three nature-aesthetic lenses create redundant scoring."
- **K-K**: "Six-culture load at 4p is above family-tier threshold; recommend cutting to four in base."

### Cluster 5 — Count-robustness and first-hour teach (2 voices: Stegmaier, K-K)

- **Stegmaier** (harshest review): D3 Count-Robustness 3/10, A6 Teachability 4.5/10. "No 2p tuning, no solo, cultural-lens cross-legibility wall."
- **K-K**: "Can this be taught in 15 minutes? Currently 6 cultural scoring systems is too many to hold in one head."

## Greenlights consensus (multi-voice)

1. **Architectural ambition is real.** Where-scored B5 averages 7.25. Chvátil's 5 is conditional on a specific fix; without that fix the axis underperforms.

## Punchlist for concept.md v2 (ordered by cluster strength)

### P1 — Cultures must gear mechanically (Cluster 1, 4 voices — HIGHEST priority)

Add a **cross-culture interaction rule**. At least one culture's scoring must depend on another culture's board-state. Candidate mechanisms:

- **Opponent-spirit modifier**: Feng Shui's alignment score is disrupted by opponent Spirits within N tiles (their cultural presence breaks the harmony).
- **Competing claim overlap**: Permaculture's tree-adjacency bonus requires NO opponent's road within N; an opponent placing a road actively disrupts your permaculture score.
- **Shared-threshold track**: one axis (e.g., "Development Level") is communal — every building placed counts toward it; each culture has a DIFFERENT ideal level (Modernist wants high; Indigenous wants low). Crossing the threshold reshapes scoring.
- **Spirit-territory gearing**: a culture's scoring in a zone is triggered ONLY if their Spirit is the nearest-adjacent Temple's occupant. Cross-culture territorial contest drives argument.

Lacerda's specific proposal: "at least one culture's scoring depends on another culture's board-state." Commit to this for v2.

### P2 — Make pop-up 3D mechanically load-bearing (Cluster 2, anchor voice)

Chvátil's three candidate mechanisms:

- **Adjacency collision** — two open-up tiles adjacent cause something (score bonus, forced flip, blocked action).
- **Line-of-sight blocking** — open-up tiles block closed-left tiles behind them from being flipped by certain actions.
- **Forced-fold cascades** — flipping one tile cascades to adjacent tiles (pop-up book physicality: open one panel, adjacent panels move).

**Pick one.** Commit in concept.md v2. If UNFOLD can't name a physical-3D-specific rule, the architectural-novelty claim is decorative.

**Recommended**: forced-fold cascades. Most tactile. Most pop-up-book-authentic. When you place/flip a tile, at least one adjacent tile MUST also state-change (in a specified direction). This creates mechanical ripple effects; pop-up physicality becomes gameplay.

### P3 — Tighten action economy (Cluster 3, 2 voices)

Current: 3 actions, PLACE=1, FLIP=2. Slack per Knizia.

Options:
- **A** — Reduce to **2 actions per turn**. PLACE=1, FLIP=1 (make flipping cheaper, not costlier — encourages frequent use). Decisions per turn go up (4-5 meaningful choices); slack goes down.
- **B** — Add a **feeding-phase-equivalent** every N rounds (Rosenberg's "Dusk phase"): undeveloped tiles spread into re-wilding, forcing development action or loss of territory.
- **C** — Make FLIP = 1 (cheap; emphasize transformation) + add CASCADE cost (if forced-fold adopted, cascades cost 1 extra).

**Recommended**: A+C combo. 2 actions per turn; PLACE=1, FLIP=1. Tighter decisions. Forced-cascade (from P2) adds spillover cost.

### P4 — Cut base cultures 6 → 4 (Cluster 4, 2 voices)

Proposed base-4 (distinct aesthetic axes, no overlap):
- **Feng Shui** — alignment + water-flow (Eastern directional)
- **Medieval Village** — road network + central temple (European fortified)
- **Permaculture** — tree-water adjacency + building-on-edge (regenerative)
- **Modernist Grid** — dense clusters + efficiency (urban)

Cultures CUT from base: Wabi-Sabi, Indigenous Steward. Reserved for future expansion. (User can reverse this call — maybe Wabi-Sabi is essential to the pop-up-book aesthetic.)

### P5 — 2p tuning + solo consideration (Cluster 5, 2 voices)

Design.md should include:
- 2p variant (smaller island, ~2 cultures)
- 4p as design target
- Solo mode deferred to v1.1 (not blocker)

First-hour teach: each culture gets a one-page reference card with iconography. Cultural scoring explained visually, not as rules text.

## Specific revisions (what to change in concept.md)

1. **Add §13: "Cross-culture gearing rule"** — lock in P1 mechanism. Recommended: Spirit-territory gearing (culture's scoring in a zone triggered by nearest-Temple Spirit ownership).
2. **Update §4 Anchor Mechanic** — add explicit "3D pop-up physicality mechanic" subsection. Recommend forced-fold cascades.
3. **Update §"Core mechanic details" → "Flip mechanic"** — revise action economy to 2 actions per turn (P3).
4. **Update §"Core mechanic details" → "Cultural asymmetry"** — cut to 4 base cultures (P4). Move Wabi-Sabi/Indigenous Steward to "future expansion" list.
5. **Update §"Non-goals"** — remove "No solo mode" line (defer to v1.1 rather than explicit non-goal); add "base game tuned for 2-4p".
6. **Update §"Open questions"** — acknowledge cross-culture gearing as decision #11.
