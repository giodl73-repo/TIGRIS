# Stegmaier on UNFOLD (concept)

*"The island is a shared canvas, but the teach is six canvases stapled together. That's the whole review."*

UNFOLD is architecturally ambitious in the Chvátil sense — pop-up tiles + cultural lenses + shared island is a genuine structural bet, and I won't contest the novelty claim. My lens is narrower: can a table of four actually sit down and play this on the first night? The concept as drafted is walking a knife-edge on teachability, and the 2p tuning is not yet addressed.

## Scores

### A6 Teachability — **4.5 / 10** (at 4p, first teach)

The 3-state tile system is teachable — ◀ quiet / ▲ structured / ▶ flat is three icons and a flip cost. That's a Scythe-mat candidate: put the three states on every tile edge and the rulebook shrinks by a page. I'd score this 8+ if it shipped alone.

What drops the score is **the cultural-lens wall**. Six cultures × distinct scoring criteria + one tool modifier + one Spirit interaction = six mini-rulebooks the table has to internalize to play credibly. At 4p, each player knows their own culture but must also know the other three to evaluate interaction — *my flip of your road changes your Medieval-Village score, and I need to know that to decide whether to spend 2 actions on it*. That's not a teach — that's a study session. Compare Wingspan: every bird card is self-contained iconography; you don't memorize other players' engines. UNFOLD as drafted requires cross-culture legibility, and the concept.md does not specify an iconography plan for the scoring conditions.

**Mitigation path:** every culture's scoring criteria must reduce to ≤ 3 pictograms printed on the culture card, and the culture cards must be public and readable across the table. If the Permaculture card says "trees touching water = 2; building on tree-edge = 1; layered greens cluster = 3" in text, this is a 4.5. If it says that in three icons anyone can parse at a glance, this is a 7.

### C8 First-Turn Compression — **6 / 10** (at 4p)

Turn 1 has a live decision: place a tile (choose one of 3 states) or flip an existing forest-tree. That's a meaningful choice against your culture card — the Modernist immediately wants to flip a closed-left tree to ▶ road; the Indigenous Steward wants to place and preserve. Good. This is a Scythe-caliber opening in principle.

What costs 3 points: **placement-order dependency**. With 15 pre-placed forest tiles and 3 open players before you at 4p, turn 1 for player 4 is evaluating a board that player 1 never had to evaluate. The Modernist at seat 1 makes a clean engine-start decision; the Indigenous Steward at seat 4 is already reacting to three flips of their sacred forest. That's not a compressed first turn — that's a reactive first turn with 40 seconds of prior events to parse. Mitigate with a simultaneous-action opener or a "sacred-seed" rule (2 forest tiles immune on round 1).

### D3 Count-Robustness — **3 / 10** (concept as drafted)

This is the scoring blocker and the axis I anchor on. The concept specifies 2-4 players with "(likely best at 3-4)" — that phrasing itself is the red flag from the Stegmaier diagnostic set. Every concept I've shipped that said "likely best at 3-4" turned out to be a 4p game with a bolted-on 2p variant. The island — 60 tiles, 15 pre-placed forest, shared interaction via flips — scales its interaction density quadratically with player count. At 2p, half the flip-interaction evaporates; the board becomes two players politely building around each other. At 4p, the flip-economy is live and cultures collide as intended.

The design as drafted has **no 2p-specific tuning notes**. Culture count scales (2 of 6 at 2p), but board size, tile pool (60), starting forest (15), and action economy (3/turn) are static. That's how you get a 4p game that's "ok" at 2p. I'd want: reduced island footprint at 2p (40 tiles?), fewer pre-placed forest, and ideally an automa — the Spirit token mechanic is a natural automa seed (a neutral culture whose Spirit moves on a simple rule).

No solo mode is called out as non-goal. That's a ship-blocker for a Stegmaier-tradition release; it's a concept-stage flag for UNFOLD.

## Verdict

Architecturally a greenlight. Accessibility — not yet. Three fixes before design.md: (1) iconography plan for all six culture scorings, (2) 2p-specific tuning section, (3) first-turn seat-order mitigation. Land those and A6 moves to 7+, D3 to 6+. Leave them and this is a 4p-only release.
