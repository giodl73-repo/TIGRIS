# Chvátil on UNFOLD concept

## Opening verdict

The concept is trying to do three novel things at once, and only one of them is actually load-bearing. The pop-up tile is the Tzolk'in-gears move — a physical mechanism that is supposed to *be* the game, not decorate it. The culture-lens scoring is a C6 Point-Salad Incommensurability play dressed as architecture. The shared island is Carcassonne with extra steps. Stack them and the concept reads as architecturally ambitious; interrogate them and I can remove two of the three and still have a game. That is the failure mode of my own worst pitches (the Galaxy Trucker prototype where the real-time build was optional — we cut it; the game died). The architectural bet needs to be the pop-up, not the trio. Right now the concept is hiding behind the trio.

## Three concerns

1. **The pop-up does not yet pay rent on B5 Architectural Novelty.** A 3-state tile where flipping costs 2 actions is mechanically a rotation token with extra manufacturing. The pop-up *physics* — the fact that open-up is literally taller, casts a shadow, occludes sightlines, cannot coexist with an adjacent open-up without collision — none of that is in the concept as a rule. If the open-up state is just "face C" with a picture of a tree on it, the pop-up is production value, not architecture. Compare Tzolk'in: the gears aren't decorative, they *compute* the return-timer. What does the pop-up compute that a 3-face rotating tile doesn't?

2. **Culture-lens scoring collapses A3 Interaction into parallel solitaire.** The concept says "your flip changes my score" — but if six cultures score six incommensurable things, your flip mostly changes *your own* projection of the board, because you can't reliably predict what hurts the Permaculture player without holding their card in your head. At 4p with 4 cultures on a shared island, the decision to flip becomes either (a) optimize my lens, ignore theirs, or (b) paralysis from tracking four scoring functions. Codenames works because there are two teams and one semantic field. UNFOLD is proposing four semantic fields on one physical field. That is a Decision Density (A2) trap, not a feature.

3. **The Spirit token is a fourth system smuggled in after "minimize asymmetry."** The concept says user wants cultural asymmetry minimized, then gives each culture a Tool AND a Spirit AND a scoring lens. Three asymmetric dimensions per culture times six cultures is thirty-six interactions to balance. This is the Dungeon Lords trap — I added evil-level scoring because it produced a great story, and it added 40 minutes to the teach. A6 Teachability at the stated ~2.5 weight cannot absorb Tool + Spirit + Lens + 3-state tiles + shared island. Something goes.

## Two greenlights

1. **The "same island, different ideals" emotional deliverable is a real one.** This is the Codenames move — a shared object that two minds read differently. If the culture-lens can produce the moment where a player stares at a tile another player just flipped and thinks "they just made my sacred grove into their marketplace," that is an A3 Interaction payoff no other tile-placer delivers. The thematic-as-shaping-force instinct is correct.

2. **"Starting forest / preservation tension" is the right narrative spine for C6 Point-Salad Incommensurability.** Beginning with 15 pre-placed closed-left tree tiles gives the Indigenous Steward a full score at turn zero, which every other culture must erode to score. That asymmetric starting position does architectural work — it makes the first placement a morally-weighted decision, not an optimization. Keep this.

## One specific revision

**Make the pop-up physically constrain placement and flipping, or drop it.** Right now open-up is a label. Give it a rule: *two open-up tiles cannot be orthogonally adjacent* (they collide physically), or *an open-up tile blocks line-of-sight between adjacent tiles for scoring-adjacency purposes*, or *flipping a tile to open-up forces adjacent open-ups to fold back to closed-left*. Pick one. Without a rule where the 3D-ness of the pop-up is the mechanic, B5 is hand-waved and the tile is a component upgrade, not an architecture. The concept must answer: what does a pop-up tile do that a 3-face flat tile cannot?

## B5 Architectural Novelty score

**5/10.** The trio is novel as a combination, but only the pop-up is a candidate for a load-bearing signature, and the concept has not yet made the pop-up load-bearing — it is currently a 3-state rotation with production cost. Fix the revision above and this moves to 8.
