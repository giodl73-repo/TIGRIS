# Le Havre — Design (Review Import)

## Overview

Le Havre is a 1–5 player resource-collection and building-conversion Euro game designed by Uwe Rosenberg. Published 2008 (Lookout Games). BGG weight 3.82. BGG rank: Strategy Games top 50.

---

## Rules summary (for TIGRIS review)

### Core loop

Each round: supply phase (add goods to harbor row); player turn (collect goods from harbor OR use a building); end-of-round (feed workers: pay food or take loans).

### Resource conversion chain (B3 design)

The conversion chain is Le Havre's load-bearing mechanism:

**Iron → Coke → Steel → Ship (VP)**

1. Collect Iron from harbor (no conversion; raw resource)
2. Use Charcoal Kiln: Iron → Coke (1-step)
3. Use Steel Mill: Coke → Steel (2-step)
4. Use Shipyard: Steel → Ship (3-step); Ships are permanent VP assets

**Parallel chain: Fish → Smoked Fish (food)**
**Parallel chain: Grain → Bread → Francs (currency)**

Depth-4 conversion across the Iron→Ship chain; depth-3 for Fish and Grain chains.

### Building dependency (C4)

Buildings reduce conversion costs. Early buildings (Smokehouse) reduce feeding costs. Mid-game buildings (Abattoir, Steel Mill) enable conversion chains. Late buildings (Shipyard) produce maximum VP ships but require prior buildings to supply Steel cheaply. This is engine-garden: the value of a Ship built in round 10 depends on 3+ buildings placed in rounds 1–6.

### Starvation mechanic (C3)

Each round-end requires feeding all workers (pay food or take 1 Franc loan per unfed worker). Loans accumulate; 1 Franc loan = 1 VP lost at game end. Neglecting food production = starvation penalty accumulates as VP debt.

### Player count effects (D3)

1-3p: full game; 4-5p: shortened variant. The harbor supply row and building availability adjust. D3 scores moderate — best at 2-3p; 5p is congested.
