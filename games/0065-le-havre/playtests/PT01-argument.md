## Rounds 4-6 — Conversion chain argument

**B3 (Rosenberg anchor) — Defend**: "Round 5: P1 uses Charcoal Kiln to convert 3 Iron → 3 Coke (step 2 of chain). P1 then waits to use Steel Mill (step 3). Next turn: P1 converts 3 Coke → 2 Steel. Next: P1 uses Shipyard: 2 Steel → 1 Large Ship (15 VP). That's 3 turns for the Iron→Ship chain after initial Iron collection (step 1). Depth-4 confirmed across real play: Iron collection → Charcoal conversion → Steel conversion → Shipyard completion. The chain is architecturally canonical — Le Havre is designed around this pipeline. B3 earns. +1 defend."

**C3 (Knizia) — Defend**: "Round 4: P3 has 2 Grain and 1 Fish — enough to feed 3 workers this round. But P3 used the Bakery (Grain → Bread → 2 Francs), converting all Grain to Francs for building purchase. Round-end: P3 must feed 3 workers; has 1 Fish = 1 food. Takes 2 Franc loans. C3 fires: starvation is common in early play and shapes every subsequent decision — exactly as Agricola's feeding phase earns C3 canonically. P3's loan accumulation (now at 2 Francs = -2 VP) creates real consequence. +1 defend."

**B1 (Lacerda) — Defend**: "The conversion chain requires specific buildings at each step. Charcoal Kiln (round 3 build) gates Iron→Coke; Steel Mill (round 5+ build) gates Coke→Steel; Shipyard (round 7+ build) gates Steel→Ship. Remove any building and the downstream steps lose anchor value. B1 fires on multiplicative building dependency. +1 defend."

### B3↔C3 COLLISION — Round 9 (OP, temporal-register)

**Rosenberg (B3 anchor) proposes OP**: "B3 and C3 fire on the same game at different analytical registers. B3 asks: how deep is the conversion pipeline? It evaluates the game's production architecture — how many steps does VP pass through between raw resource and scoring asset? That's a structural design question: Iron passes through 4 transformations. C3 asks: does the feeding-phase / action-shortage actually punish neglect? It evaluates the play-experience consequence — how sharply does starvation bite? That's an experiential question: running the conversion chain is incompatible with feeding your workers in the same turns. B3 = the architecture of the pipeline; C3 = the environmental pressure that makes the pipeline costly to run. HAUL #51 established the same decouple."

**Knizia (C3 stake) seconds OP**: "Second B3↔C3 instance. I confirm OP — the decouple is the same as HAUL: pipeline depth (B3 = structure) vs. scarcity pressure (C3 = pressure within that structure). Different analytical registers."
