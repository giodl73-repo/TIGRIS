# CAULDRON — Concept

## Premise

CAULDRON is a 3–4 player rondel game with 3 orthogonal VP scoring tracks — Guilds, Monuments, and Harvests. Each track is genuinely incommensurable: Guild VP is earned by Worker placement; Monument VP by Stone investment sequences; Harvest VP by seasonal crop completion. None of the three VP currencies is substitutable for the others.

The rondel controls which tracks are accessible in a given round. Critically, certain rondel positions trigger Disaster events — floods, fires, or collapses that permanently damage one track's infrastructure. The B2 secondary: the Disaster risk architecture creates catastrophe pressure that makes rondel navigation dangerous.

Distinct from CRUCIBLE #52 (which anchored on B2 with C6 secondary): CAULDRON anchors on C6 (the incommensurability of the three scoring tracks) with B2 as the secondary design argument (the Disaster events that arise from specific rondel positions).

## Anchor mechanic

**C6 Point-Salad Incommensurability.** Three VP tracks (Guilds, Monuments, Harvests) that cannot be directly compared or converted. A player investing in Monument VP cannot use that investment for Harvest VP. A player behind on Guild VP cannot trade Monument VP to compensate. The tracks are not flavor variations — they require fundamentally incompatible resource investments (Workers vs. Stone vs. Seasonal crops).

**B2 secondary** — Disaster events triggered by specific rondel positions. Flood disasters destroy Harvest infrastructure. Fire disasters damage Guild workshops. Collapse disasters set back Monument progress. Players navigating toward their scoring tracks must avoid or route around Disaster-triggering positions — or deliberately accept risk.
