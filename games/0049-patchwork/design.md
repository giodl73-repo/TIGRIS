# Patchwork — Design (Rules Import)

## Setup

33 polyomino patches arranged in a ring. Time track with 52 spaces (one token each). Neutral pawn starts after the patch marked with a leather button. Both players start with 5 buttons.

## Core turn

The player whose time marker is furthest back on the track acts. They may: (a) Take a patch — one of the 3 patches clockwise from the neutral pawn; pay button cost; advance time marker by the patch's time cost; place on personal 9×9 quilt board. OR (b) Advance time marker to one space ahead of opponent's marker; gain 1 button per space advanced (passing buttons).

After taking a patch, the neutral pawn advances to just past the taken patch.

## Scoring buttons

When either player passes a scoring space (7 of them, marked with a button icon on the time track), they score buttons equal to the number of button-icon patches on their quilt board.

## 1×1 special patches

7 small 1×1 patches are distributed at intervals on the time track. The first player to pass each position claims the 1×1 patch and immediately places it on their board.

## Final scoring

Game ends when both players reach the end space. Count: buttons remaining − 2 × number of empty squares on your 9×9 board. The empty-square penalty makes filling the board critical.

## Key rules

- Patches cannot overlap.
- Patches can be rotated and flipped before placing.
- You cannot skip placing a patch if you purchase it (you must fit it somewhere).

## Weight profile

Weight 1.85. 2-player only. Teach time 5 minutes. Elegant design: one rule for each of the two main actions. The time track creates an automatic turn-order management that's intuitive and teaches itself.
