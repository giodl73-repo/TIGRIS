# Patchwork — Concept

## Premise

Patchwork is a 2-player tile-drafting game where players acquire polyomino quilt patches and place them on a personal 9×9 board. Each patch has a cost in buttons (currency) and time (advances your marker on a shared time track); taking a faster patch means your opponent has fewer turns before game end. Patches produce buttons during scoring phases. End score: buttons − empty squares.

Knizia anchors on C1 Tension Budget — Patchwork is the purest Knizian resource-short game: every patch costs both buttons AND time simultaneously. You cannot optimize one without compromising the other.

## Players and length

- **Players:** 2 only
- **Length:** 20–30 min
- **Weight:** 1.85

## Anchor

**Knizia on C1 Tension Budget** — both currency (buttons) and time (the track) are perpetually short. A 4-button patch that costs 5 time means you advance 5 spaces (opponent gets 5 extra turns). A 1-time patch that costs 3 buttons means spending 3 buttons you need for future patches. The dual shortage is fundamental.

**Collision candidate: C1↔A1** — is the tension budget (C1) separable from the elegance (A1) of the dual-resource mechanic? Knizia would claim they're the same.
