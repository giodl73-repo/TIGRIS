# The Castles of Burgundy — Concept (imported for review)

## Premise

Review of *The Castles of Burgundy* (Stefan Feld, 2011). Feld's most-canonical point-salad game and arguably his most-played. 15th-century Burgundian princes compete to develop estates — dice-driven tile placement across 6 distinct VP tracks (ships, livestock, buildings, workers, tech, bonuses). Consistently cited in top 20 BGG rankings for over a decade.

- **Players:** 2–4 (3p often cited optimal; 4p common)
- **Length:** 30–90 min (≈90 at 4p, ≈60 at 3p)
- **Weight:** ~3.1/5
- **Mechanics:** dice-as-action-menu; estate board (hex grid of 6 region-types); goods depot; worker tiles to modify dice; phased round with livestock/silver market phase; 5 rounds × 5 turns per round = 25 turns per player per game
- **Scoring:** 6 distinct VP sources — building VP by type, filled-region bonuses (scaled by round), shipped goods VP, livestock VP (non-linear), tech-tile VP, endgame bonus-tile VP
- **BGG:** #12 rank cluster circa review date; Alea big-box #16

## Why Castles of Burgundy for game #12

1. **Feld's most-canonical point-salad in published form.** C6's axis-definition example. If C6 can't earn here, the definition fails.
2. **A2 Decision Density canonical case.** Every turn: 2 dice → 4+ possible conversions (tile acquire, tile place, ship, worker, sell goods). Canonical Feld density.
3. **A7 Emergence / Replayability** — variable player boards (base 4 boards + expansion 20+ boards) combined with dice timing produce distinct strategy spaces. Adopted axis test.
4. **A4 Variance Calibration genuine test (non-trivial).** Dice drive action menu. Question: is variance *calibrated* (worker tiles as mitigation tool, flexible conversion) or *noise* (lost turn if you roll wrong)? A4 still live at 10 games; this is A4's strongest non-Scythe test case.
5. **D3 Count-Robustness** — 2p/3p/4p all play; 3p often cited optimal. D3 has 2 earnings (Scythe + ZEN PATH); Castles is a third test toward adoption.
6. **A3 Interaction stress-test.** Castles of Burgundy is the famous "multiplayer solitaire" critique target. A3 is adopted but this is a diagnostic-low instance — retire-explicit likely.
7. **B2 Catastrophe Pressure** — **not applicable** in base Castles (no plague phase, no events deck). B2 retire-explicit expected.

## Artifact-as-story

The **personal estate board + depot + dice** is the centerpiece. Each player's hex-grid estate partitions into 6 terrain types (castle/mine/ship/pasture/building/knowledge), each receiving tiles by matching dice values. Filled regions score: bonus tiles = VP × remaining rounds. Turn = 2 dice, each spent on one of 4 base actions (take tile, place tile, ship, take workers) ± a 5th (sell goods once per phase).
