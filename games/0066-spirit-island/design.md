# Spirit Island — Design (Review Import)

## Overview

Spirit Island is a 1–4 player cooperative area-defense game. Players are Spirits defending an island from invading colonizers. Each Spirit has a unique power deck and a different way of interacting with the island. Players lose if the island is overrun (too many fear cards; too many blight tokens). Players win by achieving an overall Fear threshold or eliminating all Invaders.

BGG weight 4.09. BGG rank: Strategy Games top 20.

---

## Rules summary (for TIGRIS review)

### Core loop

Each round:
1. **Spirit Phase:** Each Spirit selects 1-4 powers to play (fast vs. slow); pays energy.
2. **Invader Phase:** (a) Explore: add new Invader units to unexplored territories; (b) Build: existing Invaders build/grow in their territories; (c) Ravage: Invaders in Ravaged territories damage the island.
3. **Slow Powers:** resolve; often defensive.
4. **Time Passes:** remove some Invader tokens; advance cascade.

### B2 design — Invader cascade catastrophe

The Invader Phase creates escalating catastrophe pressure:
- **Explore** adds Invaders to new territories (the cascade advances each round)
- **Build** upgrades Invader presence (Villages → Cities; more damage per Ravage)
- **Ravage** deals damage: each Invader unit deals 1 damage; Blight accumulates if threshold met

Blight tokens are the catastrophe meter. Each Blight causes cascades: adding Blight to a territory with Blight adds Blight to adjacent territories. The cascade is exponential — neglected territories cascade into neighboring lands. This is B2: escalating catastrophe events that punish preparation failure.

### A3 design — Mandatory cooperative Spirit interaction

Spirits MUST combine powers across the island. No single Spirit can defend the entire island — their power cards cover specific elements and specific terrains. The game requires Spirit players to divide coverage, coordinate timing (fast vs. slow powers), and chain powers across turns. A3 earns via mandatory cooperative coordination: Spirit A cannot win without Spirit B covering territories Spirit A cannot reach.

### B5 design — Novel spirit asymmetry

Each Spirit has a fundamentally different power architecture:
- **River Surges in Sunlight:** fast defensive; rivers are natural barriers
- **Lightning's Swift Strike:** fast offensive; immediate damage, low sustained presence
- **Vital Strength of the Earth:** slow, powerful; deep roots = massive late-game power
- **Shadows Flicker Like Flame:** information-manipulation; move Invader units, change explore targets

These are not variable starting positions — each Spirit is a different theory of how to defend an island. B5 earns on novel asymmetric Spirit architecture.

## Expected analysis angles

- **B2:** Invader cascade = escalating catastrophe events; Blight cascade = exponential punishment
- **A3:** Spirits must combine; no single Spirit covers the island
- **B1:** Spirit energy → powers → board effects → Fear track = pipeline gearing
- **D1:** Accessible (basic Spirits teach in 20 min) + expert (complexity Spirits require 50+ plays)
- **A6:** Teachability varies by Spirit; simple Spirits (River, Lightning) are learnable
- **D3:** Best at 2-4p; 1p (solo) is also designed. Good scaling via Spirit count
- **C1:** Energy is always scarce; Spirit hand management = perpetual tension budget
- **A2:** Per-turn: which powers to play, in which territories, in what order
- **B5:** Novel Spirit asymmetry (each Spirit = different defense theory)
- **A7:** Spirit combination replayability; 5 base Spirits × 4 adversaries = large combination space
