# Azul — Rules Summary (Review Import)

Condensed summary for review purposes. Canonical rules in the 2017 Plan B Games rulebook. Fair-use: critical commentary.

## 1. Overview

2–4 players draft colored tiles from shared factory displays to fill their personal player boards. The game lasts until at least one player completes a horizontal row of their wall. Final scores are tallied; highest score wins.

## 2. Components

- 100 tiles (20 each of 5 colors)
- 1 tile bag
- 1 factory center
- Factory displays (5 at 2p, 7 at 3p, 9 at 4p) — round discs, each seeded with 4 tiles
- 4 Player boards (each with a 5×5 wall, 5 pattern lines, and a floor line)
- 1 First-player marker

## 3. Round structure

**Factory phase** (repeat until all factory displays and center are empty):
Active player: pick ALL tiles of one color from either (a) one factory display, OR (b) the center pool. Remaining tiles from factory displays move to center. Place taken tiles in any one pattern line that doesn't already have a different color. Overflow goes to the floor line (−1/−2/−3 penalty points).

**Wall-tiling phase**:
For each pattern line that is completely filled (5 tiles for row 5, 1 tile for row 1, etc.): move one tile to the matching cell of the wall; discard the rest. Score immediately based on adjacency (horizontal and vertical tile chains). Empty pattern lines carry over; floor line is scored negatively, then cleared.

## 4. End condition

After the wall-tiling phase, if at least one player has completed a full horizontal row on their wall, the game ends. Otherwise, a new round begins. Final bonuses: +2 VP per complete horizontal row; +7 VP per complete vertical column; +10 VP for 5-of-same-color on wall.

## 5. Key mechanics summary

| Mechanic | Role |
|---|---|
| Factory display draft | Shared resource competition; direct interaction |
| Pattern lines | Color management; row completion sequencing |
| Wall adjacency scoring | Spatial scoring reward for strategic placement |
| Floor line | Overflow penalty; risk management |
| End-game bonuses | Vertical/horizontal/color completion incentives |
