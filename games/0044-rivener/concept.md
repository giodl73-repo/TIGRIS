# RIVENER — Concept

## Premise

RIVENER is a 2–4 player canal-network game where early placements permanently define the waterway topology. Players are 18th-century engineers competing to route canals across a hex-grid English countryside. Each canal segment placed commits the waterway permanently — there is no undo, no re-routing, no demolition. The game rewards players who lock in the best topology early while denying opponents' planned routes.

The D4 anchor is the design bet: every single placement is an irrevocable lock-in. By the game's midpoint (~60%), the waterway topology is fixed and players optimize within the constraints they've created. The Late-Game Lock-in Point is not a rule — it is the structural geometry of permanent canal placement.

## Players and length

- **Players:** 2–4
- **Length:** 75–100 min
- **Weight:** 3.0

## Anchor

**Rosenberg on D4 Late-Game Lock-in Point** — RIVENER is designed so that every action permanently constrains future options. The entire game is a sequence of lock-ins. This should earn D4 canonically as the reference design for what "commitment with consequence" means at structural scale.

**Secondary earns:** C3 (scarcity of canal segment tiles), B1 (canal topology × cargo routing × town connection = system gearing).

**Collision candidate: D4↔A3** — does the permanent blocking (D4-level lock-in) create interaction (A3) or does it merely create parallel optimization with no real engagement?
