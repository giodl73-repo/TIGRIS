# Schism — Design

## 1. Overview
4–6 players, 45–60 minutes. Area control with hidden objectives and deduction-by-action. No speech about objectives is permitted. One player is the Schismatic — they win if the group loses.

## 2. Setup
Six regions on a shared hex map. Each player draws one Objective card (private, hidden). Objectives specify a scoring formula involving which regions they control at game end. One Objective card is the Schismatic role (see §5). Each player receives 12 control tokens and 3 withdraw tokens.

## 3. Turn Structure
Each round has three phases:
1. **Declare**: All players simultaneously place face-down a region marker (which region they will act in this round).
2. **Reveal**: Flip simultaneously. Resolve conflicts (multiple players in same region: player with most tokens there wins contested majority; ties go to current token-holder).
3. **Execute**: Place 1 control token in chosen region, OR withdraw 1 token from any region (and gain 1 VP bonus for the tactical withdrawal), OR reinforce (add a second token to your already-present token; costs 2 tokens from reserve).

## 4. End-Game Scoring
After 8 rounds: each player reveals their Objective card and scores per its formula. The Schismatic scores VP equal to the number of regions where no player holds majority (fragmented regions).

## 5. Schismatic Rules
- The Schismatic's objective benefits from chaos: each region in dispute (tied token count) = 3 VP; each abandoned region = 5 VP.
- The Schismatic cannot speak about objectives (same as everyone else) but their optimal play is visibly different — they benefit from spreading conflict rather than consolidating.

## 6. Inference Engine
Since objectives are hidden, players must infer from placement patterns:
- A player who repeatedly contests Region A likely has an objective requiring Region A.
- A player who withdraws from contested regions may be the Schismatic.
- No two players have identical Objective cards (unique deals), so reading one player helps infer what others cannot have.

## 7. Teaching
One page of rules. One action per turn (chosen simultaneously). The Schismatic role is explained to all players before the game — everyone knows one Schismatic exists, but not who.
