# Schism — Concept

**Anchor:** Chvátil on A3 Interaction. In Schism, no player may speak about their objective — interaction happens entirely through placement behavior. Every region you claim or abandon is a signal; opponents must read your actions to infer your allegiance. One player holds the Schismatic objective (benefit from collective failure), which poisons inference for everyone.

**Mechanic:** 4–6 players, area control with hidden objectives. Six regions on a shared map. Each round: simultaneously choose a region to contest, reinforce, or withdraw from. End-game: objectives score based on region ownership patterns — except the Schismatic, who scores only if the group fails to hold a majority of regions.
