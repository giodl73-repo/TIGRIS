## Stakes by Persona

### Chvátil (anchor)

**A26 Social Contract Dependency** (anchor)
- *3p:* **9** — "The ceremony fails completely at 3p if one player refuses to react. The norm — react visibly and honestly — is the game's foundation. Cannot be encoded. A26 at 9."
- *5p:* **9** — "At 5p, 5 players each hold an implicit contract with 4 others simultaneously. The social contract scales with player count — more observers, more weight on each reaction. A26 at 9."

**B5 Architectural Novelty**
- *3p:* **9** — "Remove the involuntary reaction and RITE becomes a simple positional race. The reaction chain IS the architecture. No corpus sibling — not Werewolf (voluntary declaration), not Hanabi (constrained communication), not The Mind (silence). RITE leaks through bodies."
- *5p:* **9** — "At 5p the reaction chain involves 5 bodies responding to each other in real time. The architecture is maximal. B5 at 9."

**A3 Interaction**
- *3p:* **7** — "Calls target opponents directly. Reactions require physical movement toward the performer. The Officiant blocks zones. Three direct interaction mechanisms. A3 at 7."
- *5p:* **8** — "At 5p, reactions create a web of physical interactions across the room every turn. A3 at 8."

### Knizia

**C1 Tension Budget**
- *3p:* **6** — "3 Challenge tokens for a 35-minute game creates resource pressure — you feel every Call's cost. But the tension at RITE is social, not economic. The token constraint is a layer on top of the deeper social tension. C1 at 6."
- *5p:* **7** — "At 5p, 5 potential Call targets with only 3 tokens is acute scarcity. Who to spend on? When? C1 at 7."

**A1 Elegance**
- *3p:* **5** — "5 actions. Physical act vocabulary (4 types). Reaction chain. Officiant subsystem. Zone map. The rules are proportional to the depth, not understated. A1 at 5 — proportional, not elegant in the Knizia-understated sense."
- *5p:* **5** — "Same at 5p. The physical vocabulary partly replaces rule overhead (bodies communicate faster than cards) but the subsystem count is honest. A1 at 5."

**A2 Decision Density**
- *3p:* **7** — "Which zone? Which act type (true or misdirection)? Whether to Call (token cost + information value calculation)? Whether to Defer (zone-safe option)? Each turn is 3-4 live decisions. A2 at 7."
- *5p:* **8** — "At 5p, the Officiant's actions create a live variable every turn — does the Officiant enter? Where are they? The decision density increases. A2 at 8."

### Rosenberg

**D4 Late-Game Lock-in**
- *3p:* **4** — "Once at Initiate, you know your advance zone is the Altar and your act type is Offering. Your tactical options narrow — but this is not a temporal commit point in the Brass/Viticulture sense; it's a structural narrowing. D4 at 4 — retire-explicit."
- *5p:* **4** — "Same at 5p. The stage structure narrows options continuously from the start, not at a specific arc moment. Retire-explicit."

**C3 Scarcity Bite**
- *3p:* **4** — "Challenge tokens are scarce (3 total) and wrong Calls lose one permanently. But this is meta-resource scarcity, not primary-resource scarcity. The token depletion doesn't punish strategic neglect in the Agricola-feeding sense. C3 at 4 — retire-explicit."
- *5p:* **4** — "Same. Retire-explicit."

**C4 Engine-Garden Dependency**
- *3p:* **2** — "No engine. No improvements. No conditional build structure. RITE has no engine layer — its depth is social and informational, not mechanical. C4 retire-explicit at 2."
- *5p:* **2** — "Same. C4 retire-explicit."

### Feld

**A5 Downtime/Pacing**
- *3p:* **8** — "Reactions fire on every other player's turn. At 3p, each of the 2 other players' turns is a potential reaction trigger. You are never fully idle — always watching, always potentially acting. A5 at 8."
- *5p:* **9** — "At 5p, 4 other players' turns per circuit = 4 potential reaction triggers. The Officiant's Entry phase involves all zone-players in Deference. Virtually no downtime. A5 at 9."

**A29 Forgiveness Curve**
- *3p:* **3** — "Being Called correctly at Initiate regresses you to Apprentice — all that advancement undone in one moment. At 3p with fewer Calls flying, regression is rare but devastating when it occurs. A29 at 3 — punishing end of the spectrum."
- *5p:* **4** — "At 5p, more Calls circulate but also more noise (wrong Calls). Net: slightly more forgiving because the information environment is denser and confident Calls are harder to land. A29 at 4 — still punishing, slightly softened."

**B2 Catastrophe Pressure**
- *3p:* **5** — "A correct Call at Initiate is a reversal event: sudden regression from the near-win position. The catastrophic quality is real — you lose all advancement momentum. But B2's canonical anchors are sudden reversals outside player control (T&E's catastrophe tiles, Pandemic's outbreaks). A Call is a targeted choice, not an environmental reversal. B2 at 5 — contested."
- *5p:* **5** — "Same contested status at 5p. B2 fires differently here than in its canonical games."

### K-K

**A27 Physical Affordance**
- *3p:* **9** — "The reaction requires standing up, crossing to the performer, and physically responding. Digital RITE has no reactions — you cannot click 'react' and have the same game. The body is the scoring surface. A27 at 9."
- *5p:* **10** — "At 5p, the room fills with bodies reacting to bodies. The physical density IS the game at this count. A27 at 10."

**C7 Action-Menu Clarity**
- *3p:* **7** — "5 actions with distinct functions. Move/Perform/Call/Defer/Convene. The act-type vocabulary (Receiving/Witnessing/Offering/Completing) self-describes through gesture — you don't need an icon to know what cupped hands means. C7 at 7."
- *5p:* **7** — "Same at 5p. The Convene action (Officiant only) adds one additional item for the Officiant player, but is announced verbally. C7 at 7."

**D2 Spatial-Interaction Presence**
- *3p:* **7** — "Reactions require physical movement between zones. The Officiant enters zones and triggers Deference. Players physically block paths between zones. Spatial coupling is direct and continuous. D2 at 7."
- *5p:* **8** — "At 5p, the room is spatially dense — bodies in zones create live adjacency effects. D2 at 8."

### Stegmaier

**A25 Emotional Arc**
- *3p:* **8** — "RITE has a designed felt narrative: you begin ignorant (Novice, no reactions), advance through increasing visibility (Apprentice reacts to Novice acts, Initiate to Apprentice acts), and approach the climax as Elder (most exposed, most dangerous, closest to winning). Players recount the ceremony after: 'I was Called at Initiate. I had to go all the way back.' A25 at 8."
- *5p:* **9** — "At 5p, the emotional arc is shared by 5 players simultaneously. The ceremony's shape — slow opening, accelerating middle, Elder climax — is felt by the room. A25 at 9."

**A31 Failure Texture**
- *3p:* **7** — "Being Called correctly is a retellable story. 'I was at Initiate. I reacted to Rosenberg's Witnessing act — couldn't help it. Knizia Called me. I had to go back to Apprentice. The room knew.' The failure has texture. A31 at 7."
- *5p:* **8** — "At 5p, more Calls = more failure moments = richer failure narrative. The Officiant losing their role is particularly dramatic. A31 at 8."

**A6 Teachability**
- *3p:* **5** — "Physical vocabulary requires demonstration (you must show what a Receiving act looks like before play begins). Social contract requires buy-in ('you must react honestly — this is the game'). Estimated teach: 20-25 minutes. A6 at 5 — contested. The concept teaches fast; the physical vocabulary teaches slowly."
- *5p:* **5** — "Same at 5p. The Open Stage variant (§9) serves as an introductory mode."

### Vaccarino

**A7 Emergence/Replayability**
- *3p:* **8** — "5 of 12 zone cards drawn per game. Officiant rotation varies. Challenge token spending patterns diverge. Stage progression sequences differ by group. At 3p, the information environment changes significantly with zone configuration. A7 at 8."
- *5p:* **9** — "At 5p, 5 progression paths + 5 Officiant rotation patterns + 5 zone configurations = high variance. A7 at 9."

**A30 Strategy Ergodicity**
- *3p:* **4** — "The dominant strategy is: advance as fast as possible while keeping your reactions ambiguous. The Officiant mechanic creates a counter-strategy (stay at Apprentice, accumulate tokens, Call leaders down) but it's clearly suboptimal — the win condition is reaching Elder. A30 at 4 — limited ergodicity. Retire-explicit."
- *5p:* **5** — "At 5p, 'stall and Call' becomes slightly more viable because multiple players are racing. A30 at 5 — still limited, still retire-explicit."

**A4 Variance Calibration**
- *3p:* **4** — "Zone draw is random (5 of 12) and not calibrated to the game state. Players cannot manage the variance distribution the way Dominion lets you manage your deck composition. A4 at 4 — retire-explicit. The zone variance is welcome but not designed variance in the calibrated sense."
- *5p:* **4** — "Same. A4 retire-explicit."

### Lacerda

**A28 Cognitive Load Profile**
- *3p:* **7** — "Five simultaneous deductive registers: own stage (what can I perform?), others' stages (what should they react to?), Officiant position (will they enter?), Challenge token count (can I Call?), zone states (is my advance zone occupied?). Deductive-compound. A28 at 7."
- *5p:* **8** — "At 5p, the register for 'others' stages' splits into 4 separate deductive tracks. A28 at 8."

**A32 Information Architecture Type**
- *3p:* **7** — "RITE's information structure: you know your own stage; others must infer it from your acts and reactions. The reactions are INVOLUNTARY — you cannot suppress them. This is above private hands (A32=3, standard concealment) and below inverted visibility (A32=10, Hanabi). RITE = 7: hidden state with structural involuntary leakage. The leakage mechanism makes it distinct from standard hidden-role games. Second corpus earn candidate."
- *5p:* **8** — "At 5p, 5 players each leaking information about their stage simultaneously creates an information lattice — each reaction is also a datum about the reactor. A32 at 8."

**B4 Information Transparency Cost**
- *3p:* **4** — "Calls cost Challenge tokens and could be read as 'purchasing' information (if correct, you learn the target's stage). But Calls are asymmetric bets, not information purchases — a wrong Call costs you a token without giving you the information. B4's canonical sense requires the information-cost to be predictable and transactional. RITE's information is involuntarily leaked, not purchased. B4 at 4 — retire-explicit."
- *5p:* **4** — "Same. B4 retire-explicit."
