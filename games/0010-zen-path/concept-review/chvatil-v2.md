# Chvátil on ZEN PATH v1.1 — validation pass

## Did the revisions land?

Partially. The designer heard me but didn't go where I pointed. I asked for a Tzolk'in — a path that MOVES, a mechanic whose removal wrecks the interior. Instead they added four small comfortable things: a Y-fork (Tikal), aspect-partitioned Steps (color-coded movement), a private hand (Dominion), and a Moon Phase (Agricola-feeding-lite). Every one of those is a 1998-napkin addition. None of them individually is load-bearing. The designer is betting that FOUR comfortable things COMPOUND into one weird thing. That is a real bet, and I respect it enough to score it honestly — but it's the bet of a committee, not a designer. Tzolk'in didn't add four systems; it added ONE gear and derived everything else.

That said: the compound is genuinely tighter than v1.0. Aspect-partitioned Steps means you can't race without committing — that closes the "Samurai-with-movement" remainder I flagged in C2. Branching forks with asymmetric aspect-rewards means the spatial geography now interacts with aspect investment, which v1.0 didn't. Moon Phase creates periodic forced-sacrifice that at least gestures at catastrophe. Private hand + market is a real info-asymmetry layer.

**Test it by my own diagnostic 3:** if you remove the branching forks, do you still have a game? Yes — aspect-partitioned track-race with Moon Phase. If you remove Moon Phase? Yes — branching race with private hand. If you remove the private hand? Yes — branching race with Moon Phase. Each piece remains removable. No single load-bearing column. This is still combinatorial, but it's combinatorial with more interlock than v1.0.

## New B5 score

**B5 — 5/10 at 3p, 5/10 at 4p.** Up from 3. Crosses refute-direction by one point. Here's why it earns 5 and not 6: the asymmetric fork-aspect alignment (Mountain rewards Honor/Strength, River rewards Strategy) creates a real *branching-commitment decision* that couples the spatial and aspect systems — that's a genuine structural move and it does interact with the min-score endgame in a way Samurai doesn't. Here's why it's not 6+: no single removal kills the game. The weirdness is distributed, not concentrated. A Chvátil architecture has a spine; this has ribs.

## Remaining concerns

**C1'. The Moon Phase is Rosenberg cosplay.** A periodic "pick your poison" is not catastrophe pressure — it's a tax. Catastrophe in Galaxy Trucker means your ship explodes mid-flight. Moon Phase as specced is a scheduled tariff. Keep it, but don't claim B2.

**C2'. Four-subsystem compound risks the teach penalty without delivering a play-5 revelation.** Dungeon Petz is teach-heavy and pays back at play 5. ZEN PATH v1.1 is teach-medium-heavy and the designer hasn't shown me what play 5 reveals that play 1 hid. Where's the "oh" moment?

## Greenlight?

**Conditional greenlight to design.md.** Score crosses the refute line. The architecture is no longer Thebes-skin — it's a new stack, just not a novel spine. Proceed, but in design.md the designer owes me ONE thing: identify which subsystem you'd die for, and make the other three serve it. Right now all four are peers. Pick a protagonist.
