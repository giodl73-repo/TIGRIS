# Vaccarino on ZEN PATH

*"No dice. Public market. Every tile does one thing. I'm listening."*

## The no-variance commitment

Knizia anchored this on C2. Fine. But the design decision I want to underline is the one the concept treats as incidental: **no dice, no hidden hand, everything public**. That's my axis (A4 Variance Calibration) sitting center-stage — and the designer has chosen to zero it out rather than integrate it.

Ask: is that load-bearing or cowardice?

Load-bearing, I think — *if* the tile-draft market pulls its weight. Chutes & Ladders without dice isn't Chutes & Ladders; it's a puzzle. The thing that rescues ZEN PATH from puzzle-collapse is the 5-slot public market: minor refill variance in tile order, combined with 3-4 players competing for the same slot. That's where the live randomness lives. It's the Kingdom Builder move — fixed kingdoms each game, randomized board tiles. Setup-variance instead of turn-variance.

**A4 Variance Calibration, 3p: 4.5 / 4p: 5.0.** Justification: market refill is the only random element. At 3p the draft cycle is long enough that most tiles become visible before commitment; at 4p the slot competition tightens and refill order matters more. The refill is uncalibrated — I don't see the *mitigation tool* the axis demands. In Dominion, trashing and deck-sculpting let you manage shuffle variance. Here, Meditate (2 tiles in 55) is the only cycle tool. Thin. Either Meditate comes up to 4 tiles, or a second cycle mechanic joins it. Otherwise variance is noise, not a resource.

## B3 Conversion Chain Depth — I'm the advocate, I'll defend it

The concept predicts B3 earning and I agree, narrowly. The chain:

**tile → aspect-boost → aspect-threshold → ladder-trigger → position × MIN-aspect at scoring.**

That's 4-5 transformations from acquisition to VP. Dominion: Copper→Silver→Gold→Province is 4 steps and adopted B3 last cycle (Puerto Rico + TtA). ZEN PATH's chain is tighter than PR's and closer to TtA's culture→science→wonder shape.

**B3, 3p: 7.5 / 4p: 8.0.** At 4p the multi-player ladder placement adds a conversion layer (your placed ladder becomes my trigger), pushing chain depth up. At 3p the board is less densely placed and some chains stay dormant.

The B3 risk: **Step tiles are a shortcut around the chain.** 18 Step tiles (a third of the pool) move you directly without touching aspects. A player who drafts Steps and ignores Ascend/Focus skips the conversion chain entirely — their final score collapses via MIN to near-zero, but they've used a third of the tile pool as low-conversion filler. That's a chain-depth leak. The count wants examination: 12-14 Steps may be the right number, not 18.

## A1 Elegance — queued for adoption, this could be the second earning

The rule count is genuinely low. One action per turn. One scoring equation. Three aspects. Card effects printed on tiles. The kind of architecture where I can read 55 tiles and know the game in 5 minutes — that's my Dominion benchmark.

**A1, 3p: 7.5 / 4p: 7.5.** Rule-count-to-depth ratio is tight. The scoring equation alone (position × MIN) produces the strategy space described in the concept without additional rules scaffolding. The Knizia-Vaccarino overlap.

But: the ~7 tile types are not all card-simple yet.
- Step, Ascend, Focus: card-only, unambiguous. Good.
- Ladder, Chute: require *choosing* an aspect-condition from 3 options at placement time. That's fine, but the placement rule ("any unoccupied path square") will need clarification — can I stack two ladders adjacent? Does a ladder aspect-condition check apply on the landing square only, or all intermediate squares? Card text must resolve these.
- Duel: "force an aspect comparison with an adjacent-path samurai" — which aspect? Chosen by attacker or defender? This is the Militia-vs-Cutpurse problem. Resolve on the card, not in the rulebook.
- Meditate: fine, but underused at 2/55.

Fix Duel's aspect-selection on the card face and A1 earns. Leave it to the rulebook and A1 misses.

## Bottom line

The no-variance bet is defensible but requires market-refill calibration I don't yet see. B3 I'll defend. A1 is within reach if Duel's card text gets tight. Ask Knizia whether the MIN-aspect scoring makes Step tiles structurally redundant.
