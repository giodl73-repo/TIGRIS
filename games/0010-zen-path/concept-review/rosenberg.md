# Rosenberg — ZEN PATH concept review

I spent an evening tracing the pipeline before passing judgment. The scoring equation is honest Knizia; my job is to ask whether the pipeline *upstream* of that equation has the teeth my axes require. Four stakes, four verdicts.

## C3 Scarcity Bite — **5.5 / 10 (3p), 4.0 / 10 (4p)**

The 5-slot market is the only scarcity surface, and I don't trust it. Draft one tile per turn, refill from a 55-tile bag — at 3p across a 40-square race that probably resolves in 25-30 turns per seat, roughly 75-90 tiles cycle. That is most of the bag. Every player gets something close to the tile they want, most turns. Where is the Agricola feeding phase here? Where is the obligation that converts abundance to shortage? There isn't one. The Peak is a ceiling, not an upkeep. A samurai never goes hungry.

Contrast *A Feast for Odin*: the action board has fewer rows than a player's pips across a round, and the feeding arrives whether your larder is stocked. ZEN PATH's analogue would be an *aspect decay* phase, or a draft-phase where opponents can burn a tile you need. The market can be snipped (draft-out) but the bag replenishes it immediately. No bite.

**Greenlight condition:** reduce market to 3 slots, OR add an aspect-erosion trigger when another samurai crosses your path, OR make Focus tiles contested (only 6 of them, but the 18 Step tiles are a safety valve that prevents contest). Until then, C3 does not earn.

## C4 Engine-Garden Dependency — **3.0 / 10**

This is where the concept disappoints me most. An engine-garden means the value of a tile placed in turn 10 depends on 3+ prior placements. ZEN PATH's tiles are *context-free effects*: Step moves 1-3, Ascend adds 1, Focus adds 2. Playing a Focus on Honor in turn 12 has identical payoff whether your prior turns built Strategy or Strength. Nothing gears.

The placeable ladder/chute tiles gesture at gearing — you can seed an aspect-gated ladder that only *you* can trigger — but a one-tile setup followed by a one-tile payoff is not an engine-garden, it's a two-step combo. Compare *Le Havre*: the Shipping Line's value depends on which ships you built, which depends on the Iron Smelter you chose round 3, which depends on the Clay Mound you built round 1. Four-deep conditional value. ZEN PATH as written is tile-by-tile tactics with no accumulating manor.

**Fix:** introduce aspect-*synergy* tiles — "if your Honor ≥ 5 AND you own a Scroll artifact, +2 aspects on all future draft." A durable object layer beneath the tile-draft layer. Without it, C4 does not earn. At 3p the concept plays as a tactical race, which is fine for what it is, but it is not the tradition my axes were built for.

## D4 Late-Game Lock-in Point — **7.5 / 10**

Here the concept does something I respect. Once a samurai crosses roughly square 28 (⅔ of the 40-square path), the position-multiplier pressure flips: you can no longer afford to stop and pump a lagging aspect, because the opportunity cost of standing still is visible on the leaderboard. Your minimum aspect at that moment *is* your ceiling. That is a real lock-in, narratively justified (the Peak is close; turning back is absurd), with optimization remaining in the last third (which pre-printed ladder to chase, which duel to force).

I'd prefer the lock-in be slightly sharper — a rule like "within 10 squares of the Peak, Ascend tiles cost an extra aspect-point" would make the commit visible and costly — but the geometry is already doing most of the work. This is the strongest axis in the concept.

## B3 Conversion Chain Depth (adopted) — **6.0 / 10**

The chain is tile → aspect → gate-trigger → position-gain → position × min-aspect. Four transformations, which on paper satisfies the ≥ 3 anchor. But two of the four are trivial: "tile → aspect" is a direct +1, and "position × min" is multiplication, not transformation. The meaningful conversion is aspect → ladder-trigger → position, and that's a single path-feature check.

*Le Havre* compounds: Wheat → Bread → Fish → Smoked Fish → Processing → Shipping → Francs. Each node is a decision with a tempo cost. ZEN PATH's chain is shallow per step. Adopted axis re-earns, but on the low end of earning.

## Verdict

Greenlight to DESIGN with two mandates: (1) find the feeding-equivalent, or C3 does not bite; (2) add durable object-layer accumulation, or C4 refutes. D4 and B3 earn as drafted. The Knizia spine is sound; the Rosenberg ribs are missing.
