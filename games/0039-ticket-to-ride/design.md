# Ticket to Ride — Design (Rules Import)

## Core loop

Each turn, a player does exactly one of: (a) draw 2 train cards from face-up row or deck, (b) claim a route by spending matching colored cards = route length, (c) draw 3 Destination Tickets (keep ≥1). That's the complete turn.

## Components

- 240 colored train car cards (9 colors + wild Locomotives)
- 110 train pieces per player (45 each for 2p; 45 standard)
- 30 Destination Tickets (point goals from city A to city B)
- Board: 44 routes across 36 cities in North America

## Routes and scoring

Route lengths: 1–6 cars. Scoring: 1→1, 2→2, 3→4, 4→7, 5→10, 6→15. Gray routes require any single color; colored routes require matching color. Double routes (for 4-5p) allow one route per player.

## Destination Tickets

Private cards worth +2 to +22 points if the continuous path connects the two cities, or the negative of that value if not connected by game end. Players must keep ≥1 of 3 drawn.

## Game end

Triggered when any player has ≤2 trains remaining. All players complete the round; then final scoring: routes + tickets (+ / –) + Longest Continuous Railway bonus (+10).

## Weight profile

Weight 1.86. Gateway game. Teach time under 10 minutes. First turn complexity: draw cards or claim your first route.

## Key tension

Train shortage: 45 trains. A 6-car route uses 13% of your entire supply in one move. Completing 2 long tickets requires ~25 trains. You rarely complete all tickets drawn.

## Interaction surface

Route blocking: opponent claiming a route forces a longer detour or ticket failure. In 4-5p blocking is constant; in 2-3p it's strategic and selective.
