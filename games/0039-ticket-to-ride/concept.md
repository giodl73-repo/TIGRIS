# Ticket to Ride — Concept

## Premise

Ticket to Ride is a 2–5 player route-building game where players collect colored train cards and spend them to claim railway routes across North America. Destination Ticket cards create private scoring objectives — fulfilling them earns points; failing to complete them costs points. The tension core: you never have enough trains, enough cards, or enough turns.

Knizia anchors on C1 Tension Budget — the perpetual shortage of trains (45 pieces, limited supply) and time (card draw vs. route claim vs. ticket draw trade-off each turn) creates the canonical Knizian resource-short decision state throughout the game.

## Players and length

- **Players:** 2–5
- **Length:** 45–75 min
- **Weight:** 1.86

## Anchor

**Knizia on C1 Tension Budget** — every turn costs you something you can't afford to lose: drawing cards builds toward a route but delays claiming; claiming routes uses trains you can never get back; drawing new Destination Tickets risks penalty while seeking upside. The shortage is always live.

**Collision candidate: C1↔A3** — does the route-blocking interaction (A3) make C1 more tense or is it orthogonal (tension from own shortage vs. tension from opponent blocking)?
