# Dominion — Rules Summary (Review Import)

Condensed summary for review purposes. Canonical rules in the Rio Grande rulebook; this document grounds the TIGRIS panel's stakes.

## 1. Overview

Players lead rival monarchs building their dominion. Each player builds their own deck of cards from a shared kingdom of purchasable options. The deck-building loop is: play cards → buy cards → discard → reshuffle. Over ~20 turns, decks evolve from 10-card starters into personalized engines. The game ends when key card piles empty. Winner has most Victory Points in their deck.

**Object:** collect the most Victory Points in your deck when the game ends.

## 2. Components

- **25 Kingdom card types** in the base set (10 of each for 2-4 players). Each has a unique effect.
- Treasure cards (Copper, Silver, Gold — provide buying power)
- Victory cards (Estate, Duchy, Province — provide VP at endgame; useless during play)
- Curse cards (−1 VP, clog deck)
- Each game uses only **10 Kingdom card types** drawn from the base set (or any expansion).
- 500+ cards total across the base game.

## 3. Setup

1. Choose 10 Kingdom card types for this game (random or from recommended sets).
2. Lay out piles: 10 Kingdom, plus Treasures (Copper, Silver, Gold), Victory (Estate, Duchy, Province), Curses.
3. Each player starts with a 10-card deck: **7 Copper + 3 Estate**. Shuffle.
4. Each player draws 5 cards from their deck (initial hand).
5. Determine first player.

## 4. Turn structure

Each turn has three phases:

### 4.1 Action phase

Play Action cards from hand (one at a time, resolving effects). Start with 1 Action allowed. Action cards may grant `+Actions`, `+Cards` (draw), `+Coins` (buying power), `+Buys`, or trigger other players.

End Action phase when: no more Actions left, or you choose to stop.

### 4.2 Buy phase

Play Treasures from hand (they provide Coins). You may buy 1 card from the supply (default), paying its cost. Bought card goes to discard pile.

Action cards may have granted `+Buys`, allowing additional purchases.

### 4.3 Clean-up phase

Discard entire hand (plus played cards). Draw 5 new cards from your deck. If deck is empty, shuffle discard into deck and continue drawing.

Next player's turn.

## 5. Kingdom cards (sample — 10 of the 25 base types)

The 10-card kingdom varies each game. Millions of combinations.

## 6. Scoring / end condition

### End condition

Game ends when either:
- 3 Kingdom card piles are empty, OR
- The Province pile is empty.

Complete the current round; then score.

### Scoring

For each player, count VP from cards in deck:
- Estate: +1
- Duchy: +3
- Province: +6
- Colony (expansion): +10
- Curse: −1
- Victory cards from expansions: various

Most VP wins. Ties broken by fewer turns taken. Then shared victory.

## 7. Attacks and defenses

Some Action cards (Witch, Bureaucrat, Militia) are Attacks — they affect opposing players directly (give them Curses, dump cards, etc.). Moat and other Reaction cards defend.

Attacks are direct interaction but limited to a subset of cards.

## 8. Special subsystems

- **Deck shuffling** — as your discard grows, reshuffle timing matters strategically.
- **Trashing** — removing cards from your deck permanently (Chapel, Remodel, etc.). Central to engine-building (remove starting Estates).
- **Gaining vs buying** — some cards are "gained" (not bought, free); affects strategy.

## 9. Variants / expansions

Dominion has 10+ expansions adding new Kingdom cards and mechanics. This review covers the base game only.

## 10. Designer notes (secondary — summary of Vaccarino's commentary)

Vaccarino's stated goal was "a game where your deck IS your strategy." The deck-building architecture emerged from rejecting traditional card games where hands are drawn from a shared pool. Here, each player's deck is theirs — earned through purchases and modifications during play.

The genre-founding move is the **purchasable-card mechanism paired with cyclical hand-draw**. Before Dominion, card games had either:
- Drafted cards (7 Wonders: permanent after drafting)
- Dealt cards (Magic: drawn from your deck, but deck is built BEFORE play)
- Shared pool (Bohnanza, etc.)

Dominion introduced: deck-built-DURING-play, re-shuffled, purchased from a shared market. This architecture gave rise to an entire genre (Ascension, Star Realms, Legendary, Harry Potter: Hogwarts Battle, Clank!, etc.). That lineage is evidence for Architectural Novelty.

## 12. Weight & target audience

- Weight ~2.3/5 on BGG.
- Teach time: 10–15 min (very fast for the depth).
- Play time: 30 min (core), 45 min (with expansions).
- Audience: broad — family-to-expert. Tournament scene active 15+ years post-release.

## 13. What this game is NOT

- Not theme-forward (medieval/monarchy theme is decorative; mechanics are the core).
- Not spatial (no board; just card piles).
- Not cooperative (zero-sum; attacks exist).
- Not deterministic (deck-shuffle variance is significant).
- Not static (each game's 10-card kingdom is different).
