# Ra — Design (Review Mode: Rules Summary)

## Published rules summary

**Players:** 2–5. **Length:** 45–90 min. **Weight:** BGG 2.7.

### Setup
- Bag of 180 tiles: Civilization tiles, God tiles, Pharaoh tiles, Nile/Flood tiles, Monument tiles, Ra tiles, Disaster tiles.
- 3 epoch tracks (10 Ra-tile slots each).
- Sun tokens distributed to players based on player count (rotating starting sun in center).

### Turn structure
Each turn the active player does ONE of:
1. **Draw a tile** — pull 1 tile from bag. If it's a Ra tile, advance the epoch track; if the epoch track fills, epoch ends immediately. If it's any other tile, place it face-up in the auction lot.
2. **Call Ra** — voluntarily trigger an auction.
3. **Use God tile** — spend a God tile to take any one tile from the auction lot (no bidding required).

### Auction
When Ra is called (voluntarily or via Ra tile), all players may bid 1 sun token. The highest bid wins the entire auction lot. The winning bidder swaps their bid sun (now placed face-up on their player board for VP) with the center sun (now in the winner's hand for future bidding). Players who pass get nothing.

### Epoch end
When an epoch track fills (10 Ra tiles), the epoch ends. All unredeemed auction lots are discarded. Scoring fires for certain tile types (Pharaohs score each epoch; Civilizations score at end of epochs 1 and 2; Monuments score only at game end).

### End of game (3 epochs)
- Monuments score (most of a type = bonus; diversity bonus).
- Each held sun token scores its face value as positive VP.
- Civilization sets scored.
- Player with most Pharaoh tiles each epoch scored.

### Key tension (C1 mechanism)
Sun tokens are simultaneously bidding power AND end-game VP. Holding high-value suns = stronger bids but they cost you end-game points if unspent. Every bid is a choice: spend the power (win the lot, give up VP) or hold the power (lose the lot, keep VP). The tension is structural and perpetual across all 3 epochs.
