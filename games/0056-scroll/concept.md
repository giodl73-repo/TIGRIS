# SCROLL — Concept

## Premise

SCROLL is a 1–4 player simultaneous roll-and-write where players fill a personal manuscript scroll (4 rows × 8 columns = 32 cells) using symbols from a shared die pool. Each symbol has a cascading effect based on placement row: a symbol in Row 1 modifies Row 4 outcomes via the cascade rule. Die pool construction is the core skill: adding a symbol die shifts the probability curve for a specific scroll section. The cascade chain (Row 1 → Row 2 → Row 3 → Row 4) is the game's conversion spine.

## Anchor mechanic

**A4 Variance Calibration.** The shared die pool is constructed from a bank of 12 symbol dice (4 types: Quill, Seal, Ink, Reed). Each round, the active player may optionally add 1 die to the pool before rolling. Adding a Quill die increases the probability of Quill-symbol results; adding a Seal die shifts the distribution toward Seal. Players who master die-pool construction get better cascade triggers. A4 earns because variance IS the strategic tool: knowing the distribution lets you predict cascade outcomes and plan placements.

## B3 secondary

The cascade chain Row 1 → Row 4 creates a depth-4 conversion pipeline: Die Symbol → Row 1 Placement → Cascade Modifier → Row 4 Output → VP. This is the B3 earn mechanism alongside A4.

## C8 targeted earn (secondary)

By the 4th and 5th rounds of a scroll (later in the game), the cascade interactions from early rows are visible and create a growing planning puzzle. First placements commit early rows and constrain the remaining cells. This is not first-TURN compression exactly — but Round 1's choices in each game session are more consequential for an experienced player (who knows how cascades interact). The C8 claim: experienced players make non-guided strategic Roll 1 decisions.

## Collision candidate

**A4↔B3 OP** — variance calibration (A4: managing die-pool composition to favor favorable distributions) vs. conversion chain depth (B3: the cascade pipeline structure that the dice outcomes feed into). A4 = the probabilistic management layer; B3 = the deterministic cascade architecture that A4 feeds into.
