# QUORUM — Concept

## Premise

QUORUM is a 2–5 player competitive-cooperative voting game where the tension budget IS the scoring mechanism. Each round, players simultaneously commit a fixed budget of influence tokens across 5 open Proposals. The round resolves: proposals that reach a Quorum threshold (majority of total tokens cast) pass — all players who backed them score. Proposals that fail — those tokens are lost. But committing too conservatively risks others controlling which proposals pass and locking you out of scoring.

The C1 tension is structural: you never have enough tokens to back every proposal you want. You must predict which proposals will reach quorum, commit sufficient tokens to earn, but not so many that you starve other scoring paths.

## Players and length

- **Players:** 2–5
- **Length:** 45–60 min
- **Weight:** 2.5

## Anchor

**Knizia on C1 Tension Budget** — the token budget is always too small. 5 Proposals, 8 tokens (for 3-player), you need at least 2 tokens to meaningfully back a proposal. You can cover at most 4 proposals thinly. The scoring mechanism forces you to choose.

**Designed to earn C1 canonically** as the reference design for Tension Budget in a voting context. The scarcity of influence tokens is not incidental — it is the entire game.

**Collision candidate: C1↔A3** — is the quorum mechanism tension (C1) orthogonal to the player-vs-player voting dynamics (A3)?

## Non-goals

- Not a social-deduction game (no hidden roles or allegiances).
- Not a negotiation game (tokens committed simultaneously; no trading round).
- Not majority-voting as in traditional auction games (Quorum threshold varies per proposal).
