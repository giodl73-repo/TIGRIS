# Tikal — Concept (imported for review)

## Premise

Review of *Tikal* (Wolfgang Kramer & Michael Kiesling, 1999). Spiel des Jahres 1999 winner; first entry in the **Mask Trilogy** (Tikal, Java, Mexica); the canonical statement of K-K's **action-point allowance + spatial-play** tradition.

- **Players:** 2-4 (3-4 best)
- **Length:** 90 min
- **Weight:** ~3.0/5
- **Mechanics:** hex-tile draw + placement (jungle expansion); action-point allowance (10 AP per turn, menu of 8 actions with variable costs); pyramid excavation (stacked levels 1-10); treasure collection; scoring triggers at volcano-tile draws
- **Scoring:** Team scoring at pre-declared scoring rounds (triggered by volcano tiles); players count temple levels controlled + treasures held + pyramids held
- **BGG rank:** strong top-200 all-time; top-50 mid-2000s

## Artifact-as-story

The **hex-tile jungle board with stackable pyramids + camp markers** is the centerpiece. Each round: draw a tile (jungle/temple/treasure/volcano), then spend 10 AP from the menu. Volcano tiles trigger scoring rounds where players tally controlled temples (level × 1pt), treasure sets (1/2/3 matched pairs), and pyramids (level × 1pt).
