## Round 1 — Voice assignment + D3 earns

**D3 (Stegmaier anchor) — Defend**: "4-player game: P1 and P2 each manage 2 Voices (P1 = Soprano + Alto; P2 = Tenor + Bass). Identical rules as a 2-player game (where each player would manage 3 Voices) or a 6-player game (1 Voice each). The Note cards, action menu, and Harmony scoring are unchanged. The game's strategic character differs at each count (4p = 2-Voice coordination; 6p = group coordination) but the rules are identical. D3 earns: engineered count-robustness. +1 defend."

**A5 (Vaccarino) — Defend**: "Round 1: All 4 players simultaneously select actions for both their Voices. P1 chooses: Soprano=Compose, Alto=Conduct. P2 chooses: Tenor=Sustain, Bass=Compose. All resolved simultaneously. Zero downtime — no player waits for another to complete their turn. At 6p this would be 6 simultaneous single-Voice decisions; still zero downtime. A5 earns. +1 defend."

**A3 (Chvátil) — Defend**: "Round 2: P1's Soprano Voice wants to Conduct a Soprano note, but P2's Tenor Voice is Conducting a Tenor note that creates Dissonance if P1 proceeds. P1 must communicate with P2 before acting. Cooperative mandatory interaction: cannot play solitaire because your Voice actions affect the shared Harmony board. A3 earns. +1 defend."

### D3↔A5 COLLISION — Round 3 (OP, temporal-register)

**Stegmaier (D3 anchor) proposes OP**: "D3 fires on the design achievement — identical rules at 2–6 players with equivalent strategic depth at every count. D3 is the design goal. A5 fires on the pacing mechanism — simultaneous action selection means zero downtime regardless of player count. A5 is the mechanism that makes D3 achievable (simultaneous actions mean turn length doesn't scale with player count). D3 = what was achieved; A5 = one mechanism that helps achieve it. Different analytical registers: D3 = design achievement; A5 = pacing mechanism."

**Vaccarino (A5 stake) seconds OP**: "A5 would earn in a CHORUS without D3 (if CHORUS had different rules at different counts but still used simultaneous actions). D3 would earn without A5 (if CHORUS used sequential turns but had identical rules at all counts via other mechanisms). They decouple — A5 and D3 address different design questions."

**Panel votes OP (5-3):**
- Knizia → OP: "A1 elegance, D3 count-robustness, A5 pacing — three distinct design axes."
- Rosenberg → OP: "C1 tension budget and D3 count architecture are orthogonal; A5 pacing is yet another layer."
- Lacerda → OP: "A2 decision density, A5 pacing, D3 scaling — three different questions about the same simultaneous-action game."
- Chvátil → CR (D3 wins): "A5 is the mechanism D3 uses. The simultaneous action IS why the game scales. CR: D3."
- K-K → CR (D3 wins): "C7 action clarity and D3 robustness are distinct; but A5 and D3 are entangled — you can't have D3 without A5 in this design."
- Feld → CR (D3 wins): "A5 simultaneous action IS the D3 mechanism. They're the same design decision from two angles."

**Result: 5-3 OP** (Stegmaier + Vaccarino + Knizia + Rosenberg + Lacerda = 5 OP; Chvátil + K-K + Feld = 3 CR). D3 and A5 both earn. **32nd temporal-register OP.**
