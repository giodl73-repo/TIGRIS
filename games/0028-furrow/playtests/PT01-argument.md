## Season 1 — Trick-Taking Foundation

**Trick 3**: P1 leads Grain 8. P2 holds Grain 3, Barley 12, Hops 2. P2 MUST play Grain 3 (follows suit) despite Barley 12 being their best card for the next intended trick. P2 plays Grain 3. P1 wins with Grain 8; takes 1 Grain token.

**A3 (Chvátil) — Defend**: "Trick 3: P2's Barley 12 was the card they wanted to play — it would lead Barley next trick and win them a Barley token. But P2 MUST follow Grain (they hold Grain 3). P2's optimal plan was directly constrained by P1's lead choice. This is mandatory collective interaction: the follow-suit obligation ensures that every player's card choice is constrained by every other player's lead. No player can act as if others aren't at the table. Counter-pressure cycle 3 game #2. +1 defend on A3."

**B5 (Chvátil anchor) — Defend**: "Trick 6: P3 leads Flax 9. The table knows: winning this trick scores 2 VP directly (Flax = direct VP rule). P3 chose to lead Flax specifically to force this economic contest — they're controlling the harvest type, not just the score. This is trick-as-action-selection: P3's lead card chose WHAT was at stake, not just who would score. Novel mechanism in play. +1 defend on B5."

**C1 (Knizia) — Defend**: "Trick 9 (end of season 1 approaching): every remaining Grain-led trick means a Grain token in someone's hand for planting. P2 (who was forced to waste Grain 3 earlier) needs a Grain token to plant; P3 doesn't. The seasonal tension is live: there are 3 tricks left and only 2 Grain tokens in supply. The tension clock is real. +1 defend on C1."

**A2 (Feld) — Defend**: "P4's turn at Trick 8: P4 holds Grain 5, Barley 7, Hops 3, Flax 2. P1 led Barley 4.
