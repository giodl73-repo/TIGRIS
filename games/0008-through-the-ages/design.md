# Through the Ages — Rules Summary (Review Import)

Condensed summary for panel review. Canonical rules in CGE 2015 rulebook.

## 1. Overview

2-4 players each build a civilization from Age A (antiquity) through Age III (modern). Over ~20 rounds, players spend Civil Actions (CA) and Military Actions (MA) to draft cards, play wonders, develop technology, build military, manage food/happiness/culture. Events arrive each round via a separate deck. Wars can be declared; aggression cards punish weaker players. Game ends after Age III; highest Culture VP wins.

**Object:** accumulate most Culture VP across 4 ages.

## 2. Components

- Civilization mats (1 per player): workers, production, food/happiness/science/military/culture tracks
- Card deck: ~300 cards split by age (A, I, II, III)
- Military/Civil Action tokens per player
- Card market (rotating display)
- Event deck (separate track)
- Wonder tokens
- Age marker
- Culture VP tracker

## 3. Setup

Each player starts with identical Age A civilization: 2 workers, 1 farm, 1 mine, 1 warriors, 1 leader. Starting CA = 4, MA = 2 (grows with Age).

Age A card market populated. First player chosen. Event deck shuffled.

## 4. Turn structure

Each round = each player takes 1 turn in order. Round ends with event resolution.

### Per-turn actions:

Spend Civil Actions (CA) on one of:
- **Draft a card** from market (cost varies by column position)
- **Play a card** (build, tech, wonder, government change)
- **Develop population** (+1 worker; costs food)
- **Adjust production/military**

Spend Military Actions (MA) on:
- Draft/play aggression/war cards
- Increase military units
- Declare events

After all players' turns: resolve top Event card (applies to all or target-chosen player).

## 5. Key subsystems

### 5.1 Cards and ages

Each age has its own deck. Cards: wonders, technology, leaders, government types, military units, civilian buildings, production buildings, special bonuses.

Drafting cost: column 1-5 free, column 6-11 cost 1 CA each additional column.

### 5.2 Wonders

Each wonder: multi-step build, ongoing benefit. Wonder VP = printed; often +culture per turn or endgame bonus.

### 5.3 Technologies (tech ladder)

Tech cards improve production efficiency. Play a tech → it replaces a predecessor. Civilization advances through eras as technology unlocks.

### 5.4 Military + Wars

Military strength = sum of units + tech bonuses. Wars: target weaker player; steal resources/cards/culture. Aggression: minor punishment, cheaper.

Military is a balance requirement: falling behind opens you to being targeted.

### 5.5 Events + Catastrophes

Event deck drives random events: plague, famine, natural disasters, political change. Some events are CATASTROPHES — events that deliberately punish leaders or remove units/resources.

### 5.6 Culture VP (scoring)

Culture accumulates each turn based on civilization state:
- Wonders: printed VP
- Technology: reflects in science + culture
- Leaders: culture bonus
- Military Leader: military × culture multiplier (the classic "general" who multiplies)
- Events/wars end-of-Age: Culture delta from various events

## 6. Scoring / end condition

### End condition

After Age III final round. All players complete turn; event deck is exhausted or Age III's end-triggering event arrives.

### Scoring

- Sum Culture VP accumulated across ages
- Add endgame multipliers from events/military-leaders/wonder-bonuses
- Add aggression bonus/penalty

### Key multiplicative scoring (I-parliament-02 / B6)

- **Military × Culture multiplier** (General leader): if you have a specific general, your military × culture bonus each turn
- **Wonder multipliers**: several wonders give Culture × X per turn based on other conditions
- **Event multipliers**: certain events apply ×2 to current Culture if you meet condition

Multiple stacking multipliers make B6 Scoring Multiplier Dependency a canonical case.

## 7. Catastrophes (B2 relevant)

- **Plague events** — kill population
- **Famine events** — destroy food
- **Political change events** — shift government
- **Natural disasters** — destroy production
- **War damage** — strip cards, steal culture

These are discrete catastrophic reversals. Combined with the event deck's regularity (1 event per round), catastrophic pressure is constant.
