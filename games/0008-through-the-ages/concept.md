# Through the Ages — Concept (imported for review)

## Premise

Review of *Through the Ages: A New Story* (Vlaada Chvátil, 2006 original / 2015 revision). Chvátil's signature heavy-strategy civilization game; the "ledger-engine" that defined a sub-genre.

- **Players:** 2-4 (3-4 best)
- **Length:** 120-240 min (4p can hit 4 hours)
- **Weight:** ~4.4/5 (among the heaviest Euros ever published)
- **Mechanics:** card drafting (military/civil action tracks), hand management, tech ladder, wonders, wars, events, era progression (Age A → I → II → III)
- **Scoring:** Culture VP accumulated across ages; ends after Age III (~20 rounds)
- **BGG rank:** #3 all-time as of review date

## Why Through the Ages for game #8

1. **First Chvátil-on-Chvátil anchor.** Calibration value: does the rubric recognize Chvátil's canonical signature when he's reviewing it himself?
2. **B6 Scoring Multiplier Dependency second-earning candidate.** TtA has multiple stacking endgame multipliers — culture × military bonus, wonder multipliers, event multipliers. Should trigger B6 adoption if earned.
3. **B2 Catastrophe Pressure salvage-or-retire decision.** B2 currently has 1 earned (T&E) + ~2.5 refute weight cumulative (Feld/Chvátil retire-explicits in FACETS/Dominion/Scythe/PR/UNFOLD). TtA has actual catastrophe mechanics (Events, Wars) that could save B2 via second-game earning — OR if Chvátil retire-explicits again, B2 hits retirement threshold and becomes 2nd retirement event.
4. **Rosenberg-on-Rosenberg delayed.** Agricola was user-excluded; keeping Rosenberg-anchor for later.
5. **Heaviest-game test.** TtA is 4h+ at 4p. Tests whether TIGRIS's pipeline scales to the top of the weight curve.

## Artifact-as-story

The **card tableau + civilization mat** is the centerpiece. Each player builds a civilization from Age A to III via ~40-60 cards played across the game. The mat tracks resources, production, science, food, happiness, military, culture — the ledger.
