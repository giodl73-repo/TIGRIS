## Argument Shape

ESTUARY is a dense-pipeline information-economy Euro that earns its T+G+R design
target differently from CODEX: where CODEX earned G (Game-spectrum/architecture) via
the Convocation-as-law layer, ESTUARY earns G via the cargo-pipeline's multiplicative
dependency. Both designs earn B1, but ESTUARY's claim is about obligation asymmetry
rather than rule-drafting architecture.

The game's novel contribution: **A32 first corpus earn** (hidden-valuations/obligation
type, score 6-7). Combined with B5 (architectural novelty), ESTUARY's Parliament record
establishes a new design claim — obligation asymmetry has never been the primary
information economy in any reviewed corpus game.

The contested cluster (A5, A29, C8, C7) flags real calibration concerns for v1.1:
simultaneous declaration should be promoted from recommended to canonical; the Phase 3/4
split should be simplified (consider merging Process+Deliver into a single 2-part action
to resolve C7). A29's first corpus score (4 = leans punishing) is data, not failure.
