# PT01 — ESTUARY 4p Standard Argument

**Structure:** Narrated 4-player game, 12 rounds (Tide Wheel cycles 1.5×). Moment anchors
M1–M6. Each persona comments at their relevant moment. Collision events at M3 and M5.

**Player configurations (designed to stress-test stakes):**
- P1 Knizia (C1 anchor): Timber-heavy manifest (needs 3 Timber cells for high VP)
- P2 Rosenberg (D4 anchor): Spread manifest (1-2 cells per goods type; breadth strategy)
- P3 Chvátil (B5 anchor): Cloth-and-Salt manifest (strong Port Sweep play)
- P4 Lacerda (B1 anchor): Grain-heavy manifest; Grain harbors targeted early

**Starting positions:** All 4 players place 1 ship at their corner supply depot.
Lacerda starts near North Grain depot; Knizia near South Timber; Chvátil near
East Cloth; Rosenberg near West depot (mixed access).

## M1 — Setup / Manifest Arrangement

**Stegmaier (C8):** "The 3-5 minute manifest arrangement phase is a strategic decision —
P1 (Knizia) immediately knows to prioritize Timber harbors and can build their first-move
Sail toward the Timber processing harbor. This is a T0 strategic choice — but it's not
*compressed*. Five minutes of card-study is exploration, not compression. C8 contested:
the decision is strategic but not first-turn-compressed in the Wingspan sense."

**K-K (C7):** "Before round 1 even starts, P3 (Chvátil) reads the manifest and says
'I need Cloth to South Quay (5 VP cell) — that harbor is on the east side.' The Sail
action's icon (wave) communicates movement; Load (box) communicates pickup; Process

asks: 'Do I Process now or in Phase 3?' — the split between declaring Process (action)
and resolving it (Phase 3) is a genuine teach confusion. C7 contested."

**Lacerda (B1, anchor):** "P4 immediately plans the pipeline sequence: Sail to Grain depot
(1 mana), Load Grain (free), Sail to North Grain Harbor (1 mana), Process (pay 2 mana
Grain-type), Sail to River Gate delivery port (1 mana), Deliver. That's 5 mana and 5
actions across ~2 rounds. The pipeline compels every decision from the first move.
B1 at 9 — the pipeline is visible from setup."

**Chvátil (B5):** "P2 (Rosenberg) is watching P3's ships at setup and sees 'Cloth-colored
cubes, Salt-colored cubes.' P2 knows WHICH goods P3 values. P2 does NOT know the

## M2 — Rounds 1–3 (Early Pipeline Ignition)

**Round 1 events:**
- P1 Knizia: Sail→Load (6 Timber from South depot)→Sail toward Timber Harbor (South side).
  3 actions consumed. Mana: 10 - 2 (two Sails) = 8 mana remaining.
- P2 Rosenberg: Load (Grain 4 + Cloth 2)→Sail→Sail. Efficient multi-goods loading.
  Approaching central harbors.
- P3 Chvátil: Sail→Load (Cloth 5)→Build (claims East Cloth harbor: pays 4 mana Cloth-type,
  owns it, earns all future toll income). Bold R1 harbor claim.
- P4 Lacerda: Sail→Load (Grain 4)→Process at North Grain Harbor (pays 2 mana Grain = 2 mana
  toll to the harbor owner, currently neutral = goes to bank). Declares processing; will
  resolve Phase 3.

**Phase 3 R1:** Lacerda's Grain processes successfully (harbor was neutral, no queue).
Lacerda now holds 4 processed Grain on ship.

**R1 Veil event equivalent:** Tide Wheel ticks from position 1 to position 2. No change
(still High Tide; all channels navigable).

**Round 2 events:**
- P1 Knizia: Arrive at Timber Harbor (Rosenberg has claimed it — wait, neutral still).
  Claims Timber Harbor South (4 mana Timber-type). Now owns it. Process declaration.
- P3 Chvátil: Sail (P3's Cloth ship) toward South Quay delivery port. En route.
  **P3 Observes P1's manifest** — pays 3 mana, asks "P1's Cloth/River Gate cell?"
  P1 reveals: value = 0 (Knizia doesn't need Cloth to River Gate). Public information
  now. P3 learning: Knizia has 0 Cloth demand. Good — less competition for Cloth supply.
- P2 Rosenberg: Process at North Grain Harbor (now licensed by Lacerda — Rosenberg pays
  2 mana Grain toll TO Lacerda). Lacerda earns 2 mana income. **This is the toll economy
  activating.**
- P4 Lacerda: Sail to River Gate (1 mana). Deliver: 4 processed Grain → target River Gate
  manifest cell. P4 declares Grain/River Gate. Delivery check: does Lacerda have ≥ manifest
  cell value? P4's Grain/River Gate cell is face-down — P4 alone knows it's value 3.
  4 delivered ≥ 3 required. **Cell completed.** Reveal: value 3. Score: 6 VP immediately.
  Knizia and Rosenberg observe: Lacerda just completed a 3-VP-cell. Not as high-value as
  it looked. The information revealed by completion (cell value 3) recalibrates opponents.

**Rosenberg (D4):** "R2 — Lacerda commits the River Gate delivery window early. The Grain
supply from North depot is still available, but the River Gate delivery slot for Grain is
now permanently closed for Lacerda (can't re-score it). This is an early commit point
(round 2 of 12). D4 at 8: the commit is live and irreversible early."

**Feld (A5):** "R1-R2 simultaneous declaration (recommended rule): all 4 players reveal
actions together, resolve in turn order. Harbor conflicts: P1 and P4 both want the same
Grain harbor at R1. Turn-order resolution means P4 (Lacerda, 4th player) loses the race
to P1's earlier declaration. Without simultaneous declaration, this takes 12 sequential
decisions instead of 4 simultaneous reveals + targeted conflict resolution. A5 at 5 for
4p — simultaneous variant is functionally necessary; the serial variant creates measurable
downtime."

**Vaccarino (A4):** "P3 (Chvátil) drew Cloth/South-Quay = 4 units needed and Cloth/
River-Gate = 2 units needed from the distribution bag. P3 loaded 5 Cloth units from depot
— slightly over-loading against the 4-unit cell, hedge-loading for the 2-unit second cell
alternatively. This is A4: P3 knows the distribution odds (each value 1-5 appears equally)
and calibrated the over-load to hedge against which cell to target first. A4 at 8."

**Collision candidate M2 — C1 Knizia ↔ B4 Lacerda:**
Lacerda: "The Observe action (P3 spending 3 mana at R2 to see P1's Cloth/River Gate =0)

Knizia: "But C1 also fires here: P3 spent 3 mana on Observe instead of claiming the

**Rosenberg motions for OP evaluation. C1 (Knizia) vs B4 (Lacerda) — do they address

**Vote: 5-3 for OP (5 votes: Chvátil, Rosenberg, Feld, Stegmaier, Vaccarino favor OP;

## M3 — Rounds 4–6 (Mid-arc: Harbor Competition + First Tide Restriction)

**Round 4 — Tide Wheel at Position 4 (First Ebb):**
Six upper-delta channels become impassable. P1 Knizia's ship, loaded with 4 processed
Timber and en route to North Quay, is in a now-impassable channel. **Knizia's ship is
stranded.** Cannot Sail or Process for R4. Can still Observe (free action equivalent)
or Build at an adjacent harbor.

**Knizia (C1):** "My ship is stranded. My R4 is gutted — I can't move the Timber to
North Quay. I have 8 mana and nothing to spend it on efficiently. C1 fires: I know the
next best action (Sail to the open alternative channel once the ebb ends in R6) but
cannot take it now. Tension budget maintained even in stranded state."

**Rosenberg (D4):** "This is the Tide lock-in. Knizia committed that ship route in R2
(when the tide was High and the channel was open). The Tide chart was public — Knizia
could have seen this ebb coming. The decision to commit to that route in R2 is now
irreversible in outcome. D4 at 9: the lock-in moment is exactly at the predicted ⅔ arc
mark, visible and architecturally consequential."

**Chvátil (A3):** "Chvátil (P3) sees Knizia stranded. P3 uses Build to place a Survey
Post at the South Cloth Harbor — and the Survey Post's passive gives P3 a 1-mana Observe
per round when opponents process there. P3 is now building information infrastructure.
A3 at 9: the interaction here is not just harbor blocking — it's investing in an
information-extraction engine that targets other players."

**K-K (D2):** "The stranding event also demonstrates spatial interaction: P1's ship
physically blocks a channel hex (other ships can't enter either). Even stranded, P1's
ship is a spatial obstacle. D2 at 8: spatial coupling is continuous — ships in channels
create live adjacency effects even when they can't move."

**Lacerda (B4):** "Round 5: Rosenberg Observes Lacerda's Grain/South-Quay cell (pays 3
mana). P2 sees: value 5 (high-value, Lacerda needs 5 units of processed Grain to South
Quay). Rosenberg now knows Lacerda's highest-value cell. This observation costs Rosenberg
3 mana AND reveals that information permanently. Rosenberg can now block the South Quay
Grain delivery window OR race to that harbor to force a queue delay. B4 at 9: the
revealed information immediately changes Rosenberg's strategy."

**Collision M3 — B5 Chvátil ↔ B1 Lacerda:**
Chvátil: "B5 is about architectural novelty. The Survey Post's effect (free 1-mana Observe
per processor) creates an information-architecture layer that has no corpus sibling.
This IS the novel structure."
Lacerda: "B1 fires on the same mechanism: the Survey Post is a pipeline component
(Build → License → Improvement → Information Income). The Survey Post's income pipeline
gears against the observation economy. B5 and B1 both fire on the harbor improvement
system."
**Feld motions for OP. B5 (Chvátil) vs B1 (Lacerda) — is this architectural novelty
vs pipeline architecture, or is B5 subsumed into B1?**
Panel debate: B5 asks "is ESTUARY's structure novel in the corpus?" — it is, because no
prior game has obligation-asymmetry as the primary architecture. B1 asks "does the
pipeline create multiplicative dependency?" — it does, because removing the improvement
tree collapses the information-income subsystem. These are orthogonal: B5 is about
uniqueness (is there a sibling?); B1 is about mechanical interdependency (do stages
compound?). **Vote: 6-2 for OP** (dissent: Knizia and Stegmaier argue B5's novelty
claim is grounded in B1's pipeline being the novel element, making them collinear).
**Standard OP 6-2. Both earn defend marks.** → **Collision 2: B5↔B1 OP 6-2 (new pair, 127th TP).**
