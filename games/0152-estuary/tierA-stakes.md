## Stakes by Persona

### Lacerda (anchor)

**B1 System Gearing** (anchor axis)
- *3p:* **8** — "Pipeline stages multiply: Load→Sail→Process→Deliver. Remove harbor licensing and the whole processing economy collapses. Remove manifest and Delivery generates 0 VP. Remove Tide Wheel and harbor queuing has no urgency. Five stages, all load-bearing."
- *4p:* **9** — "At 4p, all 8 harbors are contested. The pipeline pressure is maximum: you cannot skip harbor access when all slots are full and the tide is closing your channel. B1 at 9."

**B4 Information Transparency Cost** (secondary)
- *3p:* **8** — "At 3p, 36 cells are hidden. Observe costs 3 mana — significant. But the revealed cell stays public permanently. Every observation is an investment that cannot be undone. B4 at 8."
- *4p:* **9** — "At 4p, 48 hidden cells, more observation targets. Seeing a competitor load 6 Cloth tells you nothing about which port they're targeting. The Observe decision is live every turn. B4 at 9."

**A28 Cognitive Load Profile**
- *3p:* **8** — "Five registers: pipeline state (which ship is where in the 5-stage chain), manifest deduction (what do opponents need?), Tide position (what closes next round?), mana type allocation (which mana for Observe vs tolls vs builds?), Observe timing (when does revealing a cell help or hurt?). Five simultaneous deductive registers."
- *4p:* **9** — "Same registers plus 4-player manifest inference. A28 = compound-deductive (score 8-9). ESTUARY earns the upper range."

### Knizia

**C1 Tension Budget**
- *3p:* **7** — "Standard 3p mana supply: 10 discs per player, max spend ~6 in a turn (Sail 1 + toll 2 + Observe 3). You can nearly always pay for what you need but never for everything. Tension budget present."
- *4p:* **8** — "At 4p, harbor queuing means your planned toll payment (2 mana) may go to an opponent's harbor you didn't plan to use (your preferred harbor is full). The budget is tight and the tightest moment — Observe vs Build vs toll — recurs every round."

**A1 Elegance**
- *3p:* **5** — "Six actions is the target. The setup procedure (manifest tile drawing + arrangement + harbor seeding + tide positioning) adds rule-overhead above what the turn-to-turn complexity justifies. A1 at 5: depth is genuine but the rules don't understate it."
- *4p:* **5** — "Same. The 12-section rules document is proportional to the game's depth. Elegance is neutral — not inelegant, not understate-complex."

**A2 Decision Density**
- *3p:* **7** — "Three actions per turn from 6 types. Each Sail decision is a routing puzzle (which channel, which ship, which destination). Each Process decision involves queue assessment. Each Observe decision involves manifest probability inference. A2 at 7."
- *4p:* **8** — "At 4p, harbor capacity competition adds a layer: your Process decision depends on knowing whether a competitor is also heading to that harbor. Decision density rises with player count. A2 at 8."

### Rosenberg

**D4 Late-Game Lock-in**
- *3p:* **7** — "Tide Wheel cycles create route closures at predictable moments. The commit point (Tide position 4, first ebb) arrives at round 4 of 12 — too early for a ⅔ lock-in. However the second ebb (round 12, if tide cycles 1.5×) arrives at the game's critical arc. D4 at 7: lock-in occurs but the first instance is early."
- *4p:* **8** — "At 4p, players run more routes simultaneously and the second Tide cycle (rounds 8–12) forecloses exactly the routes they've committed to. D4 at 8."

**C4 Engine-Garden Dependency**
- *3p:* **6** — "Harbor improvements (Expansion, Express Line, Survey Post) each require a prior license investment. The Expansion improvement only values if you already own and frequently use the harbor. Four doctrines chain: License → Control → Traffic → Improvement value. C4 at 6."
- *4p:* **7** — "At 4p, harbor licenses face competition. Winning a contested harbor (high traffic = high toll income) makes the Expansion upgrade more valuable, creating a positive feedback loop. C4 at 7."

**C3 Scarcity Bite**
- *3p:* **4** — "Depot scarcity (4 cubes per round at 3p) is present but not punishing. Players can schedule depot access without starving. The harbor capacity is the tighter constraint. C3 at 4: scarcity exists but doesn't punish neglect — it punishes simultaneous demand, which is a different mechanic. Retire-explicit."
- *4p:* **5** — "At 4p, 4 players competing for 4×4=16 goods cubes simultaneously. Depot access is more contested. C3 at 5. Retire-explicit: the punishing mechanism is competition, not scarcity of a designed feeding phase."

### Feld

**A5 Downtime/Pacing**
- *3p:* **6** — "Simultaneous declaration (recommended) keeps all 3 players active. Conflict resolution (harbor capacity, depot) adds modest sequential overhead. A5 at 6 for 3p."
- *4p:* **5** — "At 4p serial resolution: 3 actions × 4 players = 12 action resolutions per round, plus Phase 3 harbor resolution for multiple ships, plus Phase 4 delivery checks. Dense-engine pacing concern. A5 at 5 — contested: simultaneous variant resolves this; mandatory serial play does not."

**A29 Forgiveness Curve**
- *3p:* **5** — "Tide closures are punishing (stranded ship = 0 actions for 1-2 rounds) but recoverable (tide opens again, goods survive, manifests untouched). At 3p with 12 rounds, recovery is feasible. A29 at 5 — neutral, contested."
- *4p:* **4** — "At 4p, a stranded ship during peak harbor competition can mean losing a delivery window permanently (another player completes that manifest cell before you recover). A29 at 4 — leans punishing, contested."

**B2 Catastrophe Pressure**
- *3p:* **4** — "Tide shifts are predictable (Tide Chart is public). Stranded ships are disruptive but not catastrophic: goods survive, toll income continues, manifests remain. B2 at 4 — retire-explicit. ESTUARY lacks the sudden-reversal quality of T&E's catastrophe tiles or Pandemic's outbreak chains."
- *4p:* **4** — "Same. Predictable disruption ≠ catastrophe pressure. B2 retire-explicit."

### Chvátil

**B5 Architectural Novelty**
- *3p:* **8** — "Obligation asymmetry is unreviewed in the 151-game corpus. CARTELL had hidden value functions (what is your economy worth?). VIADUCT had hidden capacity (what can your pipes carry?). ESTUARY has hidden obligation (what must you deliver?). The manifest IS the architecture: remove it and ESTUARY becomes a standard port-trading logistics Euro. B5 at 8."
- *4p:* **9** — "At 4p the obligation asymmetry reaches maximum density (48 hidden cells, 4 players inferring each other's obligations simultaneously). B5 at 9 — the novel architecture scales with player count."

**A3 Interaction**
- *3p:* **7** — "Harbor capacity competition (only 1 ship per harbor slot per round), supply depot race (limited goods cubes), Observe as direct targeting, Fault Line mutual freeze. A3 at 7."
- *4p:* **8** — "At 4p, harbor queuing is reliably contested. The Observe action directly targets one opponent's hidden information. Fault Lines become live (higher ship density). A3 at 8."

**A32 Information Architecture Type**
- *3p:* **6** — "ESTUARY's information structure: goods types are public (visible on ships); manifest quantities are hidden. This is the 'hidden valuations' structural type (A32 score 5-6): known objects with unknown priorities/values. ESTUARY scores at 6 rather than 5 because the hidden valuations are OBLIGATIONS (mandatory), not preferences (elective). First corpus earn for A32."
- *4p:* **7** — "At 4p, the obligation-hidden architecture is load-bearing for all 4 players simultaneously. A32 at 7. The structural type is clearly identified: not private hands (3), not inverted visibility (10) — specifically hidden-obligation valuations (6-7)."

### K-K

**C7 Action-Menu Clarity**
- *3p:* **6** — "Six actions, each with a single-icon codification (Sail/Load/Process/Deliver/Observe/Build). The Process-vs-Deliver distinction is a teach risk: both fire at specific hexes, but Process fires in Phase 3 and Deliver fires in Phase 4. Experienced players handle this; first-session players confuse which phase. C7 at 6."
- *4p:* **5** — "At 4p, the Process→Phase 3 delay (you declare Process as an action, resolution happens later) creates a split-action feel that conflicts with C7's 'rules communicated by components' ideal. C7 at 5 — contested."

**D2 Spatial-Interaction Presence**
- *3p:* **7** — "Channels have 2-ship capacity; competition for contested hexes is direct. Harbor queuing blocks access. Supply depots are spatial anchors. Fault Lines force mutual shutdown. D2 at 7."
- *4p:* **8** — "At 4p, every navigable channel is potentially contested. Spatial coupling is continuous: ship placement affects all adjacent players' routes. D2 at 8."

**D1 Family-to-Expert Scaling**
- *3p:* **2** — "Manifest tile setup alone (draw 12 tiles, arrange 4×3 grid, read hidden values) exceeds family-game accessibility. Weight 3.8. ESTUARY does not teach in under 20 minutes at any count. D1 retire-explicit at 2."
- *4p:* **2** — "Same. D1 retire-explicit."

'Which harbor should I license first?' is grounded in manifest knowledge. C8 at 6 — contested: the decision is strategic but takes 3-5 minutes (exploration, not compression)."

- *4p:* **3** — "Same. A6 retire-explicit."

**D3 Count-Robustness**

- *4p:* **7** — "At 4p, ESTUARY is well-tuned. D3 at 7. The 3p→4p variance is significant — retire-explicit for overall count-robustness claim."

### Vaccarino

**A7 Emergence/Replayability**
- *3p:* **8** — "Manifest tile distribution creates 60+ feasible delivery sequence orderings per player. Tide Wheel adds route variance each cycle. Harbor licensing competition is positional and game-specific. A7 at 8."
- *4p:* **9** — "At 4p, manifest interaction (competing for the same goods sources), harbor competition (which 4 of 8 harbors get licensed), and Observe targeting (whose information is worth buying?) all produce uniquely-shaped games. A7 at 9."

**A4 Variance Calibration**
- *3p:* **7** — "Manifest tiles drawn from a distribution bag: the bag's value distribution is public (players see the draw odds). Each player draws 12 tiles without looking. This is calibrated variance: the distribution is known in aggregate, the individual draw is unknown. A4 at 7."
- *4p:* **8** — "At 4p, 4 players drawing from the same bag creates gentle correlation (one player's high-value Timber/North-Quay cell makes that path less likely for others). The variance is calibrated against this correlation. A4 at 8."

**B6 Scoring Multiplier Dependency**
- *3p:* **3** — "Bonus VP are additive: Goods Sweep +5 (fixed), Port Sweep +5 (fixed), Harbor Majority +3 (fixed), Residual Mana +1/3 (fixed rate). No scoring interaction between bonuses — completing a Goods Sweep does not amplify Harbor Majority. B6 requires multipliers that define the scoring space. ESTUARY's bonuses are additive, not multiplicative. Formal refutation. B6 + 1R."
- *4p:* **3** — "Same. B6 refuted. The scoring structure is additive linear."
