## Innovations

### I-haul-01 — B3 targeted earn confirmed after 18 games; farm-chain is canonical B3 habitat

- **Dimension:** B3 Conversion Chain Depth
- **Trigger pattern:** targeted_earn_confirmed; dormancy_recovery
- **Source:** games/0051-haul
- **Status:** adopted
- **Note:** B3 was at 3E/4.5R with 18 games since last earn — the longest dormancy in recent TIGRIS history for a non-dormancy-watch axis. HAUL's Farm→Mill→Warehouse→Market pipeline is the canonical B3 habitat: each stage is mechanically distinct, each has a separate cost, and removing any stage collapses the game's economic logic. B3 earns when the pipeline is genuinely load-bearing — not incidental. Design recipe for future B3 earns: ensure each conversion stage has a distinct bottleneck or cost structure.

### I-haul-02 — C4 window watch cleared; warehouse-upgrade garden earns canonically

- **Dimension:** C4 Engine-Garden Dependency
- **Trigger pattern:** monitoring_window_cleared; targeted_earn
- **Source:** games/0051-haul
- **Status:** adopted
- **Note:** C4 was last earned at Brass: Birmingham #37 (14 games ago, approaching 8-game window at ~game #58). HAUL's warehouse-upgrade architecture earns C4 via adjacency dependency: Spice-Rack + Flour → Bread only if adjacent; remove the tile and the conversion stops. This is the "garden" — you plant upgrade tiles and their positioning determines what the warehouse produces. C4's canonical earning condition: a personal architecture where specific adjacency relationships must persist across the game to sustain scoring. HAUL demonstrates this cleanly.

### I-haul-03 — B3↔C3 OP confirmed; 24th temporal-register OP

- **Dimension:** OP corpus; B3/C3 adjacency
- **Trigger pattern:** temporal_register_op_24th
- **Source:** games/0051-haul (B3↔C3 OP 6-2)
- **Status:** adopted
- **Note:** B3 (structural depth of the pipeline; timeless) vs. C3 (scarcity pressure at each node; per-session). New temporal-register OP pair: the pipeline's architecture (B3) and the friction within that architecture (C3) are asking different questions. B3 = what is the game's structure?; C3 = why is navigating that structure costly? Confirmed 24th temporal-register OP. Design implication: any supply-chain game with genuine scarcity at conversion nodes should expect B3↔C3 as a reliable OP pair.
