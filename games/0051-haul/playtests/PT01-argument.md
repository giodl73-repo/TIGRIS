## Season 1 — Pipeline Established

**Setup**: 4 players. Farmland hexes seeded. Each player holds 2 Grain in Warehouse. Season 1, Round 1.

**B3 (Rosenberg anchor) — Defend**: "Round 1: P1 Farms (2 Grain from a 2-fertility hex) → Round 2: P1 Mills (2 Grain → 1 Flour) → Round 3: P1 Warehouses Flour → Round 4: P1 Sells Flour (2 VP). Three pipeline stages in Season 1 alone. The game requires committing to each stage sequentially; no stage can be skipped without VP loss. +1 defend on B3."

**B1 (Lacerda) — Defend**: "The Farm→Mill cascade is visible from Round 1: Grain flows to the Mill, Flour flows to the Warehouse, then to Market. The gear chain is the game's production engine. Each gear amplifies the next: more Grain → more Flour → higher Market sell price. +1 defend on B1."

**C1 (Knizia) — Defend**: "Round 3: the single Mill site has 2 Carts on it — P2 arrived first (Round 2), P3 arrives Round 3 to find the Mill occupied. P3 must pass or Farm. The Mill bottleneck IS the tension clock: you can see exactly when you'll be able to process, and if P2 blocks you again in Round 4, your Season 1 Flour target may slip. +1 defend on C1."

**A6 (Stegmaier) — Defend**: "Taught in 12 minutes: Farm = take Grain; Mill = convert 2 Grain → 1 Flour; Warehouse = store; Sell = earn VP. Theme makes each action self-evident. +1 defend on A6."

**A3 (Chvátil) — fires**: "Round 2: P2 places their Cart on Hex 4A (the only 3-fertility Farmland). P1 was planning to Farm that hex in Round 3. P1 cannot take Hex 4A while P2's Cart is there. Direct blocking via Cart placement — mandatory competitive engagement. +1 defend on A3."

## Season 2 — Mill Competition + Warehouse Upgrades

**State**: Season 1 complete. P3 sold 2 Grain (2 VP). P4 milled to Flour and sold (4 VP, leads). Season 2 begins.

**C3 (Chvátil) — Defend**: "Season 2: the sole Mill at Site 2 has a queue forming. Round 1 of S2: P4 arrives first (3-Grain load); P1 arrives Round 2 and finds Mill occupied — P1 must wait until Round 3. Meanwhile P1's Warehouse has 4 Flour slots filling up. The Mill bottleneck isn't theoretical; it's causing real scarcity this season. The Warehouse capacity (8 goods) is also filling — P2 discarded 1 Grain because their Warehouse was full. Two active scarcity nodes simultaneously. +1 defend on C3."

**A2 (Feld) — Defend**: "Season 2 Round 2: P1 faces: (a) Mill now open — go Mill, (b) Farm more Grain to build inventory, (c) buy Upgrade tile with 2 Flour (spend processed goods now vs. sell them), (d) Sell what they have (lock in VP before Market stall fills). Four distinct options, each with a multi-season consequence. Decision density is real. +1 defend on A2."

**C4 (Vaccarino) — Defend**: "Season 2: P4 purchases a Spice-Rack upgrade tile and places it on their Warehouse board, adjacent to two empty Flour slots. From this point, every Flour P4 warehouses in those slots converts automatically to Bread (+2 VP more per unit). P4 has created an architectural dependency: their scoring strategy now depends on maintaining that Flour → Bread adjacency. If P4 fills those slots with Grain instead, the Spice-Rack fires nothing. The warehouse garden is established. +1 defend on C4."

**D4 (Rosenberg) — Defend**: "P4's Spice-Rack tile is permanent. They've committed the adjacent Warehouse spaces to Flour inputs. By mid-Season 2, P4's warehouse architecture is locked in: those 2 slots are now 'Flour-only' slots for the rest of the game. The lock-in is architectural, not just strategic. +1 defend on D4."

**Chvátil (C3) proposes OP**: "B3 asks: 'how deep is the conversion pipeline?' — 4 stages. That's about the game's STRUCTURE. B3 = the architectural blueprint of the supply chain. C3 = the friction that makes traversing that blueprint costly.
