# PT01 — FLOOR: Argument Record

3-player game. Moment-anchors: Round 1 (Foundation established), Round 2 (multiplier decision point), Round 4 (endgame floor calculation).

---

## Round 1 — Foundation floor + C2 earns

**C2 (Knizia anchor) — Defend**: "Setup: all 3 players have 10 Foundation tokens. Round 1: P3 gathers Stone × 2, processes to Tool tile, sells to Market Stall 3 (Tool → 4 VP). P3 now has 10 (Foundation) + 4 (sale) = 14 VP. Meanwhile P1 spent this round Gathering and Processing but not Selling (setting up for Round 2). P1 still has 10 VP. The floor means P1 isn't behind — they have a guaranteed 10 VP base, making Round 1 non-threatening even without a sale. C2 fires: the designed floor shapes how players perceive their Round 1 non-scoring turns. Non-trivial minimum in action. +1 defend."

**C1 (Rosenberg) — Defend**: "Round 1: P2 considers spending a Foundation token to activate Market Multiplier before selling a Luxury tile (8 VP → 16 VP if multiplied). But spending a Foundation token permanently reduces P2's floor from 10 VP to 9 VP. P2 calculates: spend 1 Foundation token (gain 8 VP net: lose 1 guaranteed VP, gain 16 instead of 8 from the sale = net +8) OR hold (gain 8 VP from standard sale). The perpetual budget tension is live from Round 1: Foundation tokens are both safety and resource. +1 defend."

**B1 (Lacerda) — Defend**: "Round 1: P1 Gathers Grain → Processes 3 Grain into 1 Bread tile → Sells Bread to Stall (5 VP). Foundation→Resource→Product→Sale. The 4-gear pipeline fires. +1 defend."

### C2↔C1 COLLISION — Round 2 (OP, temporal-register)

**Knizia (C2 anchor) proposes OP**: "C2 fires on the structural design — the Foundation token as a designed minimum floor that guarantees breadth regardless of play quality. C2 asks: is there a non-trivial designed floor in the scoring system? That's an architectural question about the game's design. C1 fires on the in-game experience — the perpetual scarcity of Foundation tokens as a budget resource (hold for safety vs. spend for multiplier). C1 asks: does every player always have slightly fewer resources than they'd like? That's a play-experience question. Different registers: C2 = structural design property; C1 = play-experience property."

**Rosenberg (C1 stake) seconds OP**: "C2 describes the game's scoring architecture (minimum floor exists; here is its form). C1 describes the player's experience of managing that architecture during play (spending Foundation tokens is perpetually tense). You can have C2 (a designed floor) without C1 (if Foundation tokens were so abundant that spending was never tense). You can have C1 (perpetual budget tension) without C2 (a game with no floor but perpetual scarcity of resources). They decouple."

**Panel votes OP (6-2):**
- Lacerda → OP: "B1 pipeline architecture, C2 scoring design, C1 budget tension — three distinct analytical layers."
- Chvátil → OP: "A3 market competition and C3 stall scarcity are different from C2 floor design and C1 tension experience."
- K-K → OP: "C7 action clarity is orthogonal to both C2 and C1. Similarly C2 and C1 are orthogonal."
- Stegmaier → OP: "A6 teachability, D3 scaling — separate from C2 and C1."
- Feld → CR (C2 wins): "C1's tension budget IS the experience of the C2 floor — the tension comes from the floor's dual-function. CR: C2."
- Vaccarino → CR (C2 wins): "The Foundation token is both the floor (C2) and the budget resource (C1). They're the same mechanism."

**Result: 6-2 OP** (Knizia + Rosenberg + Lacerda + Chvátil + K-K + Stegmaier = 6 OP; Feld + Vaccarino = 2 CR). **C2 and C1 both earn. 34th temporal-register OP.** C2 targeted earn confirmed with collision-vote.
