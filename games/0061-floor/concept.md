# FLOOR — Concept

## Premise

FLOOR is a 2–4 player Euro where the scoring system has an explicit designed minimum floor: every player is guaranteed at least 10 VP regardless of play quality. The **Foundation mechanic** gives each player 10 Foundation tokens at setup — permanent VP that cannot be lost, only added to. All subsequent play adds VP above this floor.

The designed floor is non-trivial: it ensures no player finishes with zero VP (dignity preservation), it creates a known baseline for scoring calculation, and it forces the scoring scale to be meaningful above the floor (a player with 25 VP has genuinely done something, whereas without a floor 15 VP could just be "slightly above average").

C2 anchors because minimum-score shape via a designed non-trivial floor is the canonical C2 argument. The Foundation token mechanic is FLOOR's load-bearing design artifact.

## Anchor mechanic

**C2 Minimum-Score Shape.** Foundation tokens (10 VP each; distributed at setup; permanent; cannot be lost). All subsequent tile purchases, market transactions, and production runs add VP above the floor. The floor is non-trivial: 10 VP is meaningful on a scale that runs to ~40 VP total. Players who optimize can reach 35-40 VP; players who struggle reach 10-15 VP. The floor preserves competitiveness without preventing meaningful differentiation.

## Collision candidate

**C2↔C1 OP** — minimum score shape (C2: the designed floor mechanism that guarantees breadth) vs. tension budget (C1: the perpetual scarcity experience above the floor). C2 = the structural design that sets the floor; C1 = the experience of playing within a scarce budget above that floor. Different temporal registers: C2 = setup/structure; C1 = in-game experience.
