# Secret Hitler — Design (Rules Import)

## Setup

Roles assigned secretly: in 5p: 3 Liberal, 1 Fascist, 1 Hitler. In 10p: 6 Liberal, 3 Fascist, 1 Hitler. Fascists know each other (and Hitler, in 7p+); Liberals know nothing. Presidential candidate marker rotates clockwise.

## Core loop

1. **Election:** President nominates Chancellor; all players vote Ja/Nein simultaneously.
   - Pass (majority Ja): proceed to Legislative Session.
   - Fail: marker passes to next player; if 3 consecutive failures, top policy tile automatically enacted.
2. **Legislative Session:** President draws 3 policy tiles (face-down), discards 1 (face-down), passes 2 to Chancellor. Chancellor enacts 1.
3. **Executive Action** (if Fascist policies enacted): President may Investigate player, Call Special Election, Peek at top policies, or Execute a player.

## Policy track

Two tracks: Liberal (5 spaces) and Fascist (6 spaces). Both draw from the same 17-card policy deck (6 Liberal, 11 Fascist). Discards are reshuffled when deck exhausts.

## Winning

- **Liberals:** Enact 5 Liberal policies OR execute Hitler after 3+ Fascist policies.
- **Fascists:** Enact 6 Fascist policies OR elect Hitler Chancellor after 3+ Fascist policies.

## Power differential

The 11:6 Fascist:Liberal policy ratio means Fascists can "blame the deck" truthfully — even Liberal governments will occasionally enact Fascist policies from bad draws. This creates genuine ambiguity in deduction.

## Weight profile

Weight 1.67. Teach time: 10 minutes. Social complexity is entirely emergent (who is lying?); rules complexity is minimal.
