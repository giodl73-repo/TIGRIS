# Secret Hitler — Concept

## Premise

Secret Hitler is a 5–10 player social deduction game set in 1930s Germany. Players are secretly assigned Liberal or Fascist roles (one Fascist is Hitler). Each round, a President nominates a Chancellor; the pair receives 3 policy tiles and enacts 1 (Fascist or Liberal). Liberals win by enacting 5 Liberal policies or assassinating Hitler; Fascists win by enacting 6 Fascist policies or electing Hitler as Chancellor after 3 Fascist policies are enacted.

Chvátil anchors on A3 Interaction — the game IS the interaction. Policy deduction, vote tracking, accusation, counter-accusation, and assassination are all direct player-to-player mechanisms.

## Players and length

- **Players:** 5–10
- **Length:** 45–60 min
- **Weight:** 1.67

## Anchor

**Chvátil on A3 Interaction** — Secret Hitler has no solo content. Remove the social interaction and the game is empty. Every mechanic (voting, policy deduction, assassination) requires direct player-to-player engagement. This is the canonical A3 design.
