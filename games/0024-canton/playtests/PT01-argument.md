# PT01 — CANTON: Argument Record

3-player game. Factions: P1 = Merchant, P2 = Soldier, P3 = Engineer.
Moment-anchors: Setup, Era 1, Era 2, FORCED-ENGAGEMENT, Era 3, Scoring.

**Faction matchup this game:**
- Merchant (P1): chain bonus on 2+ adjacent territories; non-adjacent pair scoring at era end
- Soldier (P2): contest (remove opponent marker from adjacent territory); most-contests era bonus
- Engineer (P3): Road tokens extending adjacency; Road token scoring at era end

This is a genuinely testing matchup for A7: Merchant wants dense networks; Soldier wants to disrupt those networks; Engineer wants to build infrastructure others can threaten. The faction interactions are specific to THIS combination of three — a game with Scholar/Diplomat/Farmer would play entirely differently.

Map drawn: 12 tiles arranged in a compact-ish cluster (no extreme sprawl).
P1 (Merchant) places 2 starting markers in adjacent territories (central cluster). P2 (Soldier) places 2 markers flanking P1's start. P3 (Engineer) places 2 markers on the eastern edge.
AP pool: 16 APs (3-player).

## Era 1

**AP pool: 16. Era ends at 0.**

Turn 1 — P1 (Merchant, 3 AP): Places 2 more markers adjacent to starting positions (building a chain of 4). Uses faction ability? No active ability (Merchant has no active — only passive chain bonus and era-end scoring). Places 3rd and 4th markers in territories extending the chain. Passes last AP.

Turn 2 — P2 (Soldier, 3 AP): Activates contest (1 AP): removes P1's marker from a contested territory (P2 had placed adjacent, now holds that territory). Places 1 marker (1 AP). Passes (1 AP returned to pool).

Turn 3 — P3 (Engineer, 3 AP): Places Road token (2 AP) on an edge between P3's two territories, creating adjacency across a previously separated hex edge. Places 1 marker (1 AP).

AP pool: 16 − 2 (P1) − 3 (P2) − 3 (P3) = 8 remaining.

**A3 (Chvátil) — Defend**: "P2's Turn 2 contest (remove P1 marker from adjacent territory) is the most direct player-to-player action in the game. P1 loses a territory they placed on Turn 1. P1 must respond: re-place in that territory or abandon it. This is not indirect interaction — it's a direct removal of player state. +1 defend on A3."

**B2 (Feld) — Defend**: "P2's contest against P1 created a strategic catastrophe threat: if P2 secures that territory AND adjacent territories, P1's Merchant chain is broken. The Merchant's chain bonus requires adjacency — a gap in the chain means no bonus. P2 is threatening P1's core scoring architecture. +1 defend on B2."

**C1 (Knizia) — Defend**: "Era 1 with 8 APs remaining after 3 turns: the AP pool is depleting faster than players expected. The era-end tension is immediate. P3's Pass (returned 1 AP to pool) was a tactical decision that shortened Era 1. The AP clock is the game's explicit tension budget. +1 defend on C1."

**D2 (K-K anchor) — Defend (2nd)**: "P3's Road token bridges two previously separate hex tiles. The map's physical layout was altered by a player action — P3's Engineer ability extended what 'adjacent' means in this specific map. The spatial structure of the game is dynamic, not fixed. D2 earns because the map interaction is not just about placement but about the spatial topology itself. +1 defend on D2."

**D4 (Rosenberg) — Defend**: "P1's Era 1 chain of 4 territories is now contested (P2 removed one marker). The remaining 3 territories are locked — P1 cannot un-place them. The Era 1 territory commitment is permanent; P1 must work within it or spend APs re-contesting. +1 defend on D4."

**Era 1 Scoring**: P1 (Merchant): 3 territories (broken chain, no bonus) → 3 VP. P2 (Soldier): 3 territories + contests era-bonus: +0 (only 1 contest, need more for max bonus) → 3 VP. P3 (Engineer): 3 territories + 1 Road token era-bonus (2 VP) → 5 VP.

## Era 2

**AP pool refill: 16. Era 2 begins with existing territory control.**

P1's chain is broken; P1 needs to repair or shift strategy. P3 leads on Road infrastructure.

Turn 4 — P1 (Merchant): Places in the contested territory (re-claiming from P2). Also places 2 more in chain extension. P1's Merchant chain is now 5 territories (longest chain this Era).

Turn 5 — P2 (Soldier): Contests P1 AGAIN (targeting the same territory). Removes P1's re-placed marker. Also places in 2 new territories (building own control). Contests are now 2 total for P2 — approaching the era-bonus threshold.

Turn 6 — P3 (Engineer): Places Road token #2 extending the network east. Places markers in territories now reachable via Roads.

**A7 (Lacerda) — Defend**: "Era 2 demonstrates the faction divergence. P1's Merchant strategy: 'maintain adjacency chain at all costs; repair breaks immediately.' P2's Soldier strategy: 'break chains to deny Merchant bonus; build own control through attrition.' P3's Engineer strategy: 'build infrastructure, not direct control; Roads outlast contested markers.' Three qualitatively different strategic identities, visible in how they're spending their APs. This is the faction-identity replayability claim: the same map with Scholar/Farmer/Diplomat would produce entirely different interaction patterns. +1 defend on A7."

**B6 (Chvátil) — Defend**: "P1's Merchant passive: chain of 5 territories in Era 2 scores 5 VP base + 1 chain bonus = 6 VP from this chain alone (vs. a Farmer with 5 territories scoring exactly 5 VP). The multiplier is visible: the Merchant's chain bonus is a genuine scoring multiplier that requires architectural commitment to earn. +1 defend on B6."

**A2 (Feld) — Defend**: "P2's Turn 5 decision: contest P1 again (deters chain repair) vs. place new territory (builds own control). Both options are viable; the opportunity cost is real. Contesting costs 1 AP and removes an opponent marker; placing costs 1 AP and adds your own. At Era 2 with 16 APs in the pool, P2 chose contest — a faction-ability decision that a Farmer or Scholar literally cannot make. The decision density is faction-specific. +1 defend on A2."

**B1 (Lacerda) — Defend**: "Era 1 established territory control → Era 2 built on that control (P1's chain grew from 3 to 5 because of Era 1 positioning). The map-state gearing is visible: Era 1's locked placements determined Era 2's optimal moves. +1 defend on B1."

**Era 2 Scoring**: P1 (Merchant): 5-territory chain → 5 + 1 bonus = 6 VP; non-adjacent pair era-bonus: +3 VP (2 isolated territories × 1.5 VP each) → total 9 VP. P2 (Soldier): 6 territories + 2 contests era-bonus (+3 VP) → 9 VP. P3 (Engineer): 4 territories + 2 Road tokens (+4 VP) → 8 VP.
