# Tier-A Stakes — CANTON

## Anchor

**Kramer-Kiesling** anchor **D2 Spatial-Interaction Presence** (adopted). Anchor claim: "CANTON's hex-tile map creates direct spatial interaction at every turn: players block adjacency chains, contest territory control, and position for Era-end scoring. The spatial structure of the board — which tiles are adjacent, which territory spaces are reachable — determines the entire game. D2 is not incidental; it is the game's mechanical spine."

Adjacency partner: **Lacerda on A7** (different persona). D2↔A7 is the designed collision: does spatial mechanics (D2) subsume faction-identity replayability (A7), or are they orthogonal temporal registers? GATE criterion 4 satisfied.

### Rosenberg

2. **C3 Scarcity Bite.** 5/5/6. "3 APs/turn (Farmer: 4) is tighter than Tikal's 5 APs. The scarcity is real: every AP decision has meaningful opportunity cost. But it's turn-sequence scarcity, not resource-pool starvation. Hold — earns only if AP pressure creates genuine floor-risk."
3. **C4 Engine-Garden Dependency.** 5/5/5. "Engineer builds a Road network (persistent adjacency infrastructure); Scholar places Monuments (point-multipliers). Both are garden-like: players build a personal architecture that becomes load-bearing for scoring. Hold — lighter dependency than Dominion but present."

### Feld

2. **A2 Decision Density.** 7/8/8. At 4p, opponents' actions between your turns further compress the decision. Earn."
3. **C6 Point-Salad Incommensurability.** 5/5/5. A Merchant and a Farmer cannot both optimize for the same territories. Hold — earns if the faction mix in-game produces incommensurable scoring targets for multiple active players."
