# Twilight Struggle — Design Import (fair-use rules summary)

Cold War bipolar conflict, 1945–1989. 2 players (USSR vs USA). ~2–3 hours. BGG weight 3.6.

## Core structure

**10 turns** divided into Early War (T1-3), Mid War (T4-7), Late War (T8-10). Each turn:

1. **Improve DEFCON** (+1 step toward 5).
2. **Deal cards** (8 early, 9 mid/late).
3. **Headline phase** (simultaneous secret event reveal).
4. **Action rounds** (6 early, 7 mid/late). Each AR a player plays 1 card.
5. **Check Military Ops** (each side must hit quota or lose VP).
6. **End-of-turn scoring check**, possibly final-scoring-card flush.

## Cards

~110 cards across 3 decks (Early/Mid/Late War, added cumulatively). Each card has:
- **Ops value** 1-4
- **Event text** coded USSR / US / Neutral

When playing an opponent-event card you may:
- Play for **ops only** → event triggers for opponent anyway (no-op avoidance).
- Play for **event first then ops** (if Neutral/yours).
- **Space Race** → dump a card; advance on space track (has step prerequisites).

Ops use: **Influence placement** (1 ops = 1 influence in controlled/adjacent country) / **Realignment roll** (attempt to remove opponent influence) / **Coup** (dice vs stability; may raise DEFCON).

## Map & scoring

World map of ~80 countries grouped into 6 regions (Europe, Asia, MidEast, Africa, C-America, S-America). Each country has a stability (1-5) and optional battleground/non-battleground marker. Presence/Domination/Control tiers per region grant VP when scored.

**Scoring cards** (one per region, mandatory to play in hand; forcing timing crises). Europe Scoring at Control = instant win.

## DEFCON track

5 (peace) to 1 (nuclear war, triggering side loses). Coups in battlegrounds reduce DEFCON. DEFCON caps where coups are legal (at DEFCON 2 no coups in Europe; etc.).

## Victory

- VP track ±20 = instant win (rare).
- DEFCON-1 = triggering side loses.
- Europe Control = instant USSR or US win.
- Turn 10 Final Scoring: all 6 region scoring cards resolve; VP total wins.

## Asymmetry

- USSR starts with early-war card and geographic bias (dominates Eastern Europe).
- US has late-war technology edge.
- Chinese Card — single asymmetric token passed between players, +1 ops in Asia.
- Space race tracks give passive VP / card-discard advantages.

## Design signatures

- **Forced scoring timing** — scoring cards must be played in hand during the turn drawn; can't hold.
- **Headline** — simultaneous reveal adds double-blind guessing.
- **Event-vs-ops tension** — playing opponent's strong event for ops triggers event; accepting pain to prevent worse pain.
- **DEFCON brinkmanship** — use the map pressure without triggering nuclear war.
- **Space Race escape valve** — dump bad cards at cost of forfeiting ops.
