# Orléans — Concept

## Premise

Orléans is a 2–4 player bag-building game set in medieval France. Players fill a bag with follower tiles (Knights, Farmers, Scholars, Merchants, Monks, Builders, Boatmen) and each round draw a random sample from their bag to take actions. Building your bag optimizes what draws you get; the variance is managed but never eliminated. 18 rounds of building, expanding on the river, and racing to development tracks.

Vaccarino anchors on A4 Variance Calibration — bag-building is the canonical variance-calibration mechanism. You construct the probability distribution of your draws by deciding which followers to add to your bag.

## Players and length

- **Players:** 2–4
- **Length:** 75–90 min
- **Weight:** 3.04

## Anchor

**Vaccarino on A4 Variance Calibration** — the bag is a controlled probability distribution. Every follower added changes the expected draw. High variance = wildcard potential; low variance = consistent optimization. The player actively shapes their own luck.
