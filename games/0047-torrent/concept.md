# TORRENT — Concept

## Premise

TORRENT is a 2–4 player hex-based water-routing game where river flows determine which territories gain resources. Players are watershed engineers placing source tiles, channel tiles, and dam tiles on a hex grid. Water flows downhill from sources through channels to territories; territories with water access generate resources each round. Direct spatial blocking: a dam placed upstream cuts off all downstream territories regardless of their connection to the source.

D2 Spatial-Interaction Presence is the design bet: the hex-grid water routing creates direct board-state coupling between players. Blocking a channel deprives opponents of resources; claiming the highest source controls the watershed.

## Players and length

- **Players:** 2–4
- **Length:** 60–80 min
- **Weight:** 2.5

## Anchor

**K-K on D2 Spatial-Interaction Presence** — water routing is fundamentally spatial. Every dam, every channel, every source placement directly affects every player downstream. This is mandatory spatial coupling at the mechanism level.

**Secondary earns:** A3 (blocking forces direct interaction), D4 (watershed topology locks in early).

**Collision candidate: D2↔A3** — is water-routing blocking (D2) orthogonal to player-to-player impact (A3), or does spatial coupling IS the interaction?
