# FRACTURE — Concept

## Premise

FRACTURE is a 3–5 player area-control game with a genuinely novel faction architecture: each of 5 factions has a structurally incompatible scoring mechanic. This is not variable powers — the factions have genuinely different scoring grammars that cannot be applied to any other faction.

- **Faction A (Encirclers):** Scores by fully encircling enemy territory (enclosed region VP).
- **Faction B (Chainers):** Scores by longest unbroken road/chain of controlled hexes (linear-length VP).
- **Faction C (Cascade):** Scores by voluntarily sacrificing territory to trigger cascade bonuses (sacrifice-for-amplification VP).
- **Faction D (Density):** Scores by maximum density of units in fewest hexes (concentration VP).
- **Faction E (Frontier):** Scores by controlling the most border hexes adjacent to enemy territory (exposure VP).

The key B5 claim: these are not "flavor differences" or "variable starting positions." An Encircler's scoring mechanic is meaningless if applied to a Chainer's geometry — the architectures are incompatible.

## Anchor mechanic

**B5 Architectural Novelty.** The faction-scoring incompatibility is the architectural bet. Remove any one faction's scoring architecture and no other game fills that exact structural space. The five architectures (encirclement, chain-length, sacrifice-cascade, density, frontier-exposure) form a complete design space of fundamentally different area-control scoring grammars.

**A7 secondary** — faction combination replayability (which 3-of-5 factions play in a given game; 10 distinct faction sets with 3-faction games, each with different strategic meta).
