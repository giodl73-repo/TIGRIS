## Round 1 — Faction architecture differentiation

**B5 (Chvátil anchor) — Defend**: "Round 1 placement. P1 (Encirclers) places 3 units on hexes forming a C-shape around two P2 (Chainers) territories. P2 extends their chain to 5 connected hexes — scores 5 VP. P1 has enclosed nothing yet (chain escapes the C-shape by extending north) — scores 0 VP for encirclement. The scoring grammars are already visibly incompatible: P1 is playing Go-like enclosure geometry; P2 is playing Ticket-to-Ride chain extension. These are not the same game being played on the same board — these are different games. B5 fires on timeless structural novelty. +1 defend."

**A7 (Stegmaier) — Defend**: "Round 1: the strategic meta is already faction-combination-specific. Encirclers vs. Chainers is a specific matchup with its own dynamics (Chainers must maintain connectivity against enclosure pressure). This combination would play differently with Density vs. Frontier in the other two seats. The strategic experience varies structurally based on which 3-of-5 factions are in play — A7 fires as structural replayability, not cosmetic variety. +1 defend."

**A3 (Rosenberg) — Defend**: "P3 (Cascade) places units adjacent to P2's chain and P4's frontier territory. Every faction must engage with other factions' board positions — encirclement requires opponent territory to encircle; chain-building requires opponent chains to threaten; frontier requires opponent adjacency to score. No faction can ignore others. +1 defend."

**D2 (K-K) — Defend**: "P4 (Frontier) places on border hexes adjacent to P1, P2, and P3 territories simultaneously — maximizing exposure score. P1 places to block P4's border access. The spatial coupling is direct: P1's encirclement geometry creates the borders that P4 scores from. Hex-grid blocking is live from Round 1. +1 defend."

### B5↔A7 COLLISION — Round 3 (OP, temporal-register)

**Chvátil (B5 anchor) proposes OP**: "B5 and A7 fire on the same mechanism at different analytical scales. B5 asks: is the faction-scoring incompatibility a timeless architectural contribution — would removing Faction C's sacrifice-cascade leave a structural void no other design fills? That's a design-theory question about the mechanism's position in the design space. A7 asks: does the faction combination system produce genuinely different strategic experiences across plays? That's a replayability question about emergent play variety. B5 = the mechanism's novelty relative to prior designs; A7 = the mechanism's generativity within this design."

**Stegmaier (A7 stake) seconds OP**: "A7 can earn even if B5 is contested: even a well-precedented faction system (low B5) could produce structural replayability (high A7). And B5 can earn even if A7 is marginal: a radically novel mechanism could produce limited replayability if the design space is narrow. The decouple is genuine — I vote OP."

**Panel votes OP (6-2):**
- Lacerda → OP: "B5 = novelty of the architecture in design space. A7 = generativity of the architecture in play space. Different questions."
- Rosenberg → OP: "Confirmed — same distinction I use for B3 (conversion chain depth, a structural design property) vs. C3 (scarcity pressure, a play-experience property)."
- K-K → OP: "Spatial architecture (D2) and replayability (A7) are both about the board, but different registers. B5 and A7 analogously decouple."
- Knizia → OP: "B5 = does this architecture have a prior? A7 = does this architecture produce variety? Independent questions."
- Vaccarino → CR (B5 wins): "B5's novelty claim is that faction scoring incompatibility is new. A7's claim is that faction combinations are replayable. If the novelty claim is false (B5 loses), A7 survives. If B5 earns, it implies A7. They cascade."
- Feld → CR (B5 wins): "Novel architecture produces replayability. B5 subsumes A7 here."

**Result: 6-2 OP** (Chvátil + Stegmaier + Lacerda + Rosenberg + K-K + Knizia = 6 OP; Vaccarino + Feld = 2 CR). **B5 and A7 both earn. 35th temporal-register OP. 3rd B5↔A7 instance — pair confirmed reliable.**
