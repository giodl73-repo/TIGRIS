# CARTELL — Rules

## 1. Overview

3–4 player worker placement. Players control hidden sub-economies in a shared city. Each player holds a secret Economy card (dealt at game start) defining their scoring function (e.g., "Trader: score 2 VP per Gold token + 1 VP per Road tile"). The city develops across 5 rounds; players place workers to claim tiles and tokens. End-of-game: reveal Economy cards; score according to each player's function.

## 2. Components

- 1 City board (4×4 grid of districts; each district has 2-4 action spaces)
- 4 Economy cards per player (dealt secretly; 1 kept)
- 30 City tiles (Road, Market, District, Mine, Park — 6 of each)
- Resource tokens: Gold ×20, Stone ×20, Food ×20
- Worker pawns: 4 per player
- VP score track

## 3. Setup

1. Each player is dealt 2 Economy cards face-down; keeps 1 (places other face-down under the Economy discard).
2. Seed City board: place tiles randomly on the grid.
3. Each player places 2 workers on any city district.
4. Round track to Round 1.

## 4. Turn Structure (5 rounds)

On your turn: place 1 worker on any unoccupied action space → take the action. Actions vary by district: Gather (take resource tokens from the district), Build (place 1 tile from supply), Trade (give 1 resource to another player; take 1 different resource), Observe (look at 1 face-down Economy card of any opponent). Workers stay until end of round; retrieved simultaneously at round end.

After 5 rounds: reveal all Economy cards; score.

## 5. Scoring

Each Economy card specifies: a primary resource (worth 2 VP each), a secondary resource (worth 1 VP each), and 1 tile type (worth 3 VP per tile in the city if you have adjacent workers). The formula is the same for all cards; only which resources/tiles count changes.

## 6. Information cost

The Observe action (cost: 1 worker + 1 action) lets you see 1 opponent's Economy card. This is the primary information-cost interaction. Additionally: reading placement behavior (why did P3 take that Market tile?) reveals hidden priorities without a dedicated Observe action.
