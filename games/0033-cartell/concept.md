# CARTELL — Concept

## Premise

CARTELL is a 3–4 player worker-placement game where each player controls one hidden sub-economy in a shared city. Each player's value function is private: one player scores Gold + Roads highest; another scores Stone + Districts; a third scores Food + Markets. Players place workers to develop the shared city, but their placement behavior reveals their hidden priorities to observant opponents.

The game is a deduction engine: optimal play requires inferring what opponents need from the city before they get it. Information is the scarce resource — learning an opponent's sub-economy is worth more than an extra worker.

## Players and length

- **Players:** 3–4
- **Length:** 60–90 min
- **Weight:** 3.5

## Anchor

**Lacerda on B4 Information-Transparency-Cost** — the hidden value function creates a genuine information cost structure. Players cannot optimize the shared city without knowing what opponents need, and opponents won't reveal that information cheaply.

**Collision candidate: B4↔A3** — information cost (B4) and player interaction (A3) are co-fired: you must interact (ask, observe, trade) to reduce your information cost. Are these orthogonal (B4 = structural hidden info; A3 = social exchange to resolve it) or the same mechanism (interaction IS the information-cost resolution)?

## Target review

**Earn-candidates:** B4 (anchor), A3 (deduction interaction), A2 (per-placement inference decisions), B1 (city development pipeline), D4 (district placements lock in), C1 (round-end tension). **Retire-explicit:** B5 (Lisboa lineage acknowledged), C6 (no point-salad), many others.

## Non-goals

- Not a hidden traitor game — all players are competing, no one is sabotaging.
- Does not claim B5 novelty (Lisboa + Keyflower precedent for information-asymmetry worker placement).
