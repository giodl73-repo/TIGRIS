## Stakes by Persona

### Stegmaier (D3, C8, A6)

**D3 Count-Robustness — score 5 / 10**
The fixed 3-4p range declared on the box hides an asymmetry: at 3p the map's Void Rifts corridor shortens to two hexes but Ember Peaks still has three; Wind School's *Turbulence* fires 25% fewer times per round against one fewer opponent, breaking the design's fourth opponent assumption. No 5p extension, no 2p reduction — CODEX ships a two-count game labeled as a range.

**C8 First-Turn Compression — score 8 / 10**
Convocation IS the first turn: the Founding Doctrine reveal plus the opening snake-pick (P1 gets first strike on the 25-card pool) is a live strategic decision before Construction begins. No guided tutorial round; the first draft pick is load-bearing.

**A6 Teachability — score 5 / 10**
Five terrain types × four component types × five mana types × the Veil acceleration triggers cross the 20-minute teach barrier. The Convocation-Construction-Unraveling phase separation is legible, but the per-Doctrine rules expansion (up to 20 active Doctrines across four players) means the rulebook cannot teach the actual game — only its scaffold.

### Vaccarino (A4, A7, B3)

**A4 Variance Calibration — score 8 / 10**
Founding Doctrine draw from 5-card per-school deck plus 20-card Veil Event deck plus 25-card shared pool with optional 5-cut rotation. Randomness is integrated as a resource: you draft AROUND your Founding Doctrine rather than mitigating it. *Tidal Chart* (Tide) even lets a player rearrange variance.

**A7 Emergence/Replayability — score 9 / 10**
~500 million unique opening configurations by the design's own count, plus 6 Schools choose 4 = 15 combos. The strategy space isn't just diverse — it's combinatorially diverse. Deck-building lens confirms: every game's tactical surface is fresh, no memorized opening survives.

**B3 Conversion Chain Depth — score 4 / 10**
Mana chains are 2-step shallow — Weight→Void at 3:1, Flow→anything at 2:1 under *Rising Tide*. The Copper→Silver→Gold progression doesn't exist; there's no multi-hop VP chain. *Stone Memory* (Weight→Void) is a single transformation, not compound.

### Rosenberg (C3, C4, A2)

**C3 Scarcity Bite — score 7 / 10**
Storage cap of 8 mana between rounds converts abundance into obligation. A player who over-produces Ember must spend it or lose it — Agricola's feeding phase rewritten as an inventory ceiling. The bite sharpens with Veil acceleration: late-round mana has half the turn-value of mid-round mana.

**C4 Engine-Garden Dependency — score 8 / 10**
Iron's *Tempered* founding doctrine creates permanent per-component accumulation — the Forge placed turn 3 scores compound value through round 5. *The Alloy* (Forge-adjacent-Sanctum chain) and *The Current* (Channel chains) make the value of component N conditional on components 1 through N-1. The garden gears.

**A2 Decision Density — score 8 / 10**
Three actions per round × 5 rounds × N Doctrines active means every Build/Activate/Conflict choice is a multi-axis decision (terrain, adjacency, mana state, Veil position, opponent proximity). At round 3 of a 4p game, the decision tree per action branches wider than Agricola's mid-game.

### Knizia (C1, A1, C2)

**C1 Tension Budget — score 8 / 10**
Storage cap 8 and three-action ceiling guarantee each round ends with the player seeing the next best action they cannot afford. The Veil's irreversibility adds the second tension axis — not just "can't do it now" but "can't do it later either."

**A1 Elegance — score 5 / 10**
Rule count is high: Convocation draft rules, Construction action economy, Veil acceleration triggers, Unraveling Edict procedure. Rule-to-depth ratio does not reach T&E or Modern Art territory. The Doctrine-as-rule layer adds interaction density but at literal rule cost — each drafted Doctrine is another rule to remember.

**C2 Minimum-Score Shape — score 2 / 10**
CODEX rewards specialization. *The Spider's Web* (largest connected group), *Deep Territory* (territory-type count), and single-School identity all reward one-axis accumulation. No minimum-color rule. A player who ignores three scoring tracks and floods the fourth is not punished structurally — only by opponent Edict.

### K-K (D2, D1, C7)

**D2 Spatial-Interaction Presence — score 9 / 10**
The Crown, Fault Lines, adjacency-based Doctrines (*Contested Ground*, *Encirclement*, *Scorched Earth*), and component-displacement (*The Undertow*, *Slipstream*) make every placement a spatial transaction with every opponent. Hex adjacency isn't decoration — the map IS the engine.

**D1 Family-to-Expert Scaling — score 3 / 10**
Target audience collapse: a 9-year-old cannot parse 5 concurrent Doctrines × 4 players. The 90-minute length and 3-4p fixed count disqualify family table. This is an expert-only game mislabeled — the axis score is low because the design refuses the axis entirely.

**C7 Action-Menu Clarity — score 7 / 10**
Five Construction actions (Build, Activate, Convert, Conflict, Advance) fit K-K's ≤6 menu rule. Iconography on player boards is assumed clean (designer intent); however, Doctrine cards carry text that breaks icon-only parsing. Menu clarity passes, card-text clarity fails.

### Chvátil (B5, B2, A3)

**B5 Architectural Novelty — score 8 / 10**
The two-layer Convocation/Construction split — you draft the rules of your reality before you build anything — is structurally distinct. No predecessor in the corpus writes the game's laws in Phase 1 before playing in Phase 2. Remove the Doctrine-draft layer and CODEX becomes a generic tableau-builder with hexes. The signature is load-bearing.

**B2 Catastrophe Pressure — score 9 / 10**
The Veil is the most aggressive doom clock in the corpus: it advances every round by default, accelerates on building depth (5th component), combat (destroys), and activation density (3+ Forges). Building a better engine hastens your death. Veil Events fire 10+ times across a play. This is T&E catastrophes on an exponential schedule.

**A3 Interaction — score 8 / 10**
Fault Lines force standoffs. The Crown draws all four players toward a single center hex. Edicts in Unraveling are a direct attack on opponent Doctrines. Conflict actions destroy components. CODEX is not multiplayer solitaire — remove opponents and the game has no content.

### Lacerda (B4, D4, A28)

**B4 Information Transparency Cost — score 3 / 10**
All Doctrines face-up. Founding Doctrine revealed at setup. No hidden hands, no sealed bids, no private objectives. Public information except draft-pile remainders. Lacerda's signature axis returns near-zero — CODEX is a transparency game, not an information-asymmetry game.

**D4 Late-Game Lock-in Point — score 8 / 10**
Lock-in occurs at Convocation end: your 5 Doctrines are your constitution. Round 3 locks your engine shape via terrain commitments; round 5 locks scoring via adjacency. The ⅔ target is hit almost too hard — some strategists will feel committed by end of Convocation, ~10 minutes into a 90-minute game.

**A28 Cognitive Load Profile — score 8 / 10**
Multi-register: spatial (hex adjacency), probabilistic (Veil Events), deductive (opponent Edict prediction), narrative (engine arc). This is novel-combined territory — no prior corpus game runs all four registers simultaneously across a 90-minute arc. Scores high because the combination is the cognitive demand.

### Feld (B1 ANCHOR, C6, A5)

**B1 System Gearing — score 9 / 10 (ANCHOR)**
Every Doctrine modifies how at least one other subsystem behaves. *The Current* chains Channel activations (Activate subsystem). *Consuming Fire* makes adjacency a production multiplier (Build subsystem × Activate subsystem). *Counter-Doctrine* makes Conflict bidirectional (Conflict subsystem × Doctrine subsystem). Remove any one Doctrine card from a played kingdom and the game's interior visibly contracts. Gearing is multiplicative, not additive — Lacerda-tier.

**C6 Point-Salad Incommensurability — score 8 / 10**
VP flows from: component count (*Spider's Web*), territory breadth (*Deep Territory*), map features (Crown, Monument, Founder's Mark), Conflict rewards (*Backdraft*, *Root Memory*), mana storage endgame, Doctrine-scoring triggers (*Great Work*, *Momentum*). Six+ non-substitutable tracks. Castles of Burgundy levels without a Feld rondel.

**A5 Downtime/Pacing — score 5 / 10**
Turn order: 3 actions per player × 4 players = 12 actions per round. No simultaneous resolution; Doctrine-activation cascades on one player's turn (e.g., *The Current* chain-activating six Channels) stall three opponents. At 4p with dense engines, round 4-5 downtime spikes — dense-engine paradox.
