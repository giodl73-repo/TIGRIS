## Fix validation

**Cap-12 impact**:
- Iron's engine (Tempered + IV + IB + SC) produced enough Weight to matter this time. Iron hit cap every round from R3 onward but kept 12W instead of 8W — translates to an extra ~4W at round-end → 2 VP per capped round × 3 rounds = 6 extra VP over v0.1.
- **Under v0.1 (cap-8), Iron's production advantage was erased — every player ended at 8 mana → 4 VP mana-VP uniform. With cap-12, Iron's 30 end-game mana outpaces Earth's 13 (both end game) and Tide's 12 — a real differentiation emerges (15 vs 6 vs 6 mana VP).**

**Mana-VP spread (v0.3 cap-12)**: Iron 15, Flame 10, Earth 6, Tide 6. **Range: 9 VP.**

- v0.1 cap-8 estimated range: 0-2 VP (everyone hits cap → identical mana VP).
- v0.3 cap-12 range: **9 VP. Production engines now differentiate by a full 4.5× multiplier.**

**Still overflowing?**
- **Iron: yes, heavily.** Iron lost 5W + 12W = ~17W to cap over the game. Despite cap-12, Iron's quadruple-multiplier engine exceeds the cap from R3 onward. Cap-12 is not wide enough to let Iron's full engine show.
- **Flame: once (R5, lost 3).**
- **Tide: no overflow.**
- **Earth: no overflow.**

**Production differentiation**:
- Iron's bigger engine now translates to **5× the mana-VP of Tide**, and **2.5× Earth's**. Cap-12 preserves the advantage of investing in production synergy.
- However, Iron is still leaking ~40% of its R3+ production to the cap. A bigger engine should feel rewarded — cap-14 or cap-16 would give Iron more of what it built.

**Balance verdict: 3/5.**
- Iron won by 1 VP — tight margin suggests cap-12 is approximately correct.
- BUT: Iron's MO was suspended by Tide's Edict, costing Iron 2 VP (v0.3 token-strip). Without that, Iron wins by 3.
- Earth's Deep Roots nerf (5 VP max) worked as designed: Earth scored 3 VP from DR instead of the 10+ VP it could have in v0.1.
- The slate was identified as "Iron may dominate" — with cap-12 Iron wins narrowly, not dominantly. The fix is working but Iron may still dominate in bigger slates (e.g., Slate 7 Iron Snowball with 4 production multipliers — test that separately).

**Remaining issues**:
1. **Cap-12 still punishes the biggest engine.** Iron lost 17W to cap over the game — that's 8.5 potential VP not realized. If Iron's engine is the *intended* maximum, cap should be 14-16 to let it breathe. If Iron's engine is *over-tuned*, cap-12 is the right containment.
2. **Non-MO production VP doctrines are the real gap.** Flame, Tide, and Earth have essentially no endgame-VP scoring from their drafts (only Tide's DT, which Earth Edicts; Earth's SW; Iron's MO in-game). Players are forced to rely on mana-VP because endgame-VP doctrines are too scarce. Consider adding more endgame-VP options to the Pool.
3. **Edict strip of Iron's MO (only 2 VP banked) was cheap to neutralize.** In v0.1 this might not have been worth an Edict. In v0.3 with token-strip, Tide correctly targeted Iron's only VP-scoring doctrine. This rule is working.
4. **The Earth Edict on Tide's DT was speculative**: Tide had 0 banked VP on DT (DT scores at game end), but Earth blocked future VP accrual. Correct choice, shows token-strip + forward-block combo is the new Edict design.
5. **Cap-12 should probably go to 14.** Iron's 17W-overflow is punishing investment in Iron's own identity. Raising to 14 might let Iron win by 4-5 VP — matches the "production school should reward production" design intent without Earth losing its Deep Roots counter-scoring.

**Recommendation**: test cap-14 in a follow-up playtest (PT10 or PT11). Run Slate 2 again with cap-14; if Iron wins by 5-8 VP, cap-12 is preferable for balance. If Iron still wins by 1-3, cap-14 is a better fit for rewarding engine-depth.

---

**Slate verdict (v0.3)**: Cap-12 fix successfully differentiates production engines (9 VP spread vs ~2 VP in v0.1). Iron wins narrowly as expected, not dominantly. The cap-12 fix is **validated**. Consider cap-14 as a follow-up test to see whether Iron's overflow losses are intended containment or over-correction.
