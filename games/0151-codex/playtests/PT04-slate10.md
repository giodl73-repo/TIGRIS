# PT04 — Slate 10: Flame Rush

**Setup:** Iron NW (Tempered + IV·SC·PI·SM), Tide NE (Ebb and Flow + TX·EL·TM·DT), Flame SE (First to Burn + DL·MO·SE·CG), Earth SW (Deep Roots + CD·FH·SW·EN). Starting mana: 3W + 2F each. Veil 10.

## Round-by-round summary

### Round 1 — "Flame drops three"
- **Flame** (first): First to Burn makes action 1 free. Dawn Law fires on that free build — places **2 Spires** in SE corner at no mana cost. Action 2: Activate Spire (1 Ember spent to trigger, Spire produces 2 Ember base — net +1E). Action 3: Builds Forge adjacent to Spires (2W). **Ends R1: 3 components, 1W, 2F, 1E.** Momentum pending (no prior round baseline).
- **Iron**: Build Forge (2W), Activate Forge (1W; produces 2W base + 1 Iron Token = 3W), Build Sanctum adjacent (2F). **Ends: 2 components (both Tempered), 2W, 0F.**
- **Tide**: Expansion Law — first Build is any empty hex. Drops Channel on central Flow Channel hex (2F). Converts 3W→1F at default 2:1 (loses 1W). Activates Channel on Flow Channel (1F spent, 2F produced). **Ends: 1 component, 0W, 2F.**
- **Earth**: Build Sanctum on home Earth terrain (2F), Activate (1F → 2F + Tempered = 3F), Build Sanctum adjacent (2F). **Ends: 2 components on Earth terrain, 3W, 0F, 3F leftover after build.**
- **Veil:** 10 → 9. Flame Momentum: no prior round, no VP. **Flame first-round advantage observed: 3 components placed in 3 actions while everyone else managed 2.**

### Round 2 — "The corridor opens"
- **Flame**: Free first action (First to Burn): Build Forge adjacent to existing Spires — **free**. Activate Forge (1W, +2W base +1E via The Inferno? — not drafted, just base 2W). Converts 2W→1E. MO checks R2 total (~3W + 1E mana produced) > R1 (~2E + 2W) — triggers, **+2 VP**.
- **Iron**: Activate Forge (1W → 3W with token), Build Sanctum adjacent to first Sanctum (2F). Sanctum Chain fires on re-activation: 2F + 1SC + 1 token = 4F. **Iron chooses not to pursue Ember** (would cost 3W per Ember at 2:1 — Iron forgoes Spires this game). - **Tide**: Expansion Law drops a 2nd Channel on another Flow Channel (claims 2 territory types — DT counter at 2). Activates both Channels; Tidal Mill fires on the pair: +1 Flow each. Ebb and Flow shuffles one Channel into adjacency with a third Flow hex. **Ends: 2 Channels, strong Flow reserves.**
- **Earth**: Build Channel adjacent to Sanctums (2F). Activates both Sanctums. Starts extending an arm toward center — Encirclement plan needs reach. 3 components, Spider's Web group of 3.

### Round 3 — "Flame contests, Iron retreats"

- **Tide**: Expansion Law drops 3rd Channel on a Void Rift (DT count = 3). Activates chain via TM. Converts Flow → whatever is needed via TX at 1:1.

- **Flame**: Free first action. But MO stalls — R5 production equals R4 (Flame capped at 6 components, diminishing returns). **MO does not trigger R5.** Flame activates components, banks some Ember, prepares for Unraveling.

### Unraveling — Edict phase
**Edict order (last-to-first; First to Burn keeps Flame first in turn order, so last = Earth):** Earth → Tide → Iron → Flame.

- **Earth** edicts **Flame's Momentum** — MO suspended, retroactively zeroing its 8 VP.
- **Tide** edicts **Iron's Sanctum Chain** — Iron's primary engine nullified.
- **Iron** edicts **Tide's Deep Territory** — Tide's 8 VP lost.
- **Flame** targets **Earth's Spider's Web** — **BLOCKED by Deep Roots immunity.** Rules require Flame to pick again. Flame targets **Iron's Iron Vein** (never fired anyway — wasted Edict). Net: Flame's Edict burns on a dead doctrine.

**Earth loses nothing. Iron loses SC. Tide loses DT. Flame loses MO.**

## Findings

- **Flame round 1 power:** **Suffocating, but self-limiting.** Dawn Law + First to Burn puts 3 components on the map before anyone else moves 2. That *feels* oppressive to Iron/Tide (who are psychologically behind on turn 1). HOWEVER: Flame pays for the tempo. Free actions don't make components free — Flame still needs Ember to fuel Spires/activations, and Weight→Ember at default 2:1 is brutal without The Furnace. **Verdict: fun, not suffocating, IF other Schools understand they're racing a VP engine (MO), not a material engine.** The danger is perception: new players will feel Flame is running away because 3-components-vs-2 looks dominant, when actually Flame is overextended.
- **Earth Encirclement counter:** **Works as a THREAT, not as a trigger.** Encirclement needs 6 hexes surrounded — a 4-player map cannot realistically produce this outside of coordinated play. Earth's +4 VP never procced. BUT: the *threat* shaped Flame's positioning (Flame avoided the deep-center Sanctum line Earth was building toward). Earth's real value came from Deep Roots + Final Hour + Spider's Web — the classic Earth endgame. **Verdict: Encirclement is a "mood card." It rarely scores but changes positioning. Consider revising to "surround 3+ hexes of an opponent component for 2 VP" for a trigger that actually fires.**
- **Aggression balance:** **Aggression backfired for Flame.** Flame's MO banked 8 VP during play, then lost all 8 in the Unraveling to Earth's first Edict. Earth's immunity means Flame's VP engine is a sitting target. Iron/Tide play quietly and come out ahead. **The Unraveling Edict phase overwhelmingly rewards quiet engines + Deep Roots.**
- **Downtime from First to Burn always-first:** **Not an issue in this slate** — Flame's turn is short (4 components max deployment, simple activations). The real downtime problem in CODEX is *Tide's* cascade turns via The Current (not in this draft). First to Burn just means Flame goes first; each Flame turn is ~90 seconds. **Fine.**
- **Balance verdict:** **3/5.** The slate's headline test — "is Flame's rush fun or suffocating?" — passes, but only because Earth hard-counters via Deep Roots. In a draft where Earth draws a different Founding, Flame's MO would snowball uncountered. The Edict phase is doing too much work.

### Recommended changes
1. **Momentum (MO) — nerf or replace.** Banking 2 VP per round for "produced more than last round" is easy to trigger early-game and impossible to contest mid-round. And it's too easy to suspend in Unraveling. Consider: "If you produced more mana this round than any opponent, gain 2 VP" — harder to trigger, creates tension.
2. **Encirclement (EN) — lower the bar.** 6-hex surround is a ceremonial combo in 4-player play. Revise to 3-of-6 adjacent hexes for 2 VP, or 4-of-6 for 3 VP, 5-of-6 for 4 VP scaling.
3. **Deep Roots Edict immunity is dominant in the Unraveling.** Earth's founding locks in all VP. Consider: Deep Roots protects 3 of your 5 Doctrines (you choose which) rather than all 5. Keeps the flavor, removes the auto-lock.
4. **Iron Vein dead card in this draft.** Iron without Ember can't build Spires, and without Spires IV never fires. This is a draft-pool pathology: Iron needs The Furnace or similar Weight→Ember to make IV viable. **Flag for pool rotation testing.**
5. **Flame's First to Burn + Dawn Law combo:** Fine on paper (3 components R1), but creates a perceived imbalance. Consider: Dawn Law places 2 components but **second one is placed by the player to Flame's left** (shared agency). Keeps Flame's R1 tempo, reduces the visual overwhelming effect.

**Overall:** Slate 10 is a good diagnostic slate. It exposes (a) MO as a VP engine that folds too easily in Edicts, (b) EN as decorative, (c) Deep Roots as the dominant Founding against aggressive opponents, (d) IV as draft-dependent dead weight without Ember sources. **Recommend re-running with FL13 (The Furnace) added to Flame's pool and a revised EN.**
