## Findings

### Fault Lines activated
**2 Fault Line events across the game.** R2-R3 (Tide/Iron north diagonal), R4 (Iron/Tide central). Both resolved within 1 round via Tide's Ebb-and-Flow reposition. **This is the sweet spot** — Fault Lines create decision pressure without permanent gridlock. Ebb and Flow is the natural counter; Tide's identity shines. If Tide didn't have mobility, Fault Lines would feel punitive. Consider: what happens in slates where no one has repositioning? (**Design concern:** test a slate without Tide or without Ebb-and-Flow to see if Fault Lines become stuck.)

### Map contest quality
**Fun-contested, not frustrating.** The SE-NE-NW triangle converged by R3 with overlap zones. SW-Earth stayed isolated too long (R1-R3 peaceful) — Earth was too easy to leave alone. When Earth finally engaged via Raiding Party in R3-R4, the game tightened.

**Issue**: Earth's peaceful zone in R1-R3 let Patient Iron bank +4 Weight with no counterplay. Consider: a "close corner" mechanic or map feature that pulls all corners toward conflict earlier.

### Iron's Spider's Web + Contested Ground combo
**Strong but not dominant in this slate.** Iron projected 6 VP from SW + produced ~40 Weight/Ember from CG+IV combos across R3-R5. Total projected before Edict: ~18-22 VP. **Post-Edict with SW suspended: 12 VP.** Iron won but by a small margin.

**Verdict: CG+SW is not broken. The Edict phase is doing load-bearing balancing work** — any single-VP-engine School gets cut down. **But** the Edict phase also means Slate 3 has a fragile VP ceiling: once SW and DT are suspended, the only doctrine that scores end-game VP directly is Deep Roots (immune). Earth wins by default of being unsuppressible, not by playing a strong engine.

**If Spider's Web survives Edicts** (e.g., Earth is not in the game, or Earth spends Edict elsewhere), Iron wins big. Iron's adjacency-loving identity is well-expressed.

### Conflict density
**Right level — maybe slightly too little.** Only 1 true Conflict action (Earth's Raiding Party, R3-R4). Flame's Conflict doctrines are all **passive** (SE, TRB) — Flame has no active-Conflict trigger in this slate. This is an engine gap. **Flame in Slate 3 is identity-confused** — supposed to be the aggressor, but has no way to *spend* 2 mana on a Conflict action. All of Flame's aggression is passive taxation that fires only when others build.

**Encirclement never triggered** — map too spread out. EN requires fully surrounding an opponent component; in a 48-hex map with 4 corners, this is rare in a 5-round game. EN is probably under-tuned for this slate (needs either more components or a smaller map).

### Balance verdict
**4 / 5.**
- Iron: strong (won), but not runaway. SW + CG delivered.
- Earth: solid (2nd), Deep Roots immunity is a real safety net.
- Flame: weak (3rd). **Passive-only Conflict kit** in Slate 3 leaves Flame without an active lever. Dawn Law fired R1, then Flame has no end-game VP generator.
- Tide: weakest (4th). Deep Territory was Tide's big play — suspended. Transmutation suspended. Tide's only output is Tidal Mill mana, which is sub-Iron.

The balance concern is **Flame in particular** — a Flame drafting SE+TRB+DL+EN gets 1 strong opening turn (Dawn Law) and then becomes passive scenery. **The four conflict-adjacent doctrines in Flame's slate-3 package need an active Conflict doctrine among them** to give Flame an R3-R5 lever.

### Recommend changes
1. **Flame slate adjustment**: Replace TRB or EN with an active-Conflict doctrine (War Doctrine, Flame War, or a new "activate-to-swing" card) so Flame has something to do with 2 mana on R3-R5. Current Flame plays only R1, then becomes passive taxation background.
2. **Encirclement** needs a weaker trigger — "3+ sides surrounded" instead of "fully surrounded" — to actually fire in 5-round games on a 48-hex map. Currently it's dead text outside of tightly clustered builds.
3. **Fault Line interaction with Tide** is the intended design. Confirmed fun. **Don't change.** But playtest a slate without Tide or without repositioning to check the floor.
4. **Edict phase** is carrying a lot of balance weight. Slate 3 exposed that when the main doctrine-VP sources are Spider's Web and Deep Territory, the Edict reliably suspends both, leaving Earth (immune) or raw mana as the tiebreaker. Consider: either (a) more end-game VP sources in the doctrine pool so Edicts can't gut the whole VP economy, or (b) lean into it — the Edict phase IS the balancer, mana + map features are the backup VP.
5. **Earth's peaceful opening rounds** were too safe. R1-R3 Earth got 4W free from Patient Iron (R1, R2) while building uncontested. Consider: aggressive map features in Earth's corner that pull opponents toward SW. Or rebalance Patient Iron trigger to require mana-positive activity (too strong as pure-passive accumulator).
6. **Iron's Spider's Web + Contested Ground** is powerful but not broken. Iron won but by ~3 VP. Edict phase adequately punishes the single-point-of-failure. **No nerf needed.** Retest in a slate where Earth isn't present (Earth's immunity is the only reason the Edict slot targeting SW was "free").
