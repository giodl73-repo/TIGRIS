# PT07 — v0.3 Slate 9: Earth Fortress

## v0.3 rule applications (compared to PT06 baseline)

1. **Deep Roots:** +1 VP/component on matching terrain, MAX 5 VP (was +2, uncapped → +12 VP with 6 Sanctums in PT06).
2. **Iron Vein:** triggers when Forge adjacent to **Sanctum** (was Spire). This finally gives Iron Vein a firing path in this slate — Iron drafts IV and can build Sanctums.
3. **Mana cap:** 12 total (was 8). Every School was bouncing off 8 in PT06; this should let production engines breathe.
4. **Encirclement:** 3+ adjacent hexes = 2VP, 5+ = 3VP, all 6 = 4VP (was full-surround = 4VP only). Earth's EN can now fire on partial-surround.
5. **Edict VP-token mechanic:** every time a Doctrine generates VP during play, a VP token is placed on that card; Edict strips those tokens AND blocks future VP. This makes Edicts meaningful even against engine-enablers that *banked* VP via mana-translated-to-scoring.
6. **Veil 3-Forge acceleration:** fires once per player per game (was every round). Iron only loses Veil-push once, not every Forge-round.

Setup otherwise identical to PT06: Earth returns LS at Convocation start; drafts EN as replacement.

**Earth final hand:** CD · EN · SW · DT (Founding: Deep Roots v0.3).

## Fix validation

**Deep Roots nerf effective?:** **Partial.** The nerf reduced Earth's Deep Roots VP contribution from +12 VP (PT06, uncapped +2/component × 6) to +5 VP (PT07, capped). That's a 7-VP reduction in Earth's passive scoring. Earth's raw win margin dropped from 22 VP (PT06) to 15 VP (PT07). The nerf bit — Earth's Deep Roots bonus went from largest single VP source to tied-with-SW (both 5-6 VP). **BUT Earth still wins by 15 VP.** The cap saved ~7 VP, but Earth's SW + DT + immunity stack still dominates this slate.

**Earth win margin:** 15 VP (21 vs 6 second place). PT06 was 22 VP. Reduction: 7 VP. Directionally correct, magnitudinally insufficient.

**Iron Vein fired?:** **Yes, repeatedly.** v0.3's Forge+Sanctum trigger was decisive. Iron built Sa-NW-1 in R1 and every Forge built in R2-R4 was placed adjacent to it, firing IV on every Forge activation. IV produced +2W per activation ~9 times across R2-R5 = +18W extra. Most of that spilled to cap, but it fueled Iron's MO trigger (which banked 4 VP before Edict). **v0.3's IV-on-Sanctum is the right fix for IV** — without it, Iron Vein is dead in any Spire-less slate, and Spire drafts are Flame-dominant.

**Mana cap 12 impact:** **Moderate positive for producers, no effect on mana-poor.** 
- Iron spilled less (estimate: saved ~20 mana over the game vs cap 8), which translated to one extra MO trigger and 2 more mana-VP (6 vs 4).
- Flame spilled ~4 mana in R5 vs PT06's heavier spill — marginal.
- Tide got zero benefit — Tide's problem isn't storage, it's production starvation. Cap-raise doesn't help the mana-poor.
- Earth hit cap in R5 same as PT06 — Earth over-produces F from many Sanctums; cap 12 delayed but didn't prevent spill.

**Verdict on cap 12:** Correct direction. Producers breathe. Does NOT fix mana-starvation for Schools without Founding-level mana help. Consider pairing with a "1 mana free per round" floor or similar.

**Edict VP tokens:** **Partially meaningful — only 1 card banked tokens this game (Iron's MO). That made Edicts lopsided: Earth got to strip 4 VP for free, but the other 3 Edicts had nothing to hit (because Earth's end-game VP sources are immune via Deep Roots, and no other in-play VP doctrines fired).**

This is a partial fix. The VP-token mechanic DOES create a meaningful target (Iron's 4 banked VP), but in this slate only one card fired for VP during play. Other slates with Flashpoint (FL3 — 2 Ember → 1 VP), The Great Work, or similar, would bank more tokens and make Edicts competitive.

**Slate-specific issue:** Slate 9 has only MO as a VP-banking doctrine in play. Consider whether the slate needs a non-Earth VP-banker to test this mechanic properly.

**Balance verdict: 3 / 5.** 

Improvement over PT06 (2/5). The nerfs worked in the right directions: Iron now has a real engine (IV-Sanctum), cap-12 let production matter, Edict VP-tokens gave Earth's aggressor ONE good target. But Earth still dominates by 15 VP because Deep Roots immunity + SW + DT forms a 15-VP minimum floor that opponents can't touch.

**Remaining issues:**

1. **Deep Roots immunity is the real problem, not the +2 scoring.** PT07 proves this: even with the scoring bonus capped to 5 VP, Earth's SW (6) + DT (4) + Deep Roots (5) = 15 VP that opponents literally cannot threaten. Flame, Iron, Tide banked ONE VP-token between them (Iron's MO); Earth banked zero but scores 15 end-game points that are immune to Edicts. The asymmetry: other Schools' VP sources are VP-token-interruptable; Earth's are not. Recommend: **remove Deep Roots immunity, OR give other Schools proportional end-game-VP immunity on their own terrain, OR limit Deep Roots immunity to scoring-type doctrines only (not engine-type) so Edicts can still strip banked tokens.**

2. **Tide is broken in mana-poor slates.** Tide with no Flow Memory / Rising Tide / Overflow had 0 agency in R4 after a single RP strip. This is a Tide-design issue not tied to v0.3 changes — Tide needs a baseline mana floor (e.g., "Ebb and Flow also gains 1F each round" or "Channels produce 1F passively"). Flag for v0.4.

3. **VP-token mechanic is right but understested in this slate.** Only Iron's MO banked tokens. Need re-test in slates with Flashpoint, Great Work, or other VP-generators to validate Edicts feel meaningful across more game states. Recommend running the v0.3 Edict test on Slate 5 (Tempo Race — has FH, MO, IB, GW-adjacent play) or Slate 12 (Great Work Race).

4. **Encirclement v0.3 never fired in this slate.** Earth's Sanctum cluster is too tight; opponents are too far to encircle. EN as Earth's LS-replacement was dead in PT07. This isn't a v0.3 regression (EN was dead in PT06 too because it couldn't fire pre-v0.3 without full-surround), but the 3+-adjacency relaxation didn't help in this slate's geometry either. EN is probably correctly balanced now — it just doesn't fire when the drafting School is spatially isolated. Flag for Slate 10 re-test (Earth has EN + SW there, with aggressive Flame providing adjacency targets).

5. **Veil 3-Forge once/game is a clean fix.** Iron triggered once in R4; didn't spam Veil pushes. Iron no longer pays for success every round. Keep this rule.

6. **Mana cap 12 is correct but not sufficient alone.** Raise to 14 if Tide-recovery remains the concern, OR pair with a floor rule.

### Recommendation for v0.4

- **Deep Roots immunity scope-limit:** Deep Roots immunity applies only to Earth's *passive* scoring doctrines (SW, DT, Deep Roots itself). Earth's active/conflict doctrines (CD, EN) are normal — Edictable. This preserves Earth's "fortress" identity without making Earth's opportunity-cost doctrines immune too. Alternative: Deep Roots immunity cost a draft slot — Earth gives up 1 of 4 draft picks to activate it. Expensive immunity.
- **Re-test Slate 9 with Deep Roots immunity scope-limited.** If Earth drops to 10-12 VP (losing SW 6 to Edict), the slate becomes genuinely competitive.
- **Tide mana floor:** add to Ebb and Flow: "+1 Flow at start of round." This makes Tide recoverable after a raid.
- **Slate 9 slate-design: add a non-Earth VP-banker.** Replace Iron's CG with FL3 (Flashpoint) or similar to give more Doctrine cards that generate VP-during-play. Validates Edict VP-token mechanic properly.
