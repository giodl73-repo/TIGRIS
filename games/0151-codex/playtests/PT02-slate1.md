## Findings

- **Veil pacing:** fast. Started at 10; reached 0 at end of round 5 via base decay (-5) + two 5th-component triggers (Flame R3, Earth R3) + one 6th-component trigger (Earth R4) + one more (Iron R4). That's -9 steps mid-round plus -1 end-of-round decays. Effectively the game was racing to Unraveling. Rounds never felt spacious — builders were punished for scaling. Assessment: **slightly fast** — the cascade of component-count triggers compressed rounds 3-5.

- **Dominant strategy:** Earth. Deep Roots' Edict immunity combined with Deep Territory (passive endgame VP) and Final Hour (doubled Round 5 production → mana VP) gave Earth a strategy that only *grew* under threat. Earth was the only player whose endgame VP couldn't be Edicted. This is the decisive asymmetry. Earth wins by 5 VP with zero in-game interaction spent.

- **Weakest position:** Iron. Spider's Web is Iron's only endgame VP doctrine — suspended by Earth's first Edict, its value collapsed to zero. Iron's Sanctum Chain and Iron Vein generate in-game mana but no VP. Iron's 10 VP is almost entirely from mana leftovers. Patient Iron gave +Weight, not +VP. Iron's engine built beautifully but the engine never converted to VP.

- **Deep Roots interaction:** absolutely mattered. Earth's Edict-immunity was the single most impactful doctrine in the game. Iron's SW (5 VP) was suspended by Earth's Edict; Earth's symmetric threats (DT 6 VP + Deep Roots terrain 4 VP) couldn't be touched. This is a 9+ VP swing just from immunity asymmetry. The slate's "Earth spread wide, safe from Edicts" engine summary was load-bearing.

- **Downtime:** moderate. Simultaneous activation from R3 onward prevented Tide's Tidal Mill chains from dominating table time (though TM never got geometric — only 3 Channels clustered, no cascade). Flame's Momentum required mental overhead tracking production per round. Iron's Sanctum Chain triggered per-activation but no chains. No single player hogged; however Iron's constant 8-cap recalculation slowed turns.

- **Balance verdict:** **2/5.** Earth's 5-VP win margin isn't huge, but it was *structurally* determined in the draft: Deep Roots + any endgame-VP doctrine (DT here) creates an unEdictable scoring core. Meanwhile Iron's SW is trivially Edict-able by any opponent. The game had only 2 endgame-VP doctrines active (SW, DT) — one immune, one not. This isn't a close balance; Earth has a dominant strategy from setup.

- **Recommended changes:**
  1. **The mana-VP rule (1 VP per 2 mana)** is doing too much work. When endgame-VP doctrines are scarce (as in this slate: only SW + DT), mana-leftover VP becomes the main driver and makes the game feel like Iron/Flame "lose" despite having good engines. Consider lowering to 1 VP per 3 mana OR adding a per-player mana cap that makes hoarding harder.
  2. **Deep Roots is too strong against slates with one non-Earth endgame-VP doctrine.** Options: (a) allow one Edict per game to name an Earth doctrine anyway (Edict pierces immunity once); (b) Deep Roots only blocks the *first* Edict targeting Earth, not all. The current text is an on/off switch with no counterplay.
  3. **Momentum decayed fast.** Flame's production plateaued at 6E from R3 onward because he hit component cap. MO fired only R2, R3. Consider making Momentum trigger on *net production* after activation costs, or reward ties in rounds 4-5.
  4. **Patient Iron is a trap in this slate.** +2 Weight/round is useless if Iron has no endgame Weight-VP doctrine (Weight of Ages not in slate). PI should probably also bank a small VP (1 VP/peaceful round) to match Iron Discipline's design — or PI should be rewritten to give VP in the late game.
  5. **Veil 5th/6th-component triggers** fire too often in 4-player games. Every player builds 5-6 components by R3-R4 → cluster of +1 Veils that compressed the endgame. Consider: only the *first* player to reach each threshold triggers the Veil advance.
  6. **Iron Vein never fired** — Iron drafted IV but built no Spires adjacent to Forges (Iron's drafting incentive was Forge+Sanctum, not Forge+Spire). Iron Vein + Tempered/SC pulls Iron in incompatible spatial directions. Consider rewriting IV to trigger on Forge adjacent to *Sanctum* instead — matches Iron's natural buildout.

**Slate verdict:** Slate 1 produces a *legible* 4-way differentiation (D=5 confirmed) but Earth's structural advantage makes it a poor first-playtest for balance intuition. Recommend keeping Slate 1 as the learning slate but noting that "Earth wins by design" is the expected outcome — the game teaches the rules, not the balance.
