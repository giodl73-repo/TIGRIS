# PT06 — Slate 9: Earth Fortress

## Rule application note

The v0.2 Deep Roots + Legislative Strike ban was applied at setup, *after* Founding Doctrines were revealed but *before* Convocation began. Sequence:

1. Founding Doctrines revealed. Earth drew Deep Roots (EA1).
2. Central row dealt per Slate 9 script; LS was available.
3. Ban check: Earth is the only player with Edict immunity, so Earth is the only player prohibited from drafting LS.
4. LS was announced as "locked from Earth" at start of draft. Iron, Flame, Tide could still draft LS — but none did (none had an Unraveling-lock angle, and LS's value drops sharply for non-Earth since opponents can retaliate in kind).
5. Since no one drafted LS, it went to the cut pile at end of Convocation per normal rules. Earth drafted EN in its 4th pick slot where LS was scripted.
6. Earth's final hand: CD · EN · SW · DT (Founding: Deep Roots).

**Observation on rule clarity:** The ban text currently reads as a post-draft correction ("if Earth drafts LS, return it and draft a replacement"). In practice, it's cleaner to pre-announce LS as Earth-locked at start of Convocation. Worth tightening the rule wording in v0.3.

## Findings

### Earth without Legislative Strike: still dominant

The ban removed one vector of Earth oppression (double-Edict + immunity lock) but did **not** solve the core imbalance: **Deep Roots' +2 VP per matching-terrain component scales linearly with Earth's component count**. In a 6-component endgame, that's +12 VP — larger than any single drafted-doctrine VP source in this slate. Combined with Spider's Web (6 VP for a connected cluster — Earth naturally clusters) and Deep Territory (4 VP), Earth generates **22 VP from passive/end-game scoring** before anyone can intervene.

**Legislative Strike was the wrong nerf target.** The real problem is Deep Roots' +2/component bonus stacking with Earth's natural clustering tendency. Removing LS removed the "double Edict" flavor violation but left the scoring disparity intact.

### Edict phase: hollow, Counter-Doctrine didn't fire

- **Counter-Doctrine never triggered.** CD requires an opponent's Conflict Doctrine to target Earth. No one targeted Earth with Conflict this game — Earth was SW, isolated, and Flame's RP went for Tide (closer). CD was a dead draft pick.
- **Edicts targeted engine-enablers, not VP sources.** In a slate where most doctrines generate mana not VP, Edicts defang already-done activations. The Edict phase felt like theater — everyone went through the motions with no outcome.
- **Earth's immunity was nearly irrelevant** *because* opponents had nothing good to suspend. If Earth had drafted GW or LS (a real VP engine), immunity would have been crushing. Against SW/DT/CD/EN, immunity protected 10 VP of scoring that no one could reach anyway.

### Deep Roots immunity impact: decisive, but for the wrong reason

Deep Roots' **VP bonus (+2/component)** was decisive, not its **immunity clause**. Earth won by 22 VP; 12 of those came from the Deep Roots *scoring bonus*, not the Edict shield. The immunity clause protected an additional 10 VP of SW+DT that were in principle reachable by Edicts, but the opponents' best Edict targets were Earth's own, so they wasted the shots.

### Ban effectiveness: partial fix, core problem remains

The ban solves the "double-Edict immunity lock" flavor-break. But it does NOT balance Earth in Slate 9. **PT01's concern** was about Edict asymmetry; this PT shows the deeper concern is **Deep Roots' +2/component scoring**. That mechanic is the Earth win condition in any slate where Earth builds 5+ Sanctums on Earth terrain — which is every slate.

### Balance verdict: 2 / 5

Earth wins by 22 VP in a slate specifically designed to stress-test Earth's Edict-lock. With LS removed, Earth still runs away with it via Deep Roots passive scoring. Slate 9 is not balanced. Even so, the game is *interesting* — the map play felt fine, Flame's raid on Tide was satisfying, Iron's mana cap bleeding felt like a real constraint. The failure is endgame VP scoring, not mid-game play.

### Recommend changes

1. **Nerf Deep Roots' scoring bonus.** Replace "+2 VP per component on matching terrain" with **"+1 VP per component on matching terrain, max 5 VP"** (caps the bonus at 5 VP instead of scaling to 12). This alone likely closes the gap to ~10 VP.

2. **Alternative: split Deep Roots into two Founding cards.** One card does the immunity (flavor-load-bearing), another does the terrain scoring. Earth can't have both. This matches how Flame/Iron Foundings trade off between active and passive.

3. **Address the 8-mana cap pressure.** Every School bounced off the cap in R3-R5. Mana ended up worth 4 VP for everyone (8/2 = 4), erasing production-doctrine differentiation. Consider: (a) raise cap to 10, or (b) add a "mana converts to VP at 1:2 end-game" auto-rule so excess production isn't wasted.

4. **Strengthen Edict phase VP generators.** If most doctrines are engine-enablers (no VP), Edicts feel hollow. Consider adding a "Doctrine that fires generates 1 VP banked on the card; Edict suspension forfeits banked VP" layer. Makes Edicts matter even against non-VP doctrines.

5. **Re-test Slate 9 with Deep Roots nerfed.** If Earth still wins, the problem is Spider's Web + Deep Territory's territory-hoarding synergy, which means nerfing Earth's terrain clustering (e.g., SW max 4 VP cap).

### Verdict

**The Deep Roots + Legislative Strike ban is necessary but not sufficient.** PT01's fix targeted a symptom. The root cause is Deep Roots' scoring multiplier on a School that naturally clusters 6 components on matching terrain. Until that's addressed, Earth dominates any slate where it reaches the Unraveling with a healthy board.
