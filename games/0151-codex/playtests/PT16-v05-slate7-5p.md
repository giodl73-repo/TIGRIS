# PT16 — v0.5 Slate 7 (5p): Iron Snowball + Wind

**Purpose:** Confirm two Priority-1 open items from the post-CODEX handoff:
(a) Iron mana-dominance fix confirmed at 5p — does v0.5's 50%-suspend rule give Iron real Edict leverage over Earth?

## Fix Validation

### (a) Iron mana-dominance fix: CONFIRMED

v0.5 Edict change delivers: Tide suspends Earth's SW → Earth scores 50% (2 VP instead of 5 VP). This reduces Earth's doctrine advantage from 7 VP to 4 VP. Iron wins 13-11 — narrow but decisive. The fix is:

- v0.3/v0.4: Earth 14, Iron 13. Earth wins by 1 (Earth Edict-immune).
- v0.5 4p: Earth 11, Iron 13. Iron wins by 2 (Earth doctrines now suspendable at 50%).
- v0.5 5p: same ratio — Iron 13 beats Earth 11. Wind (5th) doesn't change Iron's trajectory; it competes at 2nd-3rd level.

**Verdict: Iron mana-dominance concern RESOLVED. Slate 7 now validates Iron's identity.** Iron wins its signature configuration by 2 VP — narrow enough to be contested, decisive enough to be meaningful.

### (b) Wind balance: ACCEPTABLE WITH ONE FLAG

Wind scores 9 VP in 5p Slate 7, tied for 2nd-3rd place with Flame. Wind is competitive as a disruption school. Scattering Law (−2W on Iron Forge per round) reduced Iron's production meaningfully without preventing Iron from winning — appropriate strength level.

**Wind LS kingmaker flag:** Wind's Legislative Strike (names 2 doctrines in Edict) is strong. In PT16, Wind's LS effectively wiped Tide's 6 VP DT and partially targeted Wind's TGW (Iron took care of that). In a game where Wind's LS targets two key scoring doctrines (e.g., both Iron's residual VP and Earth's scoring), Wind becomes a kingmaker rather than a contender. This is a **design-flag, not a veto**: LS is a Conflict-category doctrine any player can draft; Wind's disruption identity synergizes with LS more than other schools, but LS was not nerfed in v0.5.

**Recommendation:** Flag LS + Wind-disruption-founding as an adjacent pair to watch in future playtests. If recurs in another slate, consider: "LS may not be combined with a Disruption founding doctrine" or "LS names 2 but one target must be a non-VP doctrine."

### (c) Ash school balance: THEORETICAL

Ash (Smoldering Core: gain 2V when any component destroyed) requires conflict-rich environments to generate mana. Slate 7 is conflict-poor (Flame's SE never triggered; Conflict actions don't pay off in Iron vs Earth accumulation race). In Slate 7, Ash would score primarily via mana conversion and end-game doctrines — an expected weak performance on this slate.

Ash is a specialist school balanced for conflict-heavy configurations (Chvátil-anchored drafts with Counter-Doctrine, Legislative Strike, Scorched Earth). Ash's weakness in Slate 7 is feature, not bug.

**Ash verdict: Conditionally balanced.** Strong in conflict slates; weak in accumulation slates. Acceptable specialist range for an expansion school.
