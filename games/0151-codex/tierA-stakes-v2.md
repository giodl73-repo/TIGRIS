# CODEX TIER-A Stakes v2 — Delta Review on v0.5

## Framing

This is a **delta review**, not a fresh review. 14 playtests and 5 versions of iteration have landed between PT01 (v0.1 Parliament) and this session. Personas are not re-staking the whole design from scratch — they are arguing specifically about what changed, which contested axes should now resolve, and whether Knizia's A1 hold on Elegance can survive v0.5's cleaner card text.

**Anchor persona this review: Knizia. Anchor axis: A1 Elegance.**

Rationale: Knizia held A1 as hold-explicit in SUMMARY-v1 — never defended, never refuted, prestige token reserved. v0.5 shipped two Elegance-relevant changes: cleaner 2-line card text (K-K's C7 concern) + Deep Roots restructure (50% VP when suspended). Either Knizia defends A1 and earns it, or he concedes — hold-explicit twice is not allowed under Parliament rules.

**Draft order Round 1:** Feld → Lacerda → Vaccarino → Rosenberg → K-K → Stegmaier → Chvátil → Knizia (Knizia last; anchor persona forced onto shared ground).

## The 8 personas × 3 drafted axes = 24 stakes

### Round 1 (snake)

**1. Feld (re-stakes B1 System Gearing)** — primary v1 anchor. Must re-stake under v0.5's Iron Vein fix: does IV firing every round (Forge+Sanctum) change the pipeline verdict? In v0.1 the anchor held with +3 defends (R1 Expansion Law × Tidal Mill × Channel; R3 Great Work; Unraveling 4-Doctrine gearing). v0.5's v0.3-fix pulls IV into near-universal ignition. Feld opens with: "B1 pipeline verdict strengthens at v0.5 — PT11 shows IV firing R2-R5 (5 triggers), producing +10W of gearing across a full game. That's not a 'hanging subsystem' anymore; it's a live pipeline." Score 4p = 9 (was 8).

**2. Lacerda (re-stakes D4 Late-Game Lock-in Point)** — held D4 at v1 anchor. v0.5 changes: Veil 3-Forge once/game means Unraveling actually arrives at R5, not R3-R4. This extends the lock-in window. Lacerda: "D4 at v0.1 was Convocation-lock confirmed at R3. v0.5's Veil pacing (Veil rarely hits 0 before end of R5 — PT11, PT13, PT14 all end at R5 natural decay) extends the arc without moving the lock-in point. Commit still at Convocation; optimization window widens from ~2 rounds to ~3." Score 4p = 8 (was 8).

**3. Vaccarino (drafts A7 Emergence/Replayability — re-stakes)** — held A7 at v1 ("500M opening configurations"). v0.5 change: the LS-ban removed (Deep Roots + Legislative Strike no longer forbidden). This *opens* new drafted configurations. Vaccarino: "A7 earns harder at v0.5 — the configuration space that was previously 500M minus LS-ban subset is now fully 500M. The removal of the ban is a configuration-space widening, not a balance concession." Score 4p = 9 (was 9).

**4. Rosenberg (re-stakes C4 Engine-Garden Dependency)** — v1 earned on Iron Vein + Sanctum Chain + Patient Iron chain conditional builds. v0.5's Iron Vein fix (Forge+Sanctum, not Forge+Spire) removes the placement-trap problem: Iron's Tempered+SC+IV engine now *actually* synergizes with Iron's natural Sanctum-Hills-adjacent placement. "C4 at v0.5 strengthens — Iron's engine is now geographically sensible (PT11 validates: Iron climbed 10→14 VP because IV fires on the actual placement pattern Iron is drafting). Engine-garden conditionalty gets real teeth." Score 4p = 9 (was 8).

**5. Kramer-Kiesling (drafts C7 Action-Menu Clarity — contested in v1)** — K-K held C7 contested at v1 ("Doctrine-text friction, secondary menu-clarity issue"). v0.5 shipped 2-line card text across all Doctrines. K-K must now defend or concede: does the 2-line format resolve action-menu clarity? "C7 at v0.5 resolves the v1 contested verdict — card text compressed to 2 lines per Doctrine; the action menu itself (Build/Activate/Convert/Conflict/Advance) was never the friction. Contested in v1 was *card text length*, and v0.5 closed that." Score 4p = 8 (was 6 contested).

**6. Stegmaier (drafts A6 Teachability — contested in v1)** — held A6 contested at v1 on rules-teach-time concern. v0.5 shipped 2-line Doctrine text + simultaneous activation for 4p. Stegmaier: "A6 at v0.5 partially resolves v1 contested. Simultaneous activation at 4p removes the cascade-chain downtime problem. But rules-teach is still ~25 min (Convocation phase needs its own teach block). Partial earn, not full." Score 4p = 7 (was 5 contested).

**7. Chvátil (re-stakes B2 Catastrophe Pressure)** — held B2 at v1 earned ("Veil step 10 in 5 rounds; Doctrinal Schism at R4 catastrophe"). v0.5 change: Veil 3-Forge now **once per player per game**. Does the tamed Veil still provide catastrophe pressure? Chvátil: "B2 at v0.5 weakens slightly — the 3-Forge once-per-game change means Veil rarely hits 0 early (PT11, PT13, PT14 all end R5 natural decay). Catastrophe pressure is now *late-game only* — the doom is real but the pace is gentler. Still earns, but B2 at 7 instead of 9." Score 4p = 7 (was 9).

**8. Knizia (anchor — drafts A1 Elegance)** — held A1 hold-explicit at v1. Must defend or concede. v0.5 ships cleaner 2-line card text + Deep Roots 50%-VP rule. The 50% rule is the sticking point — is it complexity without elegance? Knizia: "A1 at v0.5 I will defend, but with reservations. 2-line card text *is* elegance improvement. The Deep Roots 50%-rule, however, adds *computational complexity* at scoring (every suspended Doctrine now needs a halved re-score) without adding strategic depth — the suspending player already decided to suspend. I score A1 at 6 at 4p — earned on card text but dragged down by the 50% rule." Score 4p = 6 (was hold-explicit).
