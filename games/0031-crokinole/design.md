# Crokinole — Rules Summary (Review Import)

Traditional rules; public domain. Fair-use: critical commentary.

## 1. Overview

2 or 4 players (2-player or 2×2 teams) flick wooden discs at a shared circular board to score points and knock out opponents. The game is won by accumulating the most points over a set number of rounds (typically 12 or to 100 points).

## 2. Components

- 1 circular wooden board (typical diameter ~70 cm) with concentric scoring zones (5, 10, 15, 20 points and center hole), pegs around the outer ring, and a shooting line
- 12 wooden discs per player (two colors for 2-player; 4 colors for 4-player teams)

## 3. Rules

**On your turn:** Flick one disc from behind the shooting line toward the center. **If opponent discs are on the board, you must hit at least one opponent disc with your shot.** If you fail (no opponent disc hit and opponents have discs on the board): your disc is removed; it scores nothing. If you hit an opponent disc: your disc stays wherever it ends up; hit discs may be knocked off or repositioned.

If no opponent discs are on the board: flick freely toward the center hole (worth 20 points) or the scoring zones.

**Scoring:** After all discs are shot, score the discs remaining on the board. Center hole: 20 points (discs that fall in the hole are removed and placed as 20-point tokens). Zones: 15, 10, 5 from inner to outer ring. Peg zone: 0 points; discs behind the pegs score nothing. Net scoring: each player counts their discs' zone totals; subtract opponent's total from yours; the difference is your round score.

**Winning:** First to 100 points (or highest after 12 rounds, by convention).

## 4. Key mechanics summary

| Mechanic | Role |
|---|---|
| **Flick action** | Dexterity-based disc propulsion |
| **Must-hit rule** | Mandatory direct interaction when opponents have discs |
| **Center hole** | High-risk high-reward target (20 pts; disc removed if successful) |
| **Net scoring** | Both players' disc positions affect round outcome |
| **Positional play** | Where you land matters for your own scoring AND for setting up blocks |

## 5. Edge cases

- **Shooting from ditch**: discs that land in the ditch (behind pegs) score 0 and are removed.
- **Team play**: 4-player teams sit across from each other; team members' disc totals combine for net scoring.
- **Must-hit violation**: disc removed, no points. This rule is the game's primary interaction enforcer.
- **Center hole bonus**: in some variants, 20-pt hole tokens are scored separately (not netted); in standard play, they are counted as 20 pts in the zone total.
