**A4 (Vaccarino) — Defend**: "Round 1: P1 (beginner) has high-variance shots (disc ends up in ditch twice). P4 (expert) has low-variance shots (controlled placement in 15-ring consistently). The game calibrates variance to skill — A4 earns via a mechanism unique in the corpus. +1 defend."

**Chvátil (A3) proposes OP**: "D2 asks: 'does the spatial configuration of the board create meaningful strategic pressure?' A3 asks: 'are players forced to directly engage each other?' D2 fires on the LANDSCAPE — the board's physical topology (center hole, ring values, peg barrier) determines spatial strategy. A3 fires on the CONTEST — the must-hit rule forces players to interrupt their own spatial strategy to attack opponents. Same board; different registers. OP."

**K-K (D2 anchor) seconds**: "The board's geometry is timeless — the center hole is always worth 20 pts regardless of who is playing. The must-hit interaction is per-play — whether P1 is forced to hit P4's disc depends on P4's disc positions this game. D2 = structural geometry; A3 = per-game adversarial contest. OP."

**Vote (unanimous 8-0 Decisive OP)**: "The spatial landscape and the adversarial contest in Crokinole are the most clearly orthogonal of any game in the corpus — the board exists independently of the contest (you could play solitaire on the same board); the must-hit rule creates the contest. Both registers are maximally evident. 8-0 unanimous."

**Result: Decisive OP 8-0.** D2 and A3 both earn. 9th temporal-register OP; 3rd Decisive OP (after Nemesis B5↔A7 8-0 and Codenames B5↔A7 8-0). Strongest evidence yet for the D2↔A3 temporal-register pair (now confirmed twice: WEALD 6-1 and Crokinole 8-0).
