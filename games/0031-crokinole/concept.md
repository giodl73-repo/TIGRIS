# Crokinole — Concept (review-import)

## Anchor declaration

**Anchor persona:** Kramer-Kiesling (K-K)
**Anchor axis:** D2 Spatial-Interaction Presence (adopted)
**Anchor claim:** "Crokinole is the purest spatial interaction game in the TIGRIS corpus: flicking discs at opponents' discs to knock them off the board while positioning your own. The spatial interaction is not mediated by cards, resources, or actions — it is the direct physical manipulation of shared space. Every disc placement IS the spatial interaction."

**Secondary focus:** Dexterity gap closure. TIGRIS corpus had 0 dexterity reviews pre-#31. Crokinole is the BGG canonical reference for dexterity games and one of the oldest published games with consistent design.

## Context

- K-K own-anchor (K-K anchored Azul #30; this is a second K-K anchor in rapid succession — acceptable because Crokinole is the dexterity canonical and K-K's spatial signature makes them the natural anchor).
- 2–4 players; BGG weight 1.61; ~30 min.
- Tests Pool axes at pure-dexterity weight. Expected: most Euro-specific axes retire-explicit; A1, A3, D2, D3 earn.

## Expected signals

**Earn-candidates:**
- **D2 Spatial-Interaction (anchor; K-K)** — disc manipulation on a shared board; physical spatial contest is the entire game.
- **A3 Interaction (Chvátil)** — knocking opponents' discs is the PRIMARY game action; more direct than any prior A3 case.
- **A1 Elegance (Knizia)** — Crokinole's rules: flick your disc into the scoring zone; if opponent discs present, you must hit one. Total rules: ~3. Canonical A1.
- **D3 Count-Robustness (Stegmaier)** — 2 or 4 players (teams); scales cleanly.
- **A4 Variance Calibration (Vaccarino)** — flicking is physical variance; managed by skill rather than randomness. Unique variance type in the corpus.
- **A6 Teachability (Stegmaier)** — rules in under 3 minutes; the shortest teach in the corpus.
- **A5 Downtime/Pacing (Knizia)** — alternating disc flicks; fast per-turn; no calculation overhead.

**Retire-explicit candidates (nearly all Euro axes):** B2, B3, B4, C1, C3, C4, C6, D1, D4, etc. Pure dexterity game outside Euro-tradition entirely.

**Interesting contested case:**
- **A7 Emergence-Replayability (Lacerda)** — Crokinole is essentially infinite replayability (no randomization needed; skill variance provides fresh games). But A7's canonical earning condition is play-state divergence across games; Crokinole's play state is always the same (starting positions identical). Contested: earns via skill-variance but not via structural setup variety.
