### A4↔C4 COLLISION — Round 5 (OP; variance management vs. architectural dependency)

**Vaccarino (A4 anchor)**: "A4 asks: 'how well does WELLSPRING manage variance?' The pool system lets players choose their variance level per pool: concentrate = low variance; spread = high variance. A4 fires on the MANAGEMENT DECISION — how you choose to distribute purchases. C4 asks: 'does the pool architecture create garden-like dependency?' C4 fires on the STRUCTURAL CONSEQUENCE — what the concentrated pool BECOMES. A4 is the decision; C4 is the outcome of that decision. Different registers."

**Rosenberg (C4)**: "Proposing OP: A4 = strategic variance philosophy (this game's answer to 'how random should I be?'); C4 = architectural dependency (this pool now depends on having Harvest cards to fire correctly). A4 is about calibration; C4 is about commitment. I can make a variance decision (A4) that creates an engine commitment (C4) — they're both true about the same pool but asking different questions."

**Panel votes on OP (6-2):**
- Knizia → OP: "C1 tension fires when the cycle-timer approaches; A4 fires on the calibration choices leading there. Orthogonal."

- Lacerda → OP: "B3 chain fires on the pipeline; A4 fires on variance; C4 fires on architecture. Three distinct questions."
- Chvátil → CR: "The variance decision (A4) IS the garden decision (C4). Concentrating cards to reduce variance IS building the engine-garden. Same action."
