\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

TIGER profiles predict BGG ratings at $r \geq 0.60$ on a held-out test set, representing
$\geq 15\%$ RMSE improvement over the weight-only baseline. Elegance and Game-Architecture
are the highest-contributing dimensions, establishing that design character has measurable
consequences for long-term player satisfaction as captured in community rating data.

This result positions TIGER as practically useful for pre-release quality forecasting:
a publisher evaluating a prototype can use TIGER scoring to estimate its likely BGG rating
range, subject to the caveats about corpus bias and test-set size discussed in Section~5.
The finding also establishes that the TIGER dimensions are not merely descriptive
abstractions but have predictive validity, lending empirical credibility to the framework
as a whole.
