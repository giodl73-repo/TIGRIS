\section{Data and Methods}
\label{sec:method}

\subsection{Data}

We use the TIGRIS corpus~\cite{dellaLibera2026corpus} with BGG ratings retrieved via
the BGG XML API in March 2026. Games with fewer than 500 BGG ratings are excluded
to reduce noise in the prediction target, leaving $N = 138$ games. The corpus is split
80/20 into training (110 games) and held-out test sets (28 games), stratified by BGG
weight quartile.

\subsection{Feature Sets}

Three feature sets are evaluated:
\textbf{(F1) Weight-only:} BGG weight as a single predictor (baseline).
\textbf{(F2) TIGER-only:} The five TIGER dimension scores derived from within-game
normalized PCA~\cite{dellaLibera2026pca}.
\textbf{(F3) TIGER + Weight:} All five TIGER dimensions plus BGG weight.

\subsection{Model}

Ordinary least squares regression with L2 regularization (Ridge regression, $\lambda$
selected by 5-fold cross-validation on the training set). Results are reported on the
held-out test set only. Pearson $r$ and RMSE are the primary evaluation metrics.

\subsection{Ablation Analysis}

To identify the contribution of individual TIGER dimensions, we train models with each
dimension held out in turn (leave-one-dimension-out ablation) and report the change in
RMSE relative to the full F2 model. Dimensions are ranked by their ablation impact.
