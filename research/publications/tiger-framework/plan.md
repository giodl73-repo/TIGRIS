# Paper Plan: TG-B1 — TIGER Framework

## Paper ID
TG-B1 | Track B | Venue: CHI / FDG 2027

## Research Question
Can the five PCA-derived dimensions of game design variation be formalized into a
reliable, expert-validated scoring framework? Do TIGER profiles correctly discriminate
game pairs that experts rank differently?

## Primary Claim
TIGER anchor descriptors at 0, 5, and 10 for each of the five dimensions produce
profiles that correctly discriminate ≥90% of expert-rated game pairs in a blind
panel study of 20 games × 40 pairs.

## Key Sections
1. Introduction — why existing frameworks measure quality not distinctiveness
2. Related Work — design lenses, game frameworks, design space models
3. The TIGER Dimensions — one subsection per dimension: definition + anchors + examples
4. TIGER Profiles — 20 canonical games with radar chart visualizations
5. Validation Study — expert panel methodology and discrimination results
6. Discussion — applications, limitations, comparison to BGG weight
7. Conclusion

## Data Dependencies
