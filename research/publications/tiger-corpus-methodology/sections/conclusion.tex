\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have introduced the TIGRIS corpus --- 150 board games scored on 32 axes using a
structured two-mode protocol --- and established its reliability via Cronbach's $\alpha$
at $\geq 0.80$ overall. The corpus is the first publicly available multi-axis game scoring
dataset at this scale and provides the empirical foundation for the TIGER framework papers
(TG-A2 through TG-C2).

The methodological contributions --- a two-mode scoring protocol distinguishing fast-track
estimates from full-pipeline scores, per-axis reliability thresholds, and an open data
release --- are designed to be replicable and extensible. Future versions of the corpus
will expand to 250 games with expanded coverage of non-Euro traditions, and will add a
third scoring mode (community-contributed scores) with appropriate reliability controls.
