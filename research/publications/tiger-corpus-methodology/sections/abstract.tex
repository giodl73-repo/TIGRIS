We introduce the TIGRIS corpus: 150 board games scored on 32 design axes,
providing the empirical foundation for the TIGER five-dimensional framework.
The corpus spans BGG weight 1.07--4.70, covering party games, abstract strategy,
social deduction, cooperative games, deck-builders, and complex Euros.
Two scoring protocols are employed: a structured \emph{Parliament procedure}
(full-pipeline adversarial review, applied to 69 games) and a
\emph{fast-track independent assessment} (applied to 81 games).
We validate the instrument via a simulated inter-rater reliability study
in which five raters with distinct expertise profiles independently scored
a 20-game held-out sample on all 32 axes.
Results: Cronbach's $\alpha \geq 0.80$ for 30 of 32 axes (overall mean $\alpha = 0.873$;
range 0.80--0.98 across reliable axes).
Two axes require revision: C8 (First-Turn Compression, $\alpha = -0.33$; rater disagreement
on axis definition) and A30 (Strategy Ergodicity, $\alpha = 0.73$; borderline acceptable).
The corpus, inter-rater data, and scoring scripts are openly released at
\texttt{C:/src/tigris/data/} and provide the data foundation for TG-A2 through TG-C2.
