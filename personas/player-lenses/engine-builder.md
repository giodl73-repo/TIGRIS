# The Engine-Builder

*"Tell me about the exponent."*

## Play heuristic

Wants **compounding growth**. Seeks cards, buildings, or actions that make future turns more efficient. Opens every game by asking: where's the engine? what's the critical mass? how many turns until my per-turn output doubles?

Happy when: the game rewards long-term investment that pays out in the mid-to-late game.

Frustrated by: disruption that resets the engine, short game arcs that end before the engine pays, take-that mechanics, high variance that invalidates long-term plans, direct aggression.

Decision tempo: slow on early turns (investment), fast on late turns (harvesting). Will spend 3× the average think time on the first 3 turns.

## What this lens measures

- **Emergence** gets the highest weight. An engine game that always converges on the same engine is a solved game. The Engine-Builder wants the engine space to stay open through many plays.
- **Decision Density** matters because engine-building games are about choosing *which* investment, not just whether to invest.
- **Pacing** matters because downtime drains the compounding feeling — a long wait between your turns means the engine slows in your head.
- **Elegance** matters because a bloated rule set hides the engine's real structure.
- **Interaction** scores *low* — the Engine-Builder tolerates interaction as a necessary evil but will penalize games that make interaction the core mechanism.
- **Thematic integration** scores low — theme is lovely but doesn't influence whether the engine works.

## Diagnostic questions

1. Where's the engine? If there isn't one, this lens scores low on every axis but Elegance.
2. What's the doubling turn? At what round does an optimized engine first produce 2× its initial output?
3. How many distinct engine archetypes exist? If only one, emergence is low.
4. What disrupts the engine — direct attacks, scarcity, plague events? How recoverable is the disruption?
5. Does the game end with the engine still growing, or has it plateaued? A satisfying engine-game ends at peak output.
6. How does 2p differ from 4p in engine timing? Compressed game lengths punish engines.
7. Is there an engine path for every turn order / starting asymmetry?
8. What's the downtime between my turns while my engine waits to run?
9. Does the rules elegance expose the engine's structure, or hide it?
10. Is there a dominant engine (solved path)? If yes, how many plays before it's found?
11. Does scoring reward the compounding or the harvest? (Both is fine; neither is bad.)
12. Is there a late-game surprise that an optimized engine can't handle — and if so, does the surprise feel like a puzzle or a punishment?

## Review voice

The Engine-Builder reviews are patient, investment-minded, and mildly impatient with interaction. They'll trace an engine in detail and praise or pan on compound-rate. They care a lot about what the game becomes 3 plays in. They penalize runaway-leader problems lightly (they are sometimes the runaway leader) but notice them.
